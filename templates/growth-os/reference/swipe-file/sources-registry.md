# Swipe File & Marketing Asset Sources Registry

The complete reference database for finding proven marketing examples. The agent consults this registry when it needs golden examples, calibration data, or structural patterns for any marketing asset it produces.

---

## How to Use This Registry

When generating any marketing asset, the agent should:
1. Identify the asset type (email sequence, ad creative, VSL, landing page, etc.)
2. Locate the relevant sources below
3. Pull 3-5 proven examples from the highest-priority sources
4. Extract structural patterns and language patterns from those examples
5. Use patterns as calibration constraints, not templates to copy

## EMAIL SWIPE FILES

### EmailDrips.com
**URL:** https://www.emaildrips.com
**Content:** Complete email sequences -- full onboarding flows, free trial sequences, webinar sequences, product launch sequences, event sequences. Entire multi-email flows from real companies.
**Volume:** 120+ complete sequences (30 free trial, 19 webinar, 17 product launch sequences).
**Cost:** Free (examples); paid AI sequence generator.
**Agent use:** Best source for multi-email campaign architecture. Most swipe files show individual emails -- EmailDrips shows the entire flow from day 1 to close with proper pacing and logic. Use for sequence structure training, not just individual email copy.
**Quality:** Excellent. Structured by sequence type, real company examples, entire flows preserved.

### Really Good Emails (RGE)
**URL:** https://reallygoodemails.com
**Content:** Marketing emails, transactional emails, onboarding sequences, SaaS email flows. Design + copy together.
**Volume:** 10,000+ emails from thousands of brands.
**Cost:** Free (basic); paid plans for advanced features.
**Agent use:** Searchable by keyword and category. Strong on email design patterns and lifecycle sequences. Use for design + copy pairing reference.
**Quality:** Excellent. Genuine curation, not scraped noise.

### Milled.com
**URL:** https://milled.com
**Content:** E-commerce and retail marketing emails, promotional campaigns. Largest raw email archive available.
**Volume:** 38 million+ emails from 98,000+ brands, dating to 2012.
**Cost:** Free (basic); Milled Pro for full archive access.
**Agent use:** Boolean search, holiday/objective filters, full historical access. Best source for high-volume email training data across 14 years. Less curated than RGE but vastly larger.
**Quality:** Good. Raw/unfiltered but searchable.

### Email Love
**URL:** https://emaillove.com
**Content:** Full email campaigns, flows, transactional emails, abandoned cart sequences, newsletters. Captured across mobile, dark mode, image-blocked views.
**Volume:** 5,000+ brands tracked, 40,000+ marketers using platform.
**Cost:** Free (all email inspiration, brand insights, journeys, trend reports).
**Agent use:** Unique because they manually trigger abandoned cart sequences and purchase flows to capture the complete customer journey including transactional emails. Competitor benchmarking data enables calibration against industry norms.
**Quality:** Excellent. Methodical collection methodology.

### Good Email Copy
**URL:** https://www.goodemailcopy.com
**Content:** Plain-text email copy only -- no design. Pure text from companies like Slack, Google, Trello.
**Volume:** Hundreds of examples.
**Cost:** Free (created by Front).
**Agent use:** Plain text format makes it trivially easy to parse and use as training data. Use for copy quality calibration without design distraction.
**Quality:** Good. Curated, focused, narrower than RGE.

## AD CREATIVE LIBRARIES

### Meta Ad Library
**URL:** https://adslibrary.facebook.com
**Content:** All currently active ads on Facebook and Instagram. Creative, copy, format, run dates, geographic targeting.
**Volume:** Every active ad on Meta platforms.
**Cost:** Free, no account required.
**Agent use:** Primary source for competitor ad intelligence. Ads running 30+ days = proven performers. EU version reveals targeting parameters. Combine with Google Ads Transparency Center for full picture.

### Foreplay.co
**URL:** https://www.foreplay.co
**Content:** Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, Google ads. Human-curated by creative strategists.
**Volume:** 100,000+ new ads added daily. Largest community-curated ad library.
**Cost:** Paid (enterprise tier, pricing not public).
**Agent use:** Every ad was saved by a real practitioner who found it valuable. Chrome extension saves from all major ad libraries. AI-powered creative brief generation. The gold standard for ad creative swipe files among agencies.
**Quality:** Excellent. Human-curated, not scraped.

### MagicBrief
**URL:** https://magicbrief.com
**Content:** Ads with full video transcription. AI-powered analysis of what makes ads work. Competitor tracking.
**Volume:** 12 million+ ads; 100K curated ads.
**Cost:** $249/month (ad-spend based pricing).
**Agent use:** Full video transcription of ads and VSLs turns video content into parseable text. The most AI-native tool in this category. Closest modern replacement for discontinued Moat Ad Search.
**Quality:** Excellent. Premium, AI-native.

### Swiped.co (Swipe-Worthy)
**URL:** https://swiped.co
**Content:** VSLs, Facebook ads, display ads, banner ads, print ads, emails, advertorials, headlines, webinars, Dan Kennedy collection, classic direct response archive.
**Volume:** Thousands of examples including dedicated VSL section and Dan Kennedy collection.
**Cost:** Free (Insider membership for premium/archive content).
**Agent use:** Each example includes breakdown of psychological principles and techniques. "Swipe Studies" format provides structured analysis of WHY something works. Ideal for training AI to understand principles, not just patterns. Dan Kennedy Lost Web Archives (Insider) is historically irreplaceable.
**Quality:** Excellent. One of the oldest and most respected resources. Covers classic DR copy unavailable elsewhere.

### AdSpy
**URL:** https://www.adspy.com
**Content:** Facebook and Instagram ads across 222 countries, 88 languages.
**Volume:** 155 million+ ads.
**Cost:** ~$149/month.
**Agent use:** Largest pure Facebook/Instagram ad database. Searchable by audience demographics, engagement, and landing page technology.
**Quality:** Excellent. The original and most comprehensive Facebook ad spy tool.

### Minea
**URL:** https://www.minea.com
**Content:** Facebook, TikTok, Pinterest, Shopify ads. AI Magic Search (image upload to find similar ads), AI Creative Finder.
**Volume:** 900 million+ ads.
**Cost:** $49/month and up.
**Agent use:** Best for e-commerce/DTC verticals. Massive database for identifying currently-profitable ads at scale.
**Quality:** Excellent for e-commerce. Less relevant for B2B/SaaS.

### Swipekit
**URL:** https://swipekit.app
**Content:** Saves from Facebook, TikTok, Google, LinkedIn, Pinterest ad libraries. Tracks ad running duration (performance proxy).
**Volume:** Unlimited personal library.
**Cost:** $26-$66/month; 14-day free trial.
**Agent use:** Tracks how long ads run (longer = more profitable). Covers all 5 major ad libraries. More affordable than Foreplay.
**Quality:** Good. Less curated than Foreplay but cheaper and broader platform coverage.

### BigSpy
**URL:** https://bigspy.com
**Content:** Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Pinterest, Twitter ads.
**Cost:** Free tier available; paid plans.
**Agent use:** Broader platform coverage than AdSpy but less depth.
**Quality:** Good.

## Known Gaps

These asset types have no dedicated large-scale public database:

1. **VSL scripts** -- Swiped.co has some, SwipeBuilder auto-transcribes, MagicBrief transcribes video ads, but no dedicated VSL script database exists at scale.
2. **DM scripts** -- No structured database exists. Content is scattered across individual coaches' lead magnets.
3. **Webinar scripts** -- webinarswipefile.com is the closest; no large-scale public archive.
4. **Launch email sequences** -- EmailDrips covers this best; Panoramata can reconstruct competitor sequences.
5. **Subject lines with verified performance data** -- Only SwipeFile.com's big list and DigitalMarketer's lead magnet have open rates attached to examples.

For these gaps, the agent should use MagicBrief video transcription (VSLs), Panoramata sequence reconstruction (launches), and manual collection from practitioner content.
