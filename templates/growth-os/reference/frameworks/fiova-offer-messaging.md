\t**Phase 1: Crafting your high ticket offer + getting setup
****
****Step 1: Crafting the initial offering****
**
\tSo... First things first, you need to get your offer created and ideally built for success from Day 1.

At a baseline level, this is what it comes down to;
\t- Identifying the niche expertise fat you or your creator has
\t- Understanding the ICP of the person that is most likely to invest in that niche expertise
\t- Identifying the clear quantifiable goal that you can help said client \\^ achieve
\t- Packaging it into an \"offer\" that is not only attractive from a marketing perspective but also just makes sense logically.

\t**Example:** Your client's niche expertise is that they're a TT Shop Creator who's generated 7 figures GMV, and they just so happen to be a 30 y/o woman

**Expertise:** TT Shop Affiliate

**ICP: **(Technically) they could help anyone, but given their niche expertise, they're probably best suited to help moms, middle aged women, etc.

**Big promise:** Learn how to make a new disposable income stream on TT Shop in as little as a couple hours per week (something you can juggle with your career, kids, etc.)

**Offer:** Primarily focused around selling products that middle aged women would excel with, without having it take hours per week, etc.

**The part that might take some more brainstorming is more so around the POSITIONING of the offer. 

le:**
\t- Who is the offer intended for?
\t- What's the main result / outcome of the program
\t- Are we selling mass-market, or niched down?

\t**Typically, the best way to find this is to probe the creator with questions like….
**
\t- When people reach out to you right now asking for help... Who are they and what are they looking for?
\t- Who do you feel like you're best suited to help currently, and what's the most valuable outcome you can get for them?
\t- What's YOUR competitive moat against everyone else? What are YOU uniquely qualified to help people with?

\t**Step 5: Pricing + deliverables
**
\tFirst, let's cover pricing:
\t- \\$3-5k ballpark is usually a good place to start for most standard flagship offers
\t- Raise price if it's a super in-demand creator, or if close rate is insanely high
(40-50%)
\t- Would recommend building towards a 3-tiered offer ladder (downsell, flagship,
upsell). 

\tLet's talk about why:

\t**Person #1: \\$3k flagship offer,
**
**Person #2: \\$5k flagship, \\$10k upsell, \\$997 downsell
**
• More rev on \"cleanup\"
• When leveraging financing properly, this alone can boost rev drastically
• Actual ascension process into upsell
• Way higher margin for paid traffic

When it comes to deliverables...

The pillars will always be the same:
• Course
• Private community
• Live coaching
• Add-on resources
\t
\tWhen it comes to designing your curriculum, you want to think through every single step
that massive list of what needs to be covered, that it takes for someone to go from complete beginner -\\> where you are today and build

\tNot everything needs to be included in flagship, this is how you build your upsell too.

Essentially, you list out every possible resource you COULD give someone in a \\$10-50k offer,
and then just strip some away as you build the flagship offer.

Typically looks something like:
\t- Down sell = Info only
\t- Flagship = 3 core pillars above + some add on resources
\t- Upsell = Flagship + more access + more add on resources

\tListing out every single thing someone needs / someone has to buy also gives you tons of ideas for cross-sells / aff partnerships you can incorporate into your offer.

*
\t- Could position something like this as more of an investment / long term play to more affluent audience
\t- Higher ticket offer \\$25k+ with more bolt-on resources, DFY elements, etc.

\t- Position it towards ideal ICP (typically financially)
