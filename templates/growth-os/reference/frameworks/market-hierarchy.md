# The Market Hierarchy

**What it is:** The absolute hierarchy that controls outcomes in creator businesses: Market controls Audience, Audience controls Offer, Offer controls Copy. Macro always controls micro.
**When to apply:** When diagnosing why a business is underperforming. When deciding where to focus strategic effort. When a creator is considering entering or exiting a market.

---

## The Hierarchy

```
Market (where you sell)
  └── Audience (who you sell to)
        └── Offer (what you sell)
              └── Copy (how you say it)
```

Getting rich has more to do with the market you are in than the product you have. The ratio is roughly 90/10 -- 90% the market, 10% the offer. A mediocre offer in an expanding market outperforms a brilliant offer in a contracting one.

This is not a suggestion. It is a structural reality. You cannot fix a market problem with a better offer. You cannot fix an audience problem with better copy. Each level constrains everything below it.

## Three Market States

| State | Description | Implication |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| **Expanding** | Fastest growth, fastest path to revenue. Typically driven by technological innovation or cultural shifts. | Move fast. The window closes. Market momentum forgives imperfect offers and mediocre operations. |
| **Contracting** | Declining demand, fighting for scraps. | No amount of offer optimization saves you. Exit or pivot. Pouring resources into optimization here is rearranging deck chairs. |
| **Standing still** | Stable but competitive. High turnover. | Success depends entirely on differentiation and economics. This is where offer architecture and positioning become decisive. |

## Three Scaling Problems

Diagnose in this exact order. Never skip ahead.

**1. Market Problem.** Market momentum is against you. CANNOT BE FIXED with better offers or messaging. The math is against you. If the total addressable market is shrinking, no amount of conversion rate optimization matters. Recognize it and pivot.

**2. Audience Problem.** Wrong people seeing your offer. CAN BE FIXED by changing targeting, repositioning, or shifting to an adjacent audience segment. Symptoms: high traffic but low conversion, wrong objections surfacing (wrong objections = audience problem, not copy problem), engagement from people who cannot afford or do not need the offer.

**3. Offer Problem.** Right people, wrong offer. CAN BE FIXED through offer architecture, pricing, positioning, mechanism design. Symptoms: the right audience engages but does not buy, correct objections surface but the offer does not address them, price sensitivity is higher than expected for the audience's resource profile.

## Diagnostic Rules

- Never try to fix an offer problem when you actually have a market problem.
- Never try to fix a copy problem when you actually have an offer problem.
- Always diagnose upstream first.
- When a creator says "my funnel isn't converting," the first question is market state, the second is audience targeting, and the third is offer structure. Copy comes last.

## Key Metrics by Level

| Level | What to Measure | Healthy Signal |
|-------|----------------|---------------|
| Market | Trend trajectory, TAM growth/decline | Expanding or stable with room |
| Audience | Traffic quality, objection type, ICP match rate | Right objections from right people |
| Offer | Conversion rate by awareness level, LTV:CAC | 3:1+ LTV:CAC, viable unit economics |
| Copy | Click-through, engagement, message-market match | High engagement from validated audience |

## Revenue Impact of Getting the Hierarchy Wrong

- Wrong market = all investment is wasted, regardless of everything else.
- Wrong audience = marketing spend wasted on people who will not buy.
- Wrong offer = decent traffic but abysmal conversion rates.
- Wrong copy = slight underperformance that is fixable, but only after the three levels above it check out.

The Foundations agent exists to get the top three levels right. Copy is downstream agents' territory.
