# The Impact Distribution Principle

**What it is:** The fixed distribution of what drives results in any creator business.
**When to apply:** Every time something underperforms. Every time you plan a new campaign. Every time a creator asks you to "fix the copy."

---

## The Distribution

| Component | Impact on Results | Where Creators Spend Time |
|-----------|------------------|--------------------------|
| Audience quality | 40% | ~10% |
| Offer strength | 40% | ~10% |
| Copy/messaging | 20% | ~80% |

This is the single most important diagnostic rule in the system. Most creators waste 80% of their effort on the component that drives only 20% of results, while ignoring the two components that together drive 80%.

## The Diagnostic Sequence

When anything underperforms, audit in this exact order:

1. **Audience first.** Are the right people seeing this? Is the targeting specific enough? Does the ICP actually exist in the numbers needed? If the audience is wrong, nothing downstream matters.

2. **Offer second.** Is the offer compelling to the validated audience? Does the transformation match their diagnosed limiting belief? Is the pricing aligned with their identity and resources? A strong offer to the right audience converts with mediocre copy.

3. **Copy third -- and only third.** Is the messaging calibrated to their awareness level? Does it speak their language? Copy optimization is the last lever, not the first.

## Operational Rules

- Never optimize copy before the audience is validated and the offer is proven.
- A strong offer to the right audience converts with mediocre copy. Great copy to the wrong audience or with a weak offer always loses.
- When something is not working, the fix is almost always upstream -- audience or offer, not messaging.
- This principle determines the entire build sequence: Market Research Brief, then ICP Document, then Positioning Document, then Offer Document. Copy is downstream territory and must never begin until Foundations outputs are complete.

## How This Changes Agent Behavior

When a creator says "my sales page isn't converting," do not start rewriting the sales page. Start by asking: Who is seeing this page? Then: What are they being offered? Only after both check out should you look at how the offer is communicated.

When planning any new campaign, the resource allocation should mirror the impact distribution: spend 40% of strategic effort on audience definition, 40% on offer architecture, and 20% on messaging. If the plan is copy-heavy and audience-light, it is wrong by default.

The Impact Distribution Principle is why Foundations exists as the root agent. Every other agent -- Marketing, Nurture, Sales, Launch, Scale -- operates in the 20% zone. Foundations owns the 80%.
