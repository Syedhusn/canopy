\t# **🚀 SOP: How to Create an Irresistible New Opportunity (FIOVA Method)**
\t**Purpose**: To create a *new opportunity* that positions your offer as a **completely different vehicle** from anything your audience has tried before — giving them renewed hope and removing objections *before they even arise.*
\t---
\t## **✅ SECTION 1: UNDERSTAND THE CORE STRATEGY**
\t### **🔥 Why “Improvement Offers” Fail**
\t<table>
<tr>
<td>**Insight**</td>
<td>**Explanation**</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>People associate their past failures with the **vehicles** they used.</td>
<td>They don’t believe they failed — they believe the **method** failed them.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Improvement offers trigger emotional resistance.</td>
<td>“I’ve already tried that. It didn’t work.”</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>To get someone to take action again, you must present a **new vehicle**.</td>
<td>Something they’ve **never failed with**. Something that **feels fresh and hopeful**.</td>
</tr>
\t</table>
\t💡 **Key Principle**: People don’t want “better.” They want “*different*.” Something that feels like an *escape*, not a grind.
\t---
\t## **✅ SECTION 2: THE FOUR LAWS OF A NEW OPPORTUNITY**
\t<table>
<tr>
<td>**Law**</td>
<td>**Description**</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>1. **New Vehicle**</td>
<td>It must be framed as something **completely new**, even if based on an existing concept.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>2. **Tied to Dream Outcome**</td>
<td>Must promise a result they deeply want (not just a feature).</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>3. **Easier Than What They've Tried**</td>
<td>Must feel *simpler, faster, and less painful* than past failures.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>4. **Contrasts with the Old Way**</td>
<td>Must show why old methods are **broken** or outdated — so they abandon them.</td>
</tr>
\t</table>
\t---
\t## **✅ SECTION 3: THE SOP — STEP-BY-STEP FRAMEWORK**
\t### **🔍 PHASE 1: Research & Deep Market Understanding**
\t<table>
<tr>
<td>**Step**</td>
<td>**Task**</td>
<td>**Tool/Prompt**</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>1. Identify the Dream Outcome</td>
<td>What does your audience *truly* want?</td>
<td>“If you could wave a magic wand, what would your life/business look like in 6 months?”</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>2. List Old Vehicles They’ve Tried</td>
<td>What methods have they used before?</td>
<td>“What have you tried before that didn’t work?”</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>3. Capture Emotional Scars</td>
<td>What beliefs do they now hold about themselves or the process?</td>
<td>“I’m not consistent.” “I don’t have time.” “I guess I’m just not good at marketing.”</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>4. Create the “Blame Frame”</td>
<td>What can we help them blame externally instead of blaming themselves?</td>
<td>“You weren’t lazy. You were just using a broken system.”</td>
</tr>
\t</table>
\t---
\t### **🧪 PHASE 2: Engineer the New Vehicle**
\t<table>
<tr>
<td>**Step**</td>
<td>**Task**</td>
<td>**Tool/Prompt**</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>5. Invent a Unique Mechanism</td>
<td>What new **methodology**, **framework**, or **angle** delivers the result?</td>
<td>Combine or simplify existing ideas under a new name.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>6. Name It</td>
<td>Give it a brandable, proprietary name.</td>
<td>“Verb + Result + System” e.g. “DM Reactor Protocol”</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>7. Category Creation</td>
<td>Reframe it as a whole **new way of doing things**.</td>
<td>“This isn’t content marketing — it’s Authority Sequencing™.”</td>
</tr>
\t</table>
\t---
\t### **🎭 PHASE 3: Craft the Narrative and Message**
\t<table>
<tr>
<td>**Step**</td>
<td>**Task**</td>
<td>**Tool/Prompt**</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>8. Write the Big Domino Statement</td>
<td>Core positioning belief that makes all other objections irrelevant.</td>
<td>“If I can get them to believe that \\[New Vehicle\\] is the key to \\[Result\\], they’ll have no choice but to buy.”</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>9. Tell the Hero’s Journey</td>
<td>Show how *you* or a client failed with old methods and found success with the new vehicle.</td>
<td>Use: Problem → Struggle → Breakthrough → New Vehicle → Result</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>10. Break the Old Vehicle</td>
<td>Use storytelling, stats, or logic to show why the old way doesn’t work anymore.</td>
<td>“Here’s why content alone will never get you consistent leads…”</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>11. Build Proof</td>
<td>Collect testimonials, screenshots, or your own before/after story showing success with the new opportunity.</td>
<td>Even 1–2 “proof points” validate your claim.</td>
</tr>
\t</table>
