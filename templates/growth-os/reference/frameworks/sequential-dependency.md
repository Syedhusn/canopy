# Sequential Dependency and Build Order

**What it is:** The mandatory sequence for producing Foundations outputs. Each step has strict causal dependencies on the previous step. Skipping steps or building out of order creates cascading errors that corrupt every downstream agent's work.
**When to apply:** Every time a new creator engagement begins. When determining what to build next. When a creator wants to jump to offer design before audience definition is complete.

---

## The Mandatory Sequence

```
1. Market Research Brief
   └── 2. ICP Document
         └── 3. Positioning Document
               └── 4. Offer Document
                     └── 5. Offer Repositioning Document
```

### Step 1: Market Research Brief

**Validates:** Is this market worth entering? What is the market state? What maturity stage? What does the competitive landscape look like? Is there a viable path to 3:1 LTV:CAC?

**Output:** Go / Go With Conditions / No-Go recommendation with specific evidence.

**Why it must come first:** Everything that follows assumes the market is viable. If the market is contracting or the economics are non-viable, building an ICP and offer for it wastes resources and creates false confidence.

### Step 2: ICP Document

**Defines:** Who exactly are we serving? What is their limiting belief diagnosis? What awareness level? What do they want, fear, and secretly desire? What are their capabilities and resources? How do they buy?

**Requires:** Market Research Brief (or explicit override -- creator has existing market validation through an operating business).

**Why it requires Step 1:** The ICP cannot be built without knowing the market is viable. A perfect audience profile for a dead market is worthless.

### Step 3: Positioning Document

**Establishes:** What is the new vehicle? What category are we creating or entering? What is the Core Belief Statement? What old vehicles are we replacing? What is the mechanism and its name?

**Requires:** ICP Document.

**Why it requires Step 2:** Positioning cannot be set without knowing WHO you are positioning to. The vehicle switch must reference specific old vehicles the ICP has tried. The mechanism must address their diagnosed limiting belief. The Core Belief Statement must target their specific desire. Without the ICP, positioning becomes generic.

### Step 4: Offer Document

**Architects:** What is the complete offer structure? Pricing tiers, risk reversal, feedback loops, upsell ecosystem, USP, delivery model, economics validation.

**Requires:** ICP Document AND Positioning Document.

**Why it requires Steps 2 and 3:** The offer cannot be architected without knowing what vehicle and category it lives in (from positioning) and who it serves at the deepest level (from ICP). Pricing needs the ICP's resource profile. Risk reversal needs the ICP's specific fears. Tier structure needs the ICP's capability distribution. The mechanism name appears in the USP.

### Step 5: Offer Repositioning Document

**Adapts:** How do we take an existing offer and shift it for a new ICP? What messaging changes? What pricing adjustments? What congruency checks are needed?

**Requires:** Existing Offer Document AND new target ICP.

**Why it requires Step 4:** Repositioning cannot happen without a complete offer to reposition. The document compares the original ICP to the new ICP and maps every delta to a structural or messaging adjustment.

## Exception Handling

Creators who already have an existing offer and audience may enter at Step 2 or Step 3, using their existing business as the Market Research Brief equivalent. The agent must validate this explicitly -- not assume it. An existing business is not automatically a validated market. The questions from Step 1 still need answers, even if the answers come from operating history rather than fresh research.

## Why This Order is Non-Negotiable

The most common failure pattern in creator businesses: building the offer first, audience second. The creator builds what THEY want to sell, not what the market wants to buy. The Impact Distribution Principle is clear -- audience and offer together drive 80% of results, and audience must be understood before the offer can be architected.

Every document in the sequence feeds specific data to the next. Remove any step, and the downstream documents are built on assumptions instead of evidence. Assumptions compound. By the time you reach the Offer Document with skipped steps, you are building on a foundation of guesses -- and the offer will show it in conversion rates.
