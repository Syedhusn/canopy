## PART ONE: SALES SCRIPTS (7 Types)

### Type 1: Closer Script

**Purpose:** Convert qualified, booked prospects into paying customers through a 30-45 minute consultative conversation.

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes
**Talk Ratio:** Salesperson 30% / Prospect 70%
**Target Close Rate:** 20-40% of shown calls
**Prerequisites:** Application form completed, call prep brief available

#### Phase 1: Rapport [0:00-3:00]

```
[Coaching: Goal is comfort and frame-setting. Not small talk.
You have 3 minutes to establish trust and take control of the call.]

OPENER:
"Hey [Name], thanks for taking the time today. I've been looking
forward to this -- I read through your application and I'm really
curious about [SPECIFIC DETAIL FROM APPLICATION]. Before we dive
in, let me tell you how this call works so we make the best use
of your time."

FRAME-SET:
"I'm going to ask you some questions to really understand your
situation. Then, if it seems like we're a good fit to help, I'll
share exactly how we do that. And if we're not a fit, I'll tell
you that honestly and point you in the right direction. Fair enough?"

[Coaching: Getting permission to lead eliminates resistance later.
If they say "sure" or "sounds good," you have implicit permission
to control the conversation flow.]

TRANSITION:
"Great. So tell me a little about what's going on with
[their business/situation]..."
```

#### Phase 2: Discovery [3:00-20:00]

```
[Coaching: This is where the sale is made or lost. Your job is to
listen, not talk. Take notes using their EXACT words. You will
mirror these back later.]

--- SITUATION QUESTIONS (3-5 min) ---

"Walk me through where things stand right now with [their business]."

"How long have you been doing [X]?"

"What does a typical [day/week/month] look like for you right now?"

"What are you currently doing to [address the problem]?"

[Coaching: Listen for specifics. Revenue numbers, team size, time
investment, current strategies. These become your ammunition later.]

--- PAIN QUESTIONS (5-7 min) ---

"What's the single biggest challenge you're facing right now
when it comes to [topic]?"

[Let them answer fully. Then go deeper:]

"Help me understand -- when you say [THEIR EXACT WORDS], what does
that actually look like on a day-to-day basis?"

"How is this affecting you personally? Not just the business,
but you?"

"If you had to put a number on it -- what has this cost you in
the last [6/12] months? In revenue, time, opportunity?"

"And what happens if nothing changes in the next 12 months?"

[Coaching: The "cost of inaction" question is the most important
question on the call. Their answer becomes your price anchor later.
Let them feel the weight of their own answer.]

--- DESIRE QUESTIONS (3-4 min) ---

"Okay, so on the flip side -- if you could wave a magic wand
and have this completely solved, what would that look like?"

"What would change in your life -- not just your business --
if you had [their desired outcome]?"

"What's your timeline for making this happen? When do you want
to be at [their desired state]?"

"And what made you book this call specifically? What triggered
the decision to reach out now?"

[Coaching: Their answer to "why now" reveals urgency level. If
they have a real trigger, urgency is built in. If not, you may
need to create it through cost-of-waiting awareness.]

--- DECISION QUESTIONS (2-3 min) ---

"Have you invested in help with this before?"

[If yes:] "What happened? What worked and what didn't?"

[Coaching: If they invested before and it failed, they will
have trust objections. Address this proactively: "So what you
need is something fundamentally different from what you tried
before -- would you agree?"]

"Is there anyone else involved in making a decision like this?
A partner, business partner, spouse?"

[If yes:] "Great. Would it make sense to have them on the
call, or would you prefer to bring the information back?"

"Last question before I share some thoughts -- if I could show
you a clear path from where you are now to [their desired
outcome], and you felt confident it would work, is there
anything that would hold you back from getting started?"

[Coaching: This is the isolation question. Whatever they say
here is the real objection. Address it proactively before
the pitch. If they say "nothing" -- they are pre-closing
themselves.]
```

#### Phase 3: Prescription [20:00-23:00]

```
[Coaching: This is the bridge between discovery and pitch.
Mirror their exact words back to them. When they hear their
own language, trust deepens dramatically.]

"Okay [Name], let me make sure I have this right. You're
currently [THEIR SITUATION IN THEIR WORDS]. The biggest
challenge is [THEIR PAIN IN THEIR WORDS], and that's costing
you roughly [THEIR COST ESTIMATE]. Ideally, you'd love to
[THEIR DESIRED OUTCOME IN THEIR WORDS] within [THEIR TIMELINE].
Is that accurate?"

[Wait for confirmation. If they correct anything, update.]

"Based on everything you've shared, I actually think we can
help. Would you be open to me showing you how?"

[Coaching: Permission-based transition. Do NOT skip this.
Launching into the pitch without permission creates resistance.]
```

#### Phase 4: Presentation [23:00-33:00]

```
[Coaching: Present the offer mapped to THEIR specific situation.
Every component you mention should connect to a specific pain
they shared in discovery.]

TRANSFORMATION:
"So here's what we do at [program name]. We take [ICP description]
who are exactly where you are -- [their situation] -- and we help
them get to [their desired outcome] using our [Mechanism Name]."

MECHANISM:
"The reason this works, and the reason it's different from
[what they tried before], is [1-2 sentence mechanism explanation].
Instead of [old approach that failed], we [new approach]."

SOCIAL PROOF:
"Let me give you an example. [Name] came to us in a really
similar situation -- [brief parallel to prospect]. Within
[timeframe], they [specific result]. And that's not an outlier.
[Second proof point]."

OFFER COMPONENTS:
"Here's specifically what you'd get:

First, [Component 1] -- this is how we solve [their specific
pain #1]. So instead of [current frustration], you'd have
[specific outcome].

Second, [Component 2] -- this addresses [their specific pain #2].
Remember when you said [THEIR EXACT WORDS]? This is exactly
what fixes that.

Third, [Component 3] -- this is for [their specific need].
[Brief outcome description]."

BONUSES:
"And we also include [Bonus 1], which helps with [specific
objection they mentioned or implied]. Plus [Bonus 2], which
[addresses another concern]."

GUARANTEE:
"And here's our guarantee: [exact guarantee from offer document].
So the risk is essentially on us, not you."

[Coaching: Pause after the guarantee. Let it sink in. The
guarantee is a belief accelerator -- it removes the last
barrier before price.]
```

#### Phase 5: Close [33:00-38:00]

```
[Coaching: State the price with confidence. Do NOT apologize
for the price, qualify it, or minimize it. State it and stop.]

"So the investment for [program name] is [PRICE]."

[PAUSE. Say nothing. Count to 10 in your head if you need
to. The first person to speak after the price loses leverage.
Let them process.]

[If they ask about payment options:]
"We do have a payment plan -- [X] payments of $[amount]
over [Y] months. Would that work better for your situation?"

[If they're ready:]
"Great. Here's how we get you started. I'm going to
[send you the enrollment link / walk you through the
agreement / take your payment info]. You'll get
[immediate next steps] and we'll have you [first milestone]
by [date]. Sound good?"

[If they need to think about it -- move to objection handling.]
```

#### Phase 6: Objection Handling

```
--- "I NEED TO THINK ABOUT IT" ---

[Coaching: This is NEVER the real objection. Something
specific is unresolved. Your job is to uncover it.]

"I completely understand -- this is a big decision and I
respect that you want to make the right one. Can I ask you
something? When you say you need to think about it, is it
more about the investment, the timing, or whether you
believe this is the right approach for your situation?"

[Listen to their answer. That's the real objection.
Address THAT.]

--- "IT'S TOO EXPENSIVE" ---

"I hear you. Let me ask you this -- you mentioned earlier
that [their pain] has cost you roughly [THEIR OWN ESTIMATE]
over the last [timeframe]. If [program] helps you solve that
in [timeline], what's the ROI on a [PRICE] investment?"

[Pause. Let them do the math.]

"And we do have a payment plan that breaks it down to
[monthly amount]. Would that make it more workable?"

--- "I'VE TRIED BEFORE AND IT DIDN'T WORK" ---

"That actually tells me something important -- it means
you're serious enough to have invested in solving this.
Can I ask what specifically didn't work about [what they
tried]?"

[Listen. Then:]

"What you're describing is [wrong vehicle / wrong
implementation / wrong timing]. Our [Mechanism Name] is
fundamentally different because [differentiation]. And
that's exactly why [case study name] -- who had the same
experience with [similar thing] -- got [result] with us."

--- "NOT THE RIGHT TIME" ---

"I totally get that. Let me ask you -- what would need to
change for it to be the right time?"

[Listen. Then:]

"Here's what I've noticed with the [X] people we've worked
with: [their pain] doesn't improve with time. It compounds.
You mentioned it's already cost you [their estimate]. What
does another [6/12] months of that look like?"

--- "I NEED TO TALK TO MY PARTNER" ---

"Absolutely -- that's important. Would it be helpful if we
set up a quick call where your [partner/spouse] can ask
questions too? Sometimes it's easier to explain it together
than to relay secondhand."

[If they decline:]
"No problem. What would be most helpful for you to bring
back to them? I can also send you a summary of everything
we discussed with the key points highlighted."

--- POST-OBJECTION RE-CLOSE ---

"Does that address your concern? [Wait for yes.] Great --
so if that piece is resolved, is there anything else holding
you back from getting started?"

[If nothing else:] "Awesome. Let's get you enrolled."
```

#### Post-Call Protocol

```
REGARDLESS OF OUTCOME:
1. Update CRM with call notes, outcome, and next action
2. Log objections encountered (for team learning)
3. Send follow-up email within 1 hour
4. Trigger appropriate next sequence (won/lost/follow-up)

IF CLOSED:
- Send enrollment link and welcome email
- Trigger onboarding sequence
- Notify fulfillment team

IF FOLLOW-UP NEEDED:
- Book specific follow-up date/time
- Send summary email with key points
- Set CRM reminder

IF LOST:
- Document specific reason
- Send graceful close email
- Move to long-term nurture sequence
```

### Type 2: Setter Script

**Purpose:** Qualify inbound leads and book them for a closer call.
**Duration:** 10-15 minutes
**Talk Ratio:** Setter 40% / Prospect 60%
**Target Book Rate:** 40-60% of contacted leads
**Target Show Rate:** 70-85% of booked calls

```
OPENING [0:00-1:00]:
"Hey [Name], this is [your name] from [company]. I'm reaching
out because you [trigger: filled out an application / registered
for our training / messaged us about [topic]]. Do you have a
quick minute?"

[If bad time:] "No worries -- when would be a better time for
a 10-minute call? I just want to make sure we can actually
help you before we schedule anything further."

CONTEXT [1:00-2:00]:
"Great. So I saw that you [reference their action]. I just
want to learn a little about your situation so we can figure
out if it makes sense to set up a deeper conversation with
one of our [strategists/coaches]. This will only take about
10 minutes. Sound good?"

QUALIFICATION [2:00-8:00]:
"So tell me -- what's going on with [their situation]? What
made you [take the trigger action]?"

[Listen for 60-90 seconds. Then:]

"Got it. And how long has this been going on?"

"What have you tried so far?"

"If you could solve this, what would that mean for you?"

"Is this something you're actively trying to fix right now,
or more of a long-term goal?"

"And just so I can make sure we connect you with the right
person -- are you the one who makes decisions about
investments like this in your business?"

BRIDGE [8:00-10:00]:
[If qualified:]
"Based on what you're telling me, I think [closer name /
strategist] would be the perfect person for you to talk to.
They work specifically with [ICP description] who are dealing
with [their situation]. They'll be able to map out a specific
plan for you. I have some times available this week -- would
[day] at [time] or [day] at [time] work better?"

[If not qualified:]
"I appreciate you sharing that. Honestly, based on where
you're at right now, I think [alternative resource] might
be a better fit. Let me send you [resource]. And when you
get to the point where [qualification criteria], we'd love
to chat again."

BOOKING [10:00-12:00]:
"Great, I've got you locked in for [day] at [time] with
[closer name]. Here's what to expect: it's a [duration]
call where [closer name] will dive deep into your situation
and show you exactly how we'd help. A few things to make
the most of it:

1. Be in a quiet place where you can focus
2. Have your [relevant info] handy
3. [Closer name] is going to ask you some detailed questions,
   so be ready to be open about where things stand

You'll get a confirmation email with a calendar invite in
the next few minutes. Any questions?"

CLOSE [12:00-13:00]:
"Awesome. One last thing -- on a scale of 1-10, how
committed are you to showing up for this call?"

[If below 8:] "What would make it a 10?"

"Perfect. [Closer name] is really looking forward to helping
you with [their specific situation]. Talk soon, [Name]."
```

### Type 3: Dialer/SDR Script

**Purpose:** Quick outbound calls to qualify leads and book setter or closer calls.
**Duration:** 5-8 minutes
**Target:** 15-25% conversation rate from dials

```
OPENER [0:00-0:30]:
"Hey [Name], this is [your name] with [company]. You recently
[trigger action -- downloaded our guide / attended our webinar /
visited our page]. I'm just calling to see if you had any
questions about [topic]."

[If gatekeeper:] "I'm following up on something [Name]
requested from us. Could you put me through?"

QUICK QUALIFY [0:30-3:00]:
"Totally understand you're busy -- I'll be quick. I noticed
you [trigger action]. What caught your interest about [topic]?"

[Listen for 30-60 seconds.]

"Are you currently [doing the thing your offer helps with]?"

"And is this something you're looking to solve in the next
[30/60/90] days?"

BRIDGE [3:00-5:00]:
[If qualified:]
"It sounds like you'd benefit from talking to someone on our
team who specializes in exactly this. Can I get you booked
for a quick 15-minute call this week?"

[If not ready:]
"No worries at all. Would it be okay if I sent you [relevant
resource] and checked back in a few weeks?"

BOOK + CLOSE [5:00-7:00]:
"Great. Does [day] at [time] work? Perfect. You'll get a
confirmation at [email]. Look forward to helping you with
[their specific interest]. Have a great day, [Name]."
```

### Type 4: Follow-Up Script

**Purpose:** Re-engage no-shows, think-it-overs, and lapsed leads.
**Duration:** 5-15 minutes depending on scenario

#### Scenario A: No-Show Follow-Up

```
"Hey [Name], it's [your name] from [company]. We had a call
scheduled [today/yesterday] and I know things come up. No
worries at all. I still have your application and I'd love
to help you with [their specific challenge]. What's the best
time to reschedule? I have [two options] this week."

[Coaching: No guilt. No passive-aggression. Pure helpfulness.
People who no-show and get a non-judgmental follow-up often
become the most committed clients.]
```

#### Scenario B: "Think About It" Follow-Up (48-72h later)

```
"Hey [Name], it's [your name]. We spoke [X days ago] about
[their situation]. I've been thinking about what you shared,
and I actually came across something that might help --
[relevant insight, resource, or case study].

I also wanted to ask: have you had a chance to think through
what we discussed? Any questions that came up?"

[Coaching: Lead with value, not pressure. The resource or
insight proves you were thinking about them. Then naturally
check in on the decision.]
```

#### Scenario C: Long-Term Re-Engagement (30-90 days cold)

```
"Hey [Name], it's [your name] from [company]. We spoke a
while back about [their challenge]. I know the timing wasn't
right then, and I totally respected that.

I'm reaching out because [relevant trigger: we just helped
someone in a similar situation / we have a new resource /
something changed that's relevant to them]. Is [their
challenge] still something on your radar?"

[Coaching: Reference the original conversation specifically.
Show that you remember them. Provide a legitimate reason
for reaching out.]
```

### Type 5: Objection Library

**Purpose:** Comprehensive reference document for the entire sales team.

The Objection Library is structured as a lookup table organized by objection category. Each entry follows the LAER framework.

#### Structure Per Objection:

```
## [OBJECTION CATEGORY]: "[Exact objection language]"

**Frequency:** [How often this comes up: High/Medium/Low]
**Belief Category:** [Worthless/Helpless/Hopeless]
**Real Concern:** [What's actually driving this]
**ICP Segments:** [Which segments voice this most]

### LAER Response

**Listen:** Let them finish. Take note of their exact phrasing.

**Acknowledge:**
"[Specific acknowledgment script]"

**Explore:**
"[Probing question to uncover the real concern]"

**Respond:**

*If the concern is [variant A]:*
"[Response using Story method -- case study of someone
with same concern]"

*If the concern is [variant B]:*
"[Response using Reframe method -- perspective shift]"

*If the concern is [variant C]:*
"[Response using Proof method -- data or testimonial]"

### Proof Points to Deploy
1. [Specific case study or data point]
2. [Specific testimonial quote]
3. [Specific reframe statement]

### Follow-Up Question
"Does that address your concern? [If yes:] Is there
anything else holding you back?"
```

#### Objection Categories to Cover:

1. **Price/Money** -- "Too expensive" / "Can't afford it" / "Not in the budget" / "I found something cheaper"
2. **Timing** -- "Not the right time" / "Too busy" / "Maybe next quarter" / "Let me finish [X] first"
3. **Trust** -- "How do I know this works?" / "What's your track record?" / "I don't know enough about you"
4. **Authority** -- "I need to talk to my [partner/spouse/boss]" / "I'm not the decision-maker"
5. **Past Failure** -- "I've tried before" / "Nothing has worked" / "I've been burned"
6. **Self-Doubt** -- "I'm not sure I can do it" / "I'm not [qualified/experienced/ready] enough"
7. **DIY** -- "I can figure it out myself" / "I'll just use [free resource]" / "I don't need help"
8. **Differentiation** -- "How is this different?" / "Why not [competitor]?" / "What makes you special?"
9. **Risk** -- "What if it doesn't work?" / "What's the guarantee?" / "Can I get a refund?"
10. **Scope** -- "Is this right for my specific situation?" / "My case is different" / "This won't work for [my niche]"

### Type 6: Upgrade/Upsell Script

**Purpose:** Move existing clients to higher-tier offers.
**Duration:** 15-25 minutes
**Target:** 20-35% upgrade rate

```
RAPPORT [0:00-2:00]:
"Hey [Name], great to connect again. I wanted to check in
because I've been watching your progress with [current program]
and I'm genuinely impressed with [specific achievement]. How
are you feeling about things?"

DISCOVERY [2:00-10:00]:
"What's working best for you right now in the program?"

"Where are you feeling stuck or like you could be getting
more from it?"

"What's the next big milestone you're working toward?"

"What would it take to get there faster?"

[Coaching: The discovery should surface the gap between
where they are and where they want to be that the upgrade
fills.]

BRIDGE [10:00-12:00]:
"Based on what you're telling me, it sounds like you've
outgrown [current tier] -- in a good way. You've done the
foundational work, and now you need [specific support the
upgrade provides]. That's exactly why we built [upgrade
tier name]."

PRESENTATION [12:00-18:00]:
"[Upgrade tier] is designed for people who are exactly
where you are -- they've [achieved X with current tier]
and now they need [specific additional support] to reach
[next level].

Here's what's different: [Key differentiators from current
tier, each mapped to their stated needs]."

CLOSE [18:00-22:00]:
"The upgrade investment is [price difference or new price].
Since you're already a member, [loyalty pricing / credit /
seamless transition detail].

Honestly, [Name], based on what you've achieved so far and
where you want to go, I think this is the natural next step.
What do you think?"
```

### Type 7: Referral Ask Script

**Purpose:** Generate referrals from satisfied clients.
**Duration:** 5-10 minutes
**Target:** 15-25% referral rate

```
CONTEXT [0:00-1:00]:
"Hey [Name], I'm reaching out because you've been absolutely
crushing it with [specific results they've achieved]. I
genuinely love seeing your progress."

THE ASK [1:00-3:00]:
"I have a question for you, and feel free to say no -- is
there anyone in your world who's dealing with [the problem
you help solve]? Someone who's in a similar position to where
you were when you started?"

[Coaching: Be specific about who. "Do you know anyone" gets
vague answers. "Is there a [specific description] in your
network" gets names.]

FACILITATE [3:00-6:00]:
[If they have someone:]
"Amazing. What's the best way to connect? I could [have you
introduce us / reach out directly and mention you / send you
a message to forward]. What feels most natural?"

[If they need time:]
"No rush at all. If someone comes to mind, just shoot me
a text or email and I'll take great care of them. As a
thank-you, [referral incentive if applicable]."

CLOSE [6:00-7:00]:
"Thanks, [Name]. It means a lot that you'd trust us with
someone in your network. And keep up the momentum --
[encouragement about their progress]."
```

## PART TWO: DM SEQUENCES (5 Types)

---

### Type 1: Cold DM Sequence

**Purpose:** Start a conversation with a prospect who has no prior engagement with the brand.
**Messages:** 5-7 over 2-3 weeks
**Platform:** Instagram (primary) / LinkedIn (adapted version)
**Goal:** Book a call or start a qualifying conversation

#### Pre-Sequence Research Protocol

Before sending Message 1:
1. Read their bio carefully -- identify their role, business, and current focus
2. Review their last 10-15 posts for language patterns, current challenges, wins
3. Check mutual connections or shared interests
4. Identify 2-3 specific personalization hooks
5. Determine their likely awareness level (problem-aware vs. solution-aware)

#### Message 1: Connection [Day 1]

**Instagram Version:**
```
"Hey [PERSONALIZE: first name] -- I came across your
[PERSONALIZE: specific post/reel/story about specific topic]
and wanted to say [PERSONALIZE: genuine, specific compliment
about the content or insight].

Are you finding that [PERSONALIZE: question related to a
challenge they seem to be working on based on their content]?"
```

**LinkedIn Version:**
```
"Hi [PERSONALIZE: first name] -- I noticed your
[PERSONALIZE: post/article/comment about specific topic]
and it really resonated. [PERSONALIZE: specific insight
you took from it].

Curious -- [PERSONALIZE: relevant question about their
current focus or challenge]?"
```

**Voice Message Talking Points (High-Ticket, Instagram):**
- Say their name warmly
- Reference the specific content you saw (10 sec)
- Share why it stood out to you (10 sec)
- Ask one genuine question (10 sec)
- Sign off with your name (5 sec)

**If positive response** -> Message 2A
**If question/engagement** -> Message 2B
**No response after 72h** -> Follow-Up 1

#### Message 2A: Curiosity [Day 2-3, after positive response]

```
"That's really interesting -- so you're currently
[PERSONALIZE: reflect what they shared]. How long have
you been [working on/dealing with] that?

I ask because I work with a lot of [PERSONALIZE: their
type/role/description] and [PERSONALIZE: relevant
observation that shows you understand their world]."
```

#### Message 2B: Curiosity [Day 2-3, after question response]

```
"Great question. [PERSONALIZE: brief, genuine answer to
their question -- 2 sentences max].

What's your experience been with [PERSONALIZE: the topic
they asked about]?"
```

#### Message 3: Value + Qualification [Day 5-7]

```
"You know what, based on what you've shared -- I actually
have a [PERSONALIZE: resource/framework/insight] that might
be helpful for [PERSONALIZE: their specific situation].

Quick question though -- are you at the point where you're
actively looking to [PERSONALIZE: solve the problem / achieve
the outcome], or is it more of a long-term thing?"
```

#### Message 4: Bridge [Day 8-10, if qualified]

```
"Makes sense. So I help [ICP description] with exactly
this -- specifically [PERSONALIZE: 1-sentence mechanism
description relevant to their situation].

Would it be helpful if we jumped on a quick call so I
could learn more about your situation and see if I have
anything useful to share? No pitch, just a conversation.
[TIME OPTION]?"
```

#### Follow-Up 1: [72h after last unanswered message]

```
"Hey [PERSONALIZE: first name] -- totally understand if
you're slammed. I actually just [PERSONALIZE: saw/read/heard
something relevant to their situation] and thought of you.

[PERSONALIZE: 1-2 sentences sharing the insight or resource]

No pressure at all -- just wanted to pass it along."
```

#### Follow-Up 2: [72h after Follow-Up 1, if still no response]

```
"Hey [PERSONALIZE: first name], I know things get busy.
Just wanted to drop one more thing -- [PERSONALIZE:
different value piece, case study result, or resource].

If the timing's not right, totally get it. I'm here
whenever it makes sense."
```

#### Graceful Exit: [If 2 follow-ups unanswered]

```
"Hey [PERSONALIZE: first name] -- no worries at all.
I'll leave you be. If [PERSONALIZE: their challenge/goal]
ever becomes a priority, I'm a message away. Wishing
you all the best with [PERSONALIZE: something specific
about their business/project]."
```

### Type 2: Warm DM Sequence

**Purpose:** Convert engaged followers into calls/purchases.
**Messages:** 3-5 over 5-10 days
**Trigger:** They have liked multiple posts, commented, watched stories, or engaged regularly.
**Goal:** Book a call

#### Message 1: Acknowledge Engagement [Day 1]

```
"Hey [PERSONALIZE: first name] -- I've noticed you've
been engaging with my content about [PERSONALIZE: specific
topic they've engaged with most]. Thanks for that -- it
means a lot.

I'm curious -- is [PERSONALIZE: the topic] something
you're actively working on right now?"
```

#### Message 2: Deepen [Day 2-3]

```
"That's exactly what I hear from a lot of [PERSONALIZE:
their description]. The biggest thing I've found is that
[PERSONALIZE: one key insight from your methodology].

What's been your biggest roadblock with [PERSONALIZE:
their specific challenge]?"
```

#### Message 3: Value + Bridge [Day 4-6]

```
"You know what -- based on what you've told me, I think
[PERSONALIZE: specific recommendation or insight].

I actually work with [ICP description] on exactly this.
Would it be helpful to jump on a quick call? I can take
a look at your situation and give you some specific
recommendations. [TIME OPTION]"
```

## DM Platform Adaptation Guide

### Instagram Norms
- Keep messages under 3 sentences
- Use voice messages for anything longer
- React to stories as engagement warmup before DMing
- Avoid links in first 2 messages (triggers spam)
- Casual tone, contractions, conversational
- Emojis sparingly (max 1 per message, not in every message)

### LinkedIn Norms
- Slightly more professional tone but still personal
- Connection request note is the first touch
- Can include links earlier (expected on LinkedIn)
- Reference their company, role, or content specifically
- Shorter than you think -- LinkedIn DMs are scanned

### Twitter/X Norms
- Most casual platform
- Start with a public reply or quote tweet before DMing
- Very short messages (tweet-length mentality)
- Build public rapport first
- GIFs acceptable in the right context
