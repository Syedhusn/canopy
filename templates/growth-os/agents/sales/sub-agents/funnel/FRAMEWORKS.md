# Funnel Frameworks -- Complete Playbook

> Page-by-page copy frameworks for all 7 funnel types. Each framework contains the complete
> architecture, every page with section-by-section copy structure, email sequences, and
> conversion psychology. Every framework enforces the 3-second test and mobile-first design.

---

## Universal Funnel Principles

### The Micro-Commitment Ladder

Every funnel is a sequence of micro-commitments. Each page asks for one small "yes" that makes the next "yes" easier:

```
Click ad (low commitment) -> Read page (medium) -> Enter email (medium-high)
-> Watch VSL (high) -> Fill application (very high) -> Book call (very high)
-> Show up (extremely high) -> Purchase (maximum)
```

Each page's job is to earn exactly ONE next step. A page that tries to do two things does neither well.

### The 3-Second Test (Every Page)

When a visitor lands on any page, they must be able to answer within 3 seconds:
1. Who is this for?
2. What will I get/learn/experience?
3. What do I do next?

If any of the three is unclear above the fold, the page fails.

### Social Proof Placement Protocol

| Position on Page | Proof Type | Purpose |
|-----------------|-----------|---------|
| Above the fold (near headline) | Logo bar or "as seen in" | Instant credibility |
| After problem agitation | Testimonial expressing the same pain | "Others feel this too" |
| After mechanism introduction | Case study with before/after | "It actually works" |
| After price reveal | ROI testimonial | "It's worth it" |
| After guarantee | Satisfaction/trust testimonial | "You really can't lose" |
| Above final CTA | Recency proof | "Others are taking action" |

### Mobile-First Copy Rules

- Headlines: 8 words maximum (must fit on one mobile line)
- Paragraphs: 3 sentences maximum
- Bullet points: 1 line each on mobile
- CTA buttons: Large enough for thumb tap, text 4 words maximum
- Form fields: 3 maximum on mobile (name, email, phone)
- Video: Autoplay muted with captions option

## Type 1: VSL Funnel

### Architecture
```
[Ad/Traffic] -> Opt-In Page -> VSL Page -> Application/Checkout -> Thank You Page
                                  |
                          [Retarget Non-Completers]
```

### Page 1: Opt-In Page

**Purpose:** Collect email in exchange for access to the VSL. Creates a lead even if they do not watch the full VSL.

**Above the Fold:**
- Pre-headline: "Free Training for [ICP Description]"
- Headline: "How [ICP] Are [Achieving Result] Without [Main Objection]" (8-12 words)
- Sub-headline: "Watch this [X]-minute video to discover the [Mechanism Name] that [number] [ICP] are using to [specific outcome]" (15-25 words)
- Hero: Video thumbnail or image of creator with play button overlay
- Form: Name + Email (2 fields only)
- CTA Button: "Watch The Free Training" or "Show Me How"

**Below the Fold:**
- 3 bullet points of what they will learn (outcome-framed):
  - "Why [common approach] is actually keeping you stuck at [pain level]"
  - "The [number]-step [Mechanism Name] that [proof statement]"
  - "How to [quick win] in the next [short timeframe] without [sacrifice]"
- Social proof bar: "[X] people have watched this training" or testimonial snippet
- About the creator: 2-3 sentences + headshot
- Second CTA: Same as above

**Email Sequence (Post Opt-In):**

| # | Timing | Subject Line Template | Purpose |
|---|--------|----------------------|---------|
| 1 | Immediate | "Your training is ready (watch now)" | Deliver access link |
| 2 | 24h | "[Name], did you catch this part?" | Re-engage non-watchers |
| 3 | 48h | "The #1 mistake [ICP] make with [topic]" | Value + remind |
| 4 | 72h | "[Case study name]'s story will surprise you" | Social proof |
| 5 | 96h | "Last chance to watch before [reason]" | Urgency |

### Page 2: VSL Page

**Purpose:** Play the VSL and convert viewers to the next step (application or checkout).

**Above the Fold:**
- Headline: Matches the VSL's opening promise
- Video player: Auto-play enabled, no seek bar until 80% watched
- Below video (appears after X% watched): CTA button

**Below the Fold (revealed progressively or via scroll):**
- Offer summary (mirrors VSL pitch section)
- Value stack in visual format
- 3-5 testimonial blocks
- FAQ (5-7 questions matching VSL objections)
- Guarantee badge/section
- Final CTA

**Retargeting Triggers:**
- Watched 25% but left: Retarget with "Did you see the part about [teaser]?"
- Watched 75% but did not apply: Retarget with case study or testimonial
- Visited but did not watch: Retarget with alternative hook

### Page 3: Application / Checkout

**For High-Ticket (Application):**
- Short application form: 5-8 questions that qualify AND build commitment
- Questions progress from easy to invested:
  1. "What's your current [relevant metric]?" (factual, easy)
  2. "What's your biggest challenge with [topic]?" (reflective)
  3. "What have you tried before?" (builds awareness of past failures)
  4. "What would solving this be worth to you?" (anchors value)
  5. "Are you ready to invest in a solution this month?" (commitment)
- After submission: Redirect to calendar booking page or confirmation

**For Low/Mid-Ticket (Checkout):**
- Order summary with value stack
- Price with value comparison
- Guarantee badge
- Security badges (SSL, payment processor logos)
- 1-2 testimonials focused on "I was hesitant but..." narratives
- Simple checkout form: name, email, payment
- Order bump option (related low-ticket add-on)

### Page 4: Thank You Page

**Purpose:** Reduce buyer's remorse, build excitement, create next conversion opportunity.

- Confirmation message: "You're in! Here's what happens next..."
- Next steps timeline (numbered, clear)
- Welcome video from creator (60-90 seconds, personal, excited)
- For high-ticket: Calendar booking widget if not already booked
- Social share prompt: "Know someone who needs this?"
- Upsell opportunity: "While you're here, [X]% of new members also grab [bonus/upgrade]..."

## Type 2: Webinar Funnel

### Architecture
```
[Ad/Traffic] -> Registration Page -> Confirmation Page
                                         |
                         [Reminder Sequence: 5 emails + 2 SMS]
                                         |
                                  Live/Replay Page -> Application Page
                                         |
                              [Post-Event Sequence: 5 emails]
```

### Page 1: Registration Page

**Purpose:** Sell the EVENT, not the offer. The registration page must make the webinar itself feel valuable enough to block calendar time.

**Above the Fold:**
- Pre-headline: "Free Live Training" or "Free Masterclass"
- Headline: "How to [Specific Result] in [Timeframe] (Without [Main Objection])"
- Sub-headline: "Join [Creator Name] for a live [duration] training where you'll discover the exact [Mechanism Name] used by [number] [ICP description]"
- Date/Time: Prominent, with timezone
- Form: Name + Email + Phone (3 fields)
- CTA Button: "Save My Seat" or "Register Free"

**Below the Fold:**
- "In this training, you'll discover:" (3-4 bullet points teaching outcomes, NOT offer features)
  - "The [framework] for [specific outcome] that [proof point]"
  - "Why [common mistake] is costing you [quantified impact] every month"
  - "The exact [number]-step process to [quick win result]"
  - "PLUS: A special opportunity for those who want [deeper transformation]"
- About the host: Credibility paragraph + headshot
- Social proof: "[X] people registered" counter or testimonials from past attendees
- FAQ: "Is this really free?" / "Will there be a replay?" / "Who is this for?"
- Second CTA

**Reminder Email Sequence:**

| # | Timing | Subject | Key Element |
|---|--------|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Immediate | "You're registered! (save this)" | Calendar link + what to expect |
| 2 | 24h before | "Tomorrow's training: here's a sneak peek" | Content teaser + value preview |
| 3 | 6h before | "[Name] achieved [result] after attending" | Social proof + reminder |
| 4 | 1h before | "We start in 60 minutes" | Direct link |
| 5 | 15 min before | "We're starting NOW -- join here" | Link only, one line |

**SMS Reminders:**
- 3h before: "Hey [Name], don't forget -- [Creator]'s training starts at [time]. Here's your link: [URL]"
- 5 min before: "Starting in 5 min! Join now: [URL]"

### Page 2: Confirmation Page

**Purpose:** Increase show-up rate by building anticipation and commitment.

- Confirmation message with date/time
- "Add to Calendar" buttons (Google, Apple, Outlook)
- Pre-webinar video (60-90 seconds): "What to expect and how to get the most out of it"
- Engagement prompt: "Come prepared with your #1 question about [topic]"
- Share buttons: "Know someone who needs this? Send them this link"
- Post-booking social proof: "[X] people are already registered"

### Page 3: Live/Replay Page

**Purpose:** Host the webinar content and transition to the offer.

**Live Format:**
- Full-screen video player with chat widget
- Engagement prompts at key moments ("Drop a [emoji] if you've experienced this")
- CTA button appears at the pitch section (not before)
- Replay toggle after live ends

**Replay Format:**
- "REPLAY: Watch Before [Deadline]" urgency banner
- Video player with progress bar
- CTA button visible throughout (for replay viewers who are ready)
- Below video: Offer summary, testimonials, FAQ
- Replay expiration countdown (if applicable)

### Page 4: Application Page

**Purpose:** Capture qualified applications from webinar viewers.

Same structure as VSL Funnel Application page, but with webinar-specific framing:
- "Based on what you learned in today's training..."
- Reference specific webinar content in questions
- Include a "What was your biggest takeaway from the training?" question (builds commitment)

**Post-Webinar Email Sequence:**

| # | Timing | Subject | Purpose |
|---|--------|---------|---------|
| 1 | Immediate | "Replay + key takeaways from today" | Deliver replay link + summary |
| 2 | 24h | "The story I didn't have time to share" | Additional case study |
| 3 | 48h | "The #1 question people asked me after the training" | Objection handling |
| 4 | 72h | "[Deadline] is approaching -- here's my honest take" | Urgency + authenticity |
| 5 | Deadline | "Final hours: [Name], this is my last email about this" | Last call |
| 6 | Deadline +1 | "It's closed. Here's what's next for you." | Graceful close or downsell |

## Type 3: Application Funnel

### Architecture
```
[Ad/Traffic] -> Long-Form Sales Page -> Application Form -> Call Booking -> Confirmation + Nurture
```

### Page 1: Long-Form Sales Page

**Purpose:** Pre-sell the offer so completely that the application form feels like a natural next step. The reader should WANT to apply by the time they reach the CTA.

**Above the Fold:**
- Pre-headline: "For [ICP Description] Only"
- Headline: "[Outcome]: The [Mechanism Name] for [ICP] Who Want [Result] Without [Objection]"
- Sub-headline: "Apply for a free strategy call to see if [Program Name] is right for you"
- CTA Button: "Apply Now"

**Section 1: Problem Agitation [Scroll Section]**
- "If you're like most [ICP], you've probably experienced..."
- 5-7 pain points written in first person (ICP's voice)
- Each pain point is specific and emotional, not generic
- End with: "Sound familiar? You're not alone. And more importantly -- it's not your fault."

**Section 2: The Mechanism [Scroll Section]**
- "There's a reason why [common approach] isn't working..."
- Explain why alternatives fail (2-3 paragraphs)
- Introduce the mechanism: "What works instead is [Mechanism Name]"
- 3-5 pillars explained at high level
- Transition: "And we've built an entire program around this approach..."

**Section 3: The Offer [Scroll Section]**
- "Introducing [Program Name]"
- Program overview with outcome framing
- What is included (5-8 components, each with outcome description)
- Who this is for (qualification criteria framed positively)
- Who this is NOT for (disqualification criteria)

**Section 4: Social Proof [Scroll Section]**
- 5-8 testimonials/case studies
- Mix of video and text testimonials
- Each addresses a different objection or represents a different segment
- Results carousel or grid format

**Section 5: About [Creator] [Scroll Section]**
- Creator story (condensed -- 3-4 paragraphs)
- Credentials and proof of authority
- Personal photo
- "Why I built this program" mission statement

**Section 6: FAQ [Scroll Section]**
- 8-12 questions covering all major objections
- "How is this different from [alternative]?"
- "What if I'm too busy?"
- "What if it doesn't work for my specific situation?"
- "What's the investment?"
- "Is there a guarantee?"
- Each answer ends with a micro-CTA: "Apply now to discuss this on your strategy call"

**Section 7: Final CTA [Scroll Section]**
- Restate the core transformation
- "If you're ready to [transformation], apply now. If you're not sure, apply anyway -- the call is free and we'll help you figure out if this is the right move."
- Large CTA button
- P.S. with urgency or guarantee restatement

### Page 2: Application Form

**Purpose:** Qualify the applicant AND build their commitment to the process.

5-10 questions, progressing from low to high commitment:

1. "What's your name?" (easy start)
2. "What's your email?" (contact)
3. "What's your phone number?" (higher commitment)
4. "What best describes your current situation?" (multiple choice, ICP segments)
5. "What's your current [relevant metric]?" (qualification)
6. "What's your #1 challenge with [topic]?" (open-ended, reveals pain)
7. "What have you tried before to solve this?" (builds awareness of past failures)
8. "What would it mean to you to solve this in the next [timeframe]?" (desire)
9. "On a scale of 1-10, how committed are you to solving this now?" (commitment check)
10. "Is there anything else we should know before your call?" (open-ended)

**After Submission:** Redirect to calendar booking page.

### Pages 3-4: Call Booking + Confirmation

Call booking page with:
- Calendar embed (Calendly, Chili Piper, etc.)
- "What to expect on your call" explanation
- "Average call length: [X] minutes"
- Preparation instructions

Confirmation page with:
- Welcome video from closer or creator
- Calendar links
- Pre-call questionnaire or prep material
- Social proof from people who booked calls

**Post-Booking Nurture (Show-Up Sequence):**

| # | Timing | Channel | Message |
|---|--------|---------|---------|
| 1 | Immediate | Email + SMS | Confirmation + what to expect + calendar link |
| 2 | 24h before | Email | Value preview: "Here's what we'll cover on your call" |
| 3 | 6h before | Email | Case study: "Here's what [Name] achieved after their call" |
| 4 | 2-3h before | SMS | Personal text from closer: "Looking forward to our call! -[Name]" |
| 5 | 1h before | Email | Final reminder with call link |
| 6 | 10 min after no-show | SMS + Email | "We missed you! Here's a link to rebook: [URL]" |

## Type 4: Low-Ticket Funnel

### Architecture
```
[Ad/Traffic] -> Sales Page -> Checkout -> Upsell 1 -> (Downsell) -> Thank You
```

### Page 1: Sales Page

**Purpose:** Sell a $7-$497 digital product. Must convert cold traffic with no prior relationship. Copy does the heavy lifting.

**Above the Fold:**
- Pre-headline: "Attention [ICP Description]:"
- Headline: "Get [Specific Deliverable] That [Outcome] -- For Just $[Price]"
- Sub-headline: "[Number] [ICP] have already used this to [result]. Now it's your turn."
- Product mockup image (digital product visualization)
- CTA Button: "Get Instant Access -- $[Price]" or "Yes, I Want This!"
- Guarantee badge: "100% Money-Back Guarantee"

**Below the Fold:**

*Problem Section:*
- "You know the feeling..." (2-3 paragraphs of pain agitation in ICP language)
- Bullet list of specific frustrations

*Solution Introduction:*
- "What if there was a [simpler/faster/better] way?"
- Introduce the product by name
- 3-5 key outcomes (what they will have or be able to do)

*What's Inside:*
- Detailed breakdown of what they get
- Each module/section/component listed with specific outcome
- Screenshots or previews of the actual content

*Bonuses:*
- 2-3 bonuses with individual value anchors
- Each bonus addresses a specific objection: "But what about [X]?" "That's why we added [Bonus]"

*Price + Guarantee:*
- Value stack total: $[X,XXX]
- "Your investment today: $[price]"
- 30-day unconditional money-back guarantee
- "Try the entire [product] for 30 days. If you don't [specific outcome], email us and we'll refund every penny. No questions, no hassle."

*Testimonials:*
- 5-8 testimonials focused on quick wins and specific results
- Prioritize testimonials that mention the price as worth it

*FAQ:*
- 5-7 common questions
- "What format is this in?" / "How quickly will I see results?" / "What if I'm a complete beginner?"

*Final CTA:*
- "You have two choices: keep [pain] or invest $[price] in [transformation] right now."
- CTA button
- P.S.: Restate guarantee + highlight scarcity (if genuine)

### Page 2: Checkout

- Clean checkout with order summary
- Product image + name + price
- Order bump: "ADD: [Related small item] -- just $[X] (normally $[XX])" with checkbox
- Payment fields (minimize friction)
- Security badges
- Testimonial sidebar
- Guarantee reminder below payment button

### Page 3: Upsell (One-Time Offer)

**Purpose:** Increase AOV with a complementary product. Must be immediately relevant to what they just bought.

**Above the Fold:**
- "Wait! Your order is being processed..."
- Headline: "Add [Upsell Product] to Your Order and [Additional Outcome]"
- Video (60-120 seconds): Quick explanation of why this complements their purchase
- Price: "Just $[X] (normally $[XX]) -- this offer is only available right now"
- CTA: "Yes! Add This To My Order" (big, green)
- Skip: "No thanks, I'll pass on this" (small, gray, below)

**Below the Fold:**
- 3-5 reasons why this complements what they bought
- 2-3 testimonials from people who used both products together
- FAQ specific to the upsell

### Page 3b: Downsell (If Upsell Declined)

- "I totally understand -- let me offer you something else..."
- Smaller version of the upsell or different complementary product
- Lower price point
- Same CTA structure

### Page 4: Thank You

- Purchase confirmation
- Access instructions (clear, numbered steps)
- "What to do first" recommendation (reduces overwhelm, increases engagement)
- Quick win: "Start with [specific section] -- most people see [result] within [timeframe]"
- Social share: "Know someone who needs this?"
- Community invite (if applicable)

## Type 5: Challenge Funnel

### Architecture
```
[Ad/Traffic] -> Registration Page -> Welcome/Day 0 Page
                     |
         [Daily Pages: Day 1-5 with email sequence]
                     |
              Pitch Page (Day 5 or 6) -> Application/Checkout
```

### Page 1: Registration Page

**Purpose:** Sell participation in a free 5-day challenge. Must convey enough value to commit time over 5 days.

**Above the Fold:**
- Pre-headline: "Free 5-Day Challenge"
- Headline: "The [Challenge Name]: [Outcome] in Just 5 Days"
- Sub-headline: "Join [number]+ [ICP] in this free, guided experience. By Day 5, you'll have [tangible deliverable] and a clear path to [bigger outcome]."
- Date: Challenge start date
- Form: Name + Email + Phone
- CTA: "Join the Challenge Free"

**Below the Fold:**
- Day-by-day preview:
  - Day 1: "[Topic] -- You'll learn [outcome] and complete [deliverable]"
  - Day 2: "[Topic] -- You'll discover [outcome] and build [deliverable]"
  - Day 3-5: Same format
- "By the end of this challenge, you'll have:" (3-4 tangible outcomes)
- Testimonials from past challenge participants
- About the host
- FAQ: "How much time per day?" / "Is this really free?" / "What do I need to participate?"

### Pages 2-6: Daily Challenge Pages

Each daily page follows this structure:

- **Header:** "Day [X] of 5: [Day Title]"
- **Video:** 15-45 minute training video
- **Action item:** Clear homework assignment with downloadable worksheet
- **Community prompt:** "Share your Day [X] win in the group: [link]"
- **Engagement tracker:** "Check off today's task to track your progress"
- **Preview:** "Tomorrow you'll discover [teaser]..."

**Daily Email Sequence:**

| Day | Morning Email | Evening Email |
|-----|-------------|--------------|
| 0 | Welcome + what to expect + community link | "Get ready: here's what's coming tomorrow" |
| 1 | Day 1 training link + overview | "Did you complete Day 1? Here's why it matters" |
| 2 | Day 2 training link + Day 1 recap | "Amazing progress! Here's a win from [participant]" |
| 3 | Day 3 link (midpoint) + momentum check | "Halfway there! Here's what Day 3 changes" |
| 4 | Day 4 link + building to Day 5 | "Tomorrow is the big reveal -- don't miss it" |
| 5 | Day 5 link + offer teaser | "Here's the opportunity I mentioned..." |
| 6 | Offer recap + deadline | "Doors close [date] -- last chance" |

### Page 7: Pitch Page

**Purpose:** Present the paid offer as the natural next step after the challenge experience.

- "You just completed the [Challenge Name]. Here's your next step..."
- Recap of what they achieved in 5 days
- Bridge: "Now imagine what's possible with [full support/program]..."
- Full offer presentation (same structure as Application Funnel sales page but leveraging challenge context)
- Challenge-specific testimonials: "I did the challenge and then joined [program]..."
- CTA: Apply or purchase

## Type 6: Book-a-Call Funnel

### Architecture
```
[Ad/Traffic] -> VSL/Sales Page -> Booking Page -> Confirmation + Nurture Sequence
```

### Page 1: VSL or Sales Page

Hybrid page with embedded VSL and supporting copy:

**Above the Fold:**
- Headline focused on the call itself: "Book a Free [Call Type] to Discover How to [Outcome]"
- Embedded VSL (10-20 minutes, focused on building desire for the call)
- CTA: "Book Your Free Call"

**Below the Fold:**
- "On your call, you'll get:" (3-4 specific value promises for the call itself)
- "This call is for you if:" (qualification criteria)
- "This call is NOT for you if:" (disqualification)
- Case studies from people who booked calls and achieved results
- About the creator/closer who runs the calls
- FAQ
- Final CTA

### Page 2: Booking Page

- Calendar embed with available slots
- "What to expect on your [X]-minute call"
- Brief pre-call questionnaire (3-5 questions embedded or on separate page)
- Social proof: "Over [X] calls booked this month"

### Page 3: Confirmation + Nurture

Same show-up optimization structure as Application Funnel confirmation, with full email/SMS nurture sequence designed for 70%+ show-up rate.

## Type 7: Tripwire Funnel

### Architecture
```
[Ad/Traffic] -> Low-Price Offer Page -> One-Time Offer (OTO) -> Downsell -> Thank You
```

### Page 1: Low-Price Offer Page

**Purpose:** Self-liquidating offer that acquires customers at breakeven or small profit. The real revenue comes from upsells.

**Above the Fold:**
- Headline: "[Deliverable] for Just $[1-7]"
- Sub-headline: "Get the exact [template/guide/resource] that [number] [ICP] used to [result]"
- Product mockup
- CTA: "Get It Now -- $[Price]"

**Below the Fold:**
- What is included (keep brief -- it is a low-ticket impulse buy)
- 3-5 bullet points of key outcomes
- 3-5 testimonials (focused on value-for-price perception)
- "Normally $[higher price]" comparison
- 100% money-back guarantee
- CTA with security badges

### Pages 2-3: OTO + Downsell

Same structure as Low-Ticket Funnel upsell and downsell pages. The OTO should be the logical "next step" product that accelerates results from the tripwire.

Typical tripwire-to-upsell flow:
- Tripwire ($1-$7): Template, checklist, or resource
- OTO ($27-$97): Course, toolkit, or implementation guide
- Downsell ($17-$47): Simplified version of OTO or different complementary product

### Page 4: Thank You

Focus on immediate value delivery and first quick win. For tripwire buyers, the thank-you page experience determines whether they engage with the product and become candidates for the core offer later.

## Conversion Optimization Checklist (All Funnels)

- [ ] Every page passes the 3-second test
- [ ] CTA appears above the fold on every page
- [ ] Social proof placed after each major claim
- [ ] Guarantee appears after price reveal
- [ ] Mobile copy specified and tested
- [ ] Forms have minimum required fields
- [ ] Exit-intent trigger configured
- [ ] Retargeting pixels installed on all pages
- [ ] Page load time under 3 seconds
- [ ] Email sequences configured and tested
- [ ] SMS sequences configured (where applicable)
- [ ] Abandoned cart sequence active
- [ ] No-show sequence active
- [ ] All links tested
- [ ] Payment processing tested
- [ ] Offer congruence verified across all pages
- [ ] Brand voice consistent throughout
