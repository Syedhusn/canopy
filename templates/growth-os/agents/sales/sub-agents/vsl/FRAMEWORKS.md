# VSL Frameworks -- Complete Playbook

> Production blueprints for all 7 VSL types. Each framework contains the complete structural
> blueprint, section-by-section guidance, timing allocations, and psychological principles
> that govern the script. Every framework enforces the 80/20 story-to-mechanism ratio.

---

## Universal VSL Principles

Before diving into type-specific frameworks, these principles govern every VSL regardless of type:

### The 80/20 Rule

Every VSL allocates approximately 80% of its runtime to storytelling (hook + story + embedded proof) and 20% to mechanism explanation and pitch. This ratio exists because:

1. **Story creates emotional investment** -- a viewer who is emotionally invested will watch the mechanism and pitch sections. A viewer who was lectured at will not.
2. **Story handles objections invisibly** -- when the creator shares "I tried X and it failed because Y," the viewer's own objection about X dissolves without feeling sold to.
3. **Story builds identity connection** -- the viewer needs to see themselves in the story before they can believe the solution applies to them.

### The Steven Trister 13-Step Foundation

All VSL types are built on a modified version of the Trister 13-step structure:

1. Demographic callout
2. Viewer gain (promise)
3. Life before (relatable struggle)
4. Breaking point (emotional low)
5. Failed attempts (mirror ICP experience)
6. Discovery (what changed everything)
7. Building the new way (journey)
8. Results (specific, measurable)
9. Other people's results (proof stack)
10. Mechanism naming and explanation
11. Offer presentation (value stack)
12. Objection handling (belief shifts)
13. Call to action (single, clear)

Different VSL types emphasize different steps and allocate different time to each, but all 13 steps appear in some form in every script.

### Emotional Beat Mapping

Every VSL follows an emotional arc:

```
Curiosity (hook) --> Recognition (callout) --> Empathy (shared struggle)
--> Pain (breaking point) --> Hope (discovery) --> Excitement (results)
--> Understanding (mechanism) --> Desire (offer) --> Confidence (proof + guarantee)
--> Action (CTA)
```

The emotional arc must never plateau. Each section must raise the emotional stakes or shift the emotional register. Flat sections cause viewer drop-off.

## Type 1: Archetype-Based VSL

### Purpose
Creates a custom narrative for a specific ICP archetype. Most effective when the business serves 2-3 distinct segments that need different stories to connect.

### Specifications
- **Length:** 15-30 minutes (2,250-4,500 words at 150 wpm)
- **Traffic:** Warm (has some prior exposure to the brand)
- **Ticket:** Mid-to-High ($1,000-$25,000)
- **Best for:** Businesses with multiple ICP segments, identity-driven offers

### Structural Blueprint

**Section 1: Archetype Identification [0:00-1:30] (5%)**

Open by naming the archetype so the viewer self-selects:

"If you're what I call the [Archetype Name] -- someone who [defining characteristic], who has [specific experience], and who secretly feels like [emotional state] -- then this might be the most important video you watch this year."

Include 3-4 identifying markers that are specific enough to create a "that's me" reaction but broad enough to capture the full segment. The archetype should feel like a respected category, not a label.

**Section 2: Archetype-Specific Promise [1:30-3:00] (5%)**

Promise a specific transformation calibrated to this archetype's primary desire:

"In the next [X] minutes, I'm going to show you exactly how [Archetype Name] types are [achieving specific outcome] without [the thing they fear most about the process]."

The promise must be specific, time-bound, and address the archetype's dominant concern.

**Section 3: "I Was This Archetype Too" Story [3:00-12:00] (30%)**

The creator tells their own story, but framed through the archetype lens:

- Part A: Life as this archetype (the daily reality, specific frustrations, the inner monologue)
- Part B: The specific breaking point for this archetype type (not generic failure -- the failure that this type uniquely experiences)
- Part C: Failed attempts that this archetype typically makes (3-4 common approaches that do not work, with specific reasons why they fail for this type of person)
- Part D: The epiphany moment (what the creator realized that was specifically relevant to this archetype's limiting belief)

**Section 4: The Journey [12:00-18:00] (20%)**

How the creator built/discovered the solution, told through the archetype's values:

- What they tried that finally worked (and why it worked for this type)
- The early signs of success
- The compounding results
- Where they are now (the "after" picture that this archetype aspires to)

**Section 5: Proof Stack [18:00-22:00] (13%)**

3-5 case studies of other people who fit this archetype:

Each case study follows: "Meet [Name]. They were a classic [Archetype Name] -- [2 sentences of their before state]. Here's what happened when they [applied the mechanism]: [specific result with timeline]."

Prioritize proximity: the closer the case study matches the archetype, the more powerful the proof.

**Section 6: Mechanism [22:00-25:00] (10%)**

Name and explain the mechanism, calibrated to this archetype's learning style:

- Name the framework/system/methodology
- Explain WHY it works specifically for this archetype (address their dominant limiting belief)
- 3-5 pillars or steps at a high level (do not teach -- reveal)
- Differentiate from what this archetype has tried before

**Section 7: Pitch [25:00-28:00] (10%)**

Present the offer through the archetype's value system:

- Each offer component framed as solving a specific archetype problem
- Value stack with anchored pricing
- Bonuses selected specifically for this archetype's objections
- Guarantee framed around this archetype's specific fear

**Section 8: Objection Handling + CTA [28:00-30:00] (7%)**

Address the 3-5 objections most common to this archetype, then single CTA.

## Type 2: 15-Minute VSL

### Purpose
Designed for cold traffic and high-volume funnels where attention is limited. Gets to the point fast while still maintaining the story-driven approach. Ideal for paid ad traffic landing on a dedicated VSL page.

### Specifications
- **Length:** 7-15 minutes (1,050-2,250 words)
- **Traffic:** Cold (first exposure to the brand)
- **Ticket:** Low-to-Mid ($97-$2,000)
- **Best for:** Paid traffic funnels, volume plays, lower-ticket offers

### Structural Blueprint

**Section 1: Hook + Callout [0:00-0:45] (5%)**

Compressed opening that combines demographic callout and promise:

"If you're a [specific person] who's struggling with [specific problem], pay attention -- because in the next [X] minutes I'm going to show you the [number]-step process that [specific result] without [main objection]."

Cold traffic has a 5-second attention window. The first sentence must create both recognition and curiosity.

**Section 2: Credibility Seed [0:45-1:30] (5%)**

Quick authority establishment -- no long credentials list:

"My name is [PERSONALIZE], and over the past [time period] I've helped [number] [ICP description] achieve [specific result]. But I wasn't always in this position..."

Transition directly into the story.

**Section 3: Compressed Story [1:30-7:00] (37%)**

The full story arc compressed to essential beats:

- Part A: "I used to be..." -- 60-90 seconds on the before state. Hit the 2-3 most relatable pain points fast. Use vivid, specific details, not generalities.
- Part B: "The moment everything changed..." -- 30-60 seconds on the breaking point. One specific, emotionally charged moment.
- Part C: "What I discovered..." -- 60-90 seconds on the discovery. Focus on the single insight that shifted everything.
- Part D: "Here's what happened next..." -- 60-90 seconds on results. Specific numbers, timelines, before/after contrast.
- Part E: "And I'm not the only one..." -- 60 seconds embedding 2-3 quick proof snapshots within the story flow.

Key difference from longer VSLs: every beat gets ONE moment, not a developed scene. Specificity replaces length.

**Section 4: Mechanism [7:00-9:30] (17%)**

For cold traffic, the mechanism must be:
- Named clearly (gives it legitimacy and memorability)
- Explained in simple terms (why it works, in 2-3 sentences)
- Differentiated from alternatives (why other approaches fail, in 2-3 sentences)
- Broken into 3 components maximum (pillars, steps, phases -- keep it digestible)

"This is what I call the [Mechanism Name]. And it works because [core principle in one sentence]. Most people try to [common alternative], but that fails because [why]. The [Mechanism Name] is different because [differentiation]."

**Section 5: Pitch + Close [9:30-13:00] (23%)**

Compressed value stack and close:

- Offer overview: "Here's what you get..." (outcome-framed, 3-5 items maximum)
- Quick value anchoring: "If you were to [alternative], you'd pay $[X]"
- Price reveal: Direct for low-ticket. "Your investment today is $[price]."
- Guarantee: Unconditional for low-ticket, conditional for mid-ticket
- Bonus (1-2 maximum, each handles a specific objection)
- CTA: Clear, immediate, friction-free

**Section 6: Objection Handling [13:00-14:30] (10%)**

Quick FAQ-style objection handling -- 3-5 objections with 15-20 second responses each:

"You might be thinking, '[objection].' I get it. Here's the thing -- [reframe in 2 sentences]."

**Section 7: Final CTA [14:30-15:00] (3%)**

Restate the transformation + single action:

"If you're ready to [transformation], click the button below. [What happens when they click]. [What they get]. [Restate the guarantee]. The only question is whether you're going to keep [current pain] or take the first step toward [desired outcome]."

## Type 3: 30-Minute VSL

### Purpose
The balanced workhorse. Enough time to build real trust and emotional connection while maintaining momentum. Works for warm traffic that has some exposure but needs convincing.

### Specifications
- **Length:** 20-30 minutes (3,000-4,500 words)
- **Traffic:** Warm (knows the brand, consumed some content)
- **Ticket:** Mid ($997-$5,000)
- **Best for:** Warm traffic retarget, email list, social media audience

### Structural Blueprint

**Section 1: Recognition Hook [0:00-2:00] (7%)**

Open with recognition, not cold pitch. The viewer already knows the creator:

"Hey, it's [PERSONALIZE]. If you've been following my content about [topic], you've probably noticed that I keep coming back to one idea -- [core concept]. Today I want to go deeper than I ever have in a post or video, because what I'm about to share is the complete picture of how [transformation] actually works."

Acknowledge the existing relationship. Reward them for paying attention.

**Section 2: The Full Origin Story [2:00-14:00] (40%)**

With 30 minutes, the story can breathe:

- Part A: The Before [2:00-5:00] -- Paint a vivid picture of life before the transformation. Include specific moments, conversations, feelings. The viewer should see their own life reflected.
- Part B: The Descent [5:00-7:00] -- The compounding failures. Not just one bad moment but the pattern of things not working. Failed attempts that the ICP will recognize from their own experience.
- Part C: The Breaking Point [7:00-8:00] -- One crystallized moment of "I can't keep doing this." Make it specific and emotionally resonant.
- Part D: The Discovery [8:00-10:00] -- What changed. This is not the mechanism yet -- it is the insight, the conversation, the experience that opened the door.
- Part E: The Building [10:00-12:00] -- The messy middle. How the creator built the solution. Include early failures and iterations (this builds authenticity).
- Part F: The Results [12:00-14:00] -- Where the creator is now, told with specific metrics and emotional contrast to the "before." Include the lifestyle/emotional transformation, not just the financial one.

**Section 3: Social Proof Section [14:00-18:00] (13%)**

4-5 case studies told as mini-stories (60-90 seconds each):

Each follows: "[Name] came to us [before state]. Their biggest fear was [objection this addresses]. Within [timeframe], they [specific result]. Here's what [Name] said: '[testimonial quote].'"

Sequence the case studies to address different objections and represent different segments.

**Section 4: Mechanism Deep Dive [18:00-22:00] (13%)**

With warm traffic, the mechanism can be explained with more depth:

- Name and frame the mechanism
- Explain the core principle (the WHY behind it)
- 3-5 components with brief explanations (the WHAT, not the HOW)
- Why other approaches fail (contrast and differentiation)
- Why this works specifically for [ICP] (personalization)

"The [Mechanism Name] works because of a principle most people in [industry] overlook: [principle]. While everyone else is focused on [common approach], which fails because [reason], the [Mechanism Name] starts with [differentiated approach]."

**Section 5: The Offer [22:00-26:00] (13%)**

Full value stack presentation:

- The transformation promise (before/after for THEM)
- Each component presented as an outcome: "[Component] -- so you can [outcome] without [pain]"
- Value anchoring for each component
- Total value vs. investment
- Payment options

**Section 6: Bonuses + Guarantee [26:00-28:00] (7%)**

- 2-3 bonuses, each addressing a specific objection
- Present second-best bonus first, best bonus last (end on highest perceived value)
- Guarantee with specific terms matched to the offer document

**Section 7: Objection Handling + Close [28:00-30:00] (7%)**

5-7 objections handled in Q&A or story format, followed by the single CTA:

"Here's what to do next: [specific action]. When you [click/apply/call], here's what happens: [immediate next steps]. And remember, [guarantee restatement]."

## Type 4: 45-Minute VSL

### Purpose
The deep-trust builder for high-ticket offers. Designed for warm-to-hot traffic where the price point demands significant belief-building. This is where the 80/20 principle truly shines -- the extended story section creates a level of connection that short-form cannot achieve.

### Specifications
- **Length:** 35-45 minutes (5,250-6,750 words)
- **Traffic:** Warm-to-Hot (significant prior engagement)
- **Ticket:** High ($5,000-$50,000+)
- **Best for:** High-ticket coaching, consulting, done-for-you services

### Structural Blueprint

**Section 1: Intimate Opening [0:00-2:00] (4%)**

No hard pitch. Open like a conversation with someone you respect:

"I've been going back and forth about whether to make this video. Not because the content is controversial, but because what I'm about to share is deeply personal -- and it reveals the real reason I built [offer/company name]. If you're a [specific ICP description], I think you owe it to yourself to hear this."

Create vulnerability and exclusivity simultaneously.

**Section 2: Extended Origin Story [2:00-22:00] (44%)**

This is the core of the 45-minute VSL. The story gets cinematic treatment:

- Part A: The World Before [2:00-6:00] -- Full sensory detail of the old life. The specific morning routine, the conversations with spouse/partner, the feeling of waking up knowing something needs to change. Paint a movie scene.
- Part B: The First Attempts [6:00-10:00] -- Each failed attempt gets a mini-narrative. "I remember signing up for [thing]. The sales page promised [X]. What actually happened was [Y]. By month 3, I was more confused than when I started." 3-4 failed attempts that mirror what the ICP has tried.
- Part C: The Breaking Point [10:00-12:00] -- A specific, emotionally charged scene. Not "I was frustrated" but "I was sitting in my car in the parking lot at 11pm, staring at a spreadsheet that showed me I'd lost $47,000 in the last quarter, and I realized I had two options..."
- Part D: The Discovery [12:00-15:00] -- The person, book, event, or insight that cracked the code. Tell it as a story, not a lesson. "I almost didn't go to that event. I remember my wife saying..."
- Part E: The Messy Middle [15:00-18:00] -- Building the new approach. Early wins, skepticism, iterating. Include moments of doubt: "Honestly, I wasn't sure it was going to work until..."
- Part F: The Breakthrough [18:00-20:00] -- The moment results became undeniable. Specific metrics, specific timeline, specific emotional shift.
- Part G: The Mission [20:00-22:00] -- Why the creator is now teaching/offering this. "Once I saw these results, I couldn't keep it to myself. I started helping [first clients] and realized..."

**Section 3: Deep Proof Stack [22:00-28:00] (13%)**

5-7 case studies with significant depth:

Each gets 45-90 seconds with full narrative arc: before state (relatable), specific challenge (mirrors viewer), the journey (what the program experience was like), the result (quantified), and a direct quote.

Sequence case studies to represent:
1. Someone who was skeptical (Hopeless belief)
2. Someone who felt unqualified (Worthless belief)
3. Someone who was overwhelmed (Helpless belief)
4. Someone with the highest result (aspiration)
5. Someone most similar to the average viewer (proximity)

**Section 4: Mechanism [28:00-33:00] (11%)**

For high-ticket, the mechanism needs to create intellectual respect:

- Name the mechanism with gravitas
- Explain the underlying principle with depth (this audience is sophisticated)
- Show the 3-5 components and how they interconnect (system thinking)
- Address why simpler approaches are insufficient (acknowledge complexity)
- Position the mechanism as the culmination of significant experience and iteration

"The [Mechanism Name] isn't something I invented overnight. It's the result of [X years] of testing, [Y] iterations, and working with [Z] clients. It works because it addresses the three core bottlenecks that every [ICP] faces: [bottleneck 1], [bottleneck 2], and [bottleneck 3]."

**Section 5: The Offer [33:00-38:00] (11%)**

High-ticket offers are not "sold." They are presented as an invitation:

- Frame as an exclusive opportunity, not a product for sale
- Each component presented as a partnership element
- Value stack with anchored values (but do not make it feel transactional)
- Bonuses presented as "extras we added because we noticed clients needed..."
- Do NOT reveal exact price -- use price hints: "This is a significant investment -- we're talking about a five-figure commitment, not a $500 course"
- Direct prospects to apply/book a call to discuss fit and investment

**Section 6: Objection Handling [38:00-43:00] (11%)**

7-10 objections handled as belief shifts, not rebuttals:

"I know what you might be thinking: '[objection].' And honestly, if I were in your shoes, I'd probably wonder the same thing. Here's what I've learned from working with [X] people in your exact situation: [belief shift]."

Weave in additional proof with each objection response.

**Section 7: Close [43:00-45:00] (6%)**

Intimate, non-pushy close:

"Look, I'm not going to pressure you. If what I've shared resonates, and you're ready to stop [current pain] and start building toward [desired outcome], then I'd love to talk. Click below to apply for a conversation. It's not a sales call -- it's a fit call. We'll talk about where you are, where you want to be, and whether [program name] is the right vehicle to get you there. If it's not, I'll tell you. [Guarantee reference]. [CTA]."

## Type 5: Webinar-Style VSL

### Purpose
Presentation-format VSL that teaches real content before transitioning to the offer. Designed for mixed-temperature audiences and mid-to-high ticket offers. Functions as both education and sales.

### Specifications
- **Length:** 45-90 minutes (6,750-13,500 words)
- **Traffic:** Mixed (cold through warm)
- **Ticket:** Mid-to-High ($997-$25,000+)
- **Best for:** Education-first brands, webinar funnels, challenge culmination events

### Structural Blueprint

**Section 1: Webinar Opening [0:00-5:00] (6%)**

- Welcome and housekeeping (1 min)
- "Here's what we'll cover today" -- 3 teaching points + 1 offer reveal (1 min)
- Brief credibility: "Who am I and why should you listen?" (1 min)
- Social proof drop: "We've helped [X] people [result]" (30 sec)
- Set expectation: "Stay until the end because I have something special for you" (30 sec)
- Engagement prompt: "Drop a [X] in the chat if you've ever [relatable experience]" (1 min)

**Section 2: Teaching Section 1 [5:00-20:00] (17%)**

Teach a genuine principle that provides real value:

- Name the concept/framework
- Explain why most people get this wrong
- Teach the correct approach with actionable steps
- Show a quick case study of someone who applied this
- Transition: "Now that you understand [concept 1], let me show you [concept 2], which is where most people get stuck..."

The teaching must be real. If someone left after this section without buying, they should have learned something useful. This builds trust and reciprocity.

**Section 3: Teaching Section 2 [20:00-35:00] (17%)**

Second teaching block, building on the first:

- More advanced concept that builds on section 1
- Include the creator's personal experience with this concept
- Address common mistakes (which mirror ICP's current behavior)
- Case study showing this concept in action
- Transition to section 3

**Section 4: Teaching Section 3 [35:00-50:00] (17%)**

Third teaching block that naturally leads to the gap:

- This section should reveal the full picture of what success requires
- The viewer should realize: "I understand the framework, but implementing it on my own would be difficult/slow/risky"
- The teaching creates informed desire: they want the outcome, understand the approach, but see the value in guided implementation
- This is not manipulation -- it is honest education about the complexity of implementation

**Section 5: Story Bridge [50:00-55:00] (6%)**

Transition from teaching to offer with the creator's personal story:

"Now let me tell you why I'm sharing this with you today and not just selling it as a course..."

Quick version of the origin story that explains why the creator packaged this as an offer.

**Section 6: The Offer [55:00-70:00] (17%)**

Full offer presentation with value stack:

- "Here's what I built to help you implement everything I just taught..."
- Each component maps to a teaching section: "Remember in Section 1 when I showed you [concept]? [Component] is how you implement that with support"
- Value stack with anchored pricing
- Bonuses (2-4, each handling a specific objection)
- Investment reveal (direct for mid-ticket, hint for high-ticket)
- Guarantee
- Payment options

**Section 7: Q&A / Objection Handling [70:00-85:00] (17%)**

Extended objection handling in Q&A format:

"I know you probably have questions. Let me address the most common ones..."

7-10 objections with detailed responses. Each response includes a proof point or case study.

**Section 8: Final Close [85:00-90:00] (3%)**

"Here's the thing -- you now know the framework. You could try to implement it on your own. Some of you will, and I genuinely hope it works. But if you want the guided path, the accountability, the community, and the [specific support], [CTA]."

## Type 6: Story-Driven VSL

### Purpose
The most narrative-heavy format. Nearly the entire VSL is a story, with the mechanism and pitch emerging naturally from the narrative. Best for brands built on personal storytelling and emotional connection.

### Specifications
- **Length:** 15-30 minutes (2,250-4,500 words)
- **Traffic:** Warm (audience with emotional connection to the brand)
- **Ticket:** Mid ($997-$5,000)
- **Best for:** Personal brands, narrative-first creators, transformation stories

### Structural Blueprint

**Section 1: Cold Open [0:00-1:00] (4%)**

Start mid-story for immediate immersion:

"I was sitting in [specific location], staring at [specific detail], when [specific person] said something that changed the entire trajectory of my business. But to understand why that moment mattered, I need to take you back to [time period]..."

No demographic callout. No promise. Pure narrative hook. The cold open creates immediate curiosity.

**Section 2: Act One -- The World Before [1:00-8:00] (28%)**

Full cinematic treatment of life before the transformation:

This is not a summary. This is a movie. Include:
- Specific scenes (not "I was struggling" but "I was sitting at my kitchen table at 2am, surrounded by Post-it notes, watching my third YouTube video on [topic], and I still couldn't figure out [problem]")
- Dialogue ("My [person] looked at me and said, '[specific quote]'")
- Internal monologue ("And the worst part was, deep down I knew [fear/belief]")
- Sensory details (sight, sound, feeling)
- 2-3 failed attempts woven into the narrative naturally

**Section 3: Act Two -- The Turning Point [8:00-14:00] (24%)**

The discovery and early journey:

- The specific moment/person/event that created the shift
- The first tentative steps (vulnerability, doubt, small wins)
- The growing realization that this approach was different
- The first significant result (told with surprise and emotion)
- The decision to go all-in

**Section 4: Act Three -- The New World [14:00-20:00] (24%)**

Results, proof, and mission -- still told as narrative:

- Where the creator is now (specific, vivid contrast with Act One)
- Other people's stories woven in: "When I started sharing this with [first client name]..."
- Each case study is a mini-story within the larger narrative
- The mechanism is revealed organically: "That's when I realized that what made this work was [mechanism]"
- The mission: why the creator turned this into a program/offer

**Section 5: The Natural Transition [20:00-22:00] (8%)**

The offer emerges from the story:

"So when people ask me, 'Can you teach me how to do this?' -- that's exactly why [offer name] exists. It's the complete [mechanism name] system, designed specifically for [ICP] who want [transformation] without [main objection]."

**Section 6: Offer + Close [22:00-28:00] (12%)**

Condensed offer presentation and close:

- Brief value stack (the story has already done the heavy lifting)
- Investment and guarantee
- 3-4 objections handled quickly (the story has pre-handled most)
- CTA that references the story: "Remember that moment I described at the beginning, when [reference the cold open]? That's the moment you're in right now. You have two paths..."

## Type 7: Case Study VSL

### Purpose
Results-first format that leads with proof rather than personal story. Best for offers where the creator's personal story is less relevant than client results, or when the ICP is highly skeptical and needs evidence before emotional engagement.

### Specifications
- **Length:** 10-20 minutes (1,500-3,000 words)
- **Traffic:** Cold-to-Warm (skeptical audiences)
- **Ticket:** Mid-to-High ($997-$25,000)
- **Best for:** Results-focused audiences, skeptical markets, B2B, agency/consulting offers

### Structural Blueprint

**Section 1: Results Hook [0:00-1:00] (5%)**

Lead with the most impressive result:

"In the last [time period], we've helped [number] [ICP description] achieve [specific result]. Today I'm going to show you exactly how three of them did it -- including one who went from [before state] to [after state] in just [timeframe]. And at the end, I'll show you the system behind these results."

**Section 2: Case Study 1 -- The Skeptic [1:00-5:00] (20%)**

Full case study of someone who was deeply skeptical:

- Their before state (detailed, relatable)
- Why they were skeptical (mirrors viewer's skepticism)
- What convinced them to try (reduces viewer's barrier)
- The process (what the experience was actually like)
- The result (specific, quantified, timeline)
- Their own words (testimonial quote)
- Specific objection this case study dismantles

**Section 3: Case Study 2 -- The Underdog [5:00-9:00] (20%)**

Full case study of someone who started from a disadvantaged position:

- Their "unique" challenge (that the viewer might share)
- Why they thought it would not work for them (mirrors "my situation is different")
- How the mechanism adapted to their situation
- The result (proving it works even for "underdogs")
- Their own words
- Specific objection this dismantles (usually the Hopeless belief)

**Section 4: Case Study 3 -- The High Achiever [9:00-13:00] (20%)**

Full case study of the best result:

- Their starting point (already somewhat successful)
- What they were still struggling with despite success
- How the mechanism created breakthrough to the next level
- The result (the aspirational proof)
- Their own words
- Specific objection this dismantles (usually the "I can do it myself" belief)

**Section 5: The Pattern [13:00-15:00] (10%)**

Extract the mechanism from the case studies:

"Now, here's what's interesting about [Name 1], [Name 2], and [Name 3]. They're completely different people with different backgrounds, different challenges, and different starting points. But they all achieved [result category] because of the same underlying system: [Mechanism Name]."

Explain the mechanism as the pattern behind the proof. The case studies make the mechanism credible before it is even named.

**Section 6: Offer [15:00-18:00] (15%)**

Present the offer as access to the system behind the case studies:

- "The [Mechanism Name] is the system behind every result you just saw"
- Brief value stack
- Investment (direct or hint based on ticket level)
- Guarantee
- "The question isn't whether the system works -- you just saw that it does. The question is whether you're ready to be the next case study."

**Section 7: Additional Proof + Close [18:00-20:00] (10%)**

Quick-fire proof stack (5-8 additional results in 15-20 seconds each) followed by single CTA:

"And it's not just [Name 1], [Name 2], and [Name 3]. Here's what [Name 4] achieved... and [Name 5]... and [Name 6]..."

Close with: "Click below to [CTA]. [Next steps]. [Guarantee restatement]."

## Cross-Type Production Notes

### Visual Direction Conventions

All VSL scripts use these visual direction markers:

- `**[VISUAL: description]**` -- What appears on screen
- `**[TONE: direction]**` -- Vocal delivery guidance
- `**[MUSIC: direction]**` -- Background music/audio guidance
- `**[PAUSE: X seconds]**` -- Deliberate silence for impact
- `**[CUT TO: description]**` -- Scene or perspective change
