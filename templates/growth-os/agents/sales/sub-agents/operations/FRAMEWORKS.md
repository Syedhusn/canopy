## Framework 1: Call Prep Briefs

### Purpose
Transform scattered prospect data into a 5-minute actionable intelligence brief that makes closers feel fully prepared for every call.

### Data Collection Protocol

#### Source Priority (Signal Quality Ranking)

| Priority | Source | Signal Quality | What to Extract |
|----------|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Application/intake form | Highest -- self-reported, intentional | Stated challenges, goals, revenue, timeline, past investments |
| 2 | DM/email conversation history | Very high -- natural communication | Communication style, specific concerns, urgency signals |
| 3 | Webinar/event attendance | High -- behavioral | Content preferences, engagement level, questions asked |
| 4 | Social media profile + content | Medium-high -- public identity | Business stage, values, positioning, audience size |
| 5 | Email engagement history | Medium -- passive behavior | Content preferences, open/click patterns |
| 6 | Website behavior | Medium -- passive behavior | Pages visited, time on pricing page, resources downloaded |
| 7 | Referral context | Variable | Who referred them, why, relationship quality |

#### Intelligence Extraction Matrix

For each data point collected, assess across three dimensions:

| Dimension | Question | Impact on Brief |
|-----------|----------|----------------|
| Qualification | Does this confirm they match our ICP? | Fit score, segment mapping |
| Personalization | Can the closer use this to build rapport? | Rapport hooks, conversation starters |
| Prediction | Does this hint at their likely objections? | Objection preparation, proof selection |

### ICP Segment Mapping Protocol

Step-by-step process for mapping a prospect to an ICP segment:

1. **List all documented ICP segments** from the ICP reference document
2. **Score the prospect** against each segment (0-10) on these criteria:
   - Demographic match (business type, revenue, team size, stage)
   - Psychographic match (stated goals, language patterns, values)
   - Behavioral match (content consumed, engagement patterns)
3. **Identify the best-fit segment** (highest score)
4. **Pull segment-specific intelligence:**
   - Primary pain points for this segment
   - Dominant limiting belief pattern (Worthless/Helpless/Hopeless)
   - Common objections for this segment
   - Language patterns and VOC phrases
   - Best-performing proof points for this segment
5. **Cross-reference** segment expectations with prospect's actual data
6. **Flag mismatches** (prospect data contradicts segment expectations)

### Awareness Level Assessment Framework

| Level | Behavioral Indicators | Call Strategy |
|-------|----------------------|---------------|
| Unaware | Cold lead, no prior engagement, came through referral with no context | Heavy discovery, educate on the problem first, do not pitch unless awareness shifts during the call |
| Problem-Aware | Engaged with problem-focused content, asked questions about challenges | Validate their pain, introduce the solution category, then the mechanism |
| Solution-Aware | Consumed solution-focused content, attended webinar, compared options | Focus on differentiation: why YOUR mechanism, why YOUR approach |
| Product-Aware | Watched full VSL, visited sales page, read case studies, applied | Minimal education needed, focus on fit and close |
| Most-Aware | Returning lead, client referral, previous client | Customize and streamline, address specific lingering concerns, close efficiently |

### Objection Prediction Model

**Step 1: Segment-based prediction**
Pull the top 3 objections documented for their ICP segment.

**Step 2: Data-based prediction**
Analyze prospect-specific signals:

| Signal | Predicted Objection |
|--------|-------------------|
| Low revenue / early stage | Price sensitivity |
| Mentioned past failures | Trust / "tried before" resistance |
| No mentioned past investments | First-time buyer anxiety |
| Complex decision structure (partner, boss) | Authority / need to consult |
| High-achiever profile | DIY / "I can figure it out" |
| Long time in pipeline | Timing / "not right now" |
| Questions about specific results | Proof / "how do I know it works" |

**Step 3: Engagement-based prediction**
What they did NOT do reveals concerns:

| Non-Action | Predicted Concern |
|-----------|------------------|
| Did not watch full VSL | Not fully convinced of mechanism |
| Skipped pricing page | Price anxiety |
| Did not complete application | Commitment hesitancy |
| No social engagement | Trust deficit |
| Did not attend webinar | Low urgency |

**Step 4: For each predicted objection, prepare:**
1. The objection in their likely language
2. The underlying concern beneath the surface
3. 1-2 sentence reframe
4. Best matching case study or proof point
5. Follow-up question to confirm resolution

### Red Flag Assessment Framework

#### Yellow Flags (Proceed with Caution)

| Flag | What It Means | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| Vague application answers | Low commitment or unclear need | Discovery must dig deeper |
| No specific timeline | Low urgency | Build urgency through cost-of-inaction |
| Past refund mentioned | Possible chronic dissatisfaction | Stronger qualification, explicit expectations |
| Unrealistic expectations | May churn even if closed | Set realistic milestones before closing |
| Shopping multiple providers | Comparison mode, harder close | Differentiation-heavy presentation |

#### Red Flags (Consider Disqualification)

| Flag | What It Means | Recommendation |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| Cannot articulate problem | Not ready to buy | Defer to nurture sequence |
| Revenue/stage below minimums | Cannot afford or benefit | Graceful disqualification |
| History of buying and never implementing | Will not get results | Honest conversation about commitment |
| Adversarial tone | Difficult client risk | Sales director review before proceeding |
| Demands guarantees beyond offer | Unrealistic expectations | Clear expectation-setting or pass |

### Pricing Recommendation Framework

| Prospect Profile | Recommended Tier | Payment Approach | Notes |
|-----------------|-----------------|-----------------|-------|
| Revenue $10K+/mo, team, invested before | Premium/Flagship | Full pay, present confidently | They can afford it and know the value of investment |
| Revenue $5-10K/mo, solo, some history | Flagship | Payment plan available | Present full pay first, offer plan if resistance |
| Revenue under $5K/mo, no history | Entry or defer | Extended plan or defer | Honestly assess if they can benefit at this stage |
| Corporate/enterprise buyer | Custom | Annual/procurement-friendly | Different sales process, longer cycle |
| Returning lead, previously declined | Same or adjusted | Address what changed | Different pitch angle, acknowledge history |

## Framework 2: Custom Proposals

### Purpose
Transform the generic offer into a deeply personalized proposal that makes the prospect feel the solution was designed specifically for their situation.

### Input Requirements

Before building any proposal, these inputs are mandatory:

| Input | Source | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Offer document | `reference/offers.md` | Value stack, pricing, guarantee, mechanism |
| ICP segment match | Call prep brief or manual mapping | Segment-specific psychology |
| Discovery call notes | Closer's notes | Direct quotes, pain points, desired outcomes |
| Prospect data | Application + CRM | Business details, history |
| Call prep brief | Call prep agent output | Predicted objections, proof selection |
| Closer recommendation | Post-call notes | Recommended tier, approach |

### Proposal Development Process

#### Step 1: Situation Synthesis
1. Extract all prospect quotes from discovery notes (their exact words)
2. Identify the PRIMARY pain point (what hurts most RIGHT NOW)
3. Identify the ASPIRATIONAL outcome (what they want MOST)
4. Quantify the cost of inaction using their own numbers from discovery
5. Map their timeline to program milestones

#### Step 2: Offer Customization
1. Select which offer components to EMPHASIZE (based on their top needs)
2. DE-EMPHASIZE components less relevant to their situation
3. Reframe each component as solving THEIR specific problem
4. Select 2-3 bonuses that address THEIR specific objections
5. Choose the pricing tier and payment option matching their signals

#### Step 3: Proof Selection (Proximity Protocol)
Proof is selected by proximity -- the closer the case study matches the prospect, the more persuasive:

| Proximity Level | Match Criteria | Persuasion Power |
|----------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Highest | Same niche + same stage + same challenge | Maximum |
| High | Same niche + similar stage | Very strong |
| Medium | Same business model + similar challenge | Strong |
| Low | Same challenge + different context | Moderate |
| Lowest | Same general category | Weak |

Select 2-3 case studies at the highest proximity level available. If exact matches do not exist, acknowledge the parallel: "While [Client] is in [different niche], their situation mirrors yours in [specific ways]."

#### Step 4: Investment Presentation
Follow this exact sequence:

1. **Anchor to their cost of inaction:** "Based on what you shared, [their pain] is costing you approximately $[X] per [period]. Over the next 12 months, that's $[12X]."
2. **Value stack total:** "The total value of everything included is $[value stack total]."
3. **Investment reveal:** "Your investment is $[price]."
4. **ROI calculation:** "Based on your numbers, if [program] helps you [specific outcome], the ROI is [X]:1."
5. **Payment options:** "You can invest as a single payment of $[X] or [Y] monthly payments of $[Z]."

#### Step 5: Personalization Verification

Before finalizing, verify:
- [ ] Prospect's exact words appear at least 5 times in the proposal
- [ ] Every case study shares at least 2 attributes with the prospect
- [ ] The executive summary stands alone as a complete value proposition
- [ ] Investment section includes ROI framing using their specific numbers
- [ ] Guarantee is framed around their specific desired outcome
- [ ] Next steps include a specific action by a specific date
- [ ] Proposal expiration is set (7-14 days)

## Framework 3: CRM & Pipeline Management

### Purpose
Design the complete operational infrastructure for sales pipeline management, automation, lead scoring, and reporting.

### Pipeline Architecture Design Process

#### Step 1: Map the Actual Sales Motion

Before configuring any CRM, document the real sales process:

1. How do leads enter? (sources: ads, organic, referral, webinar, challenge, DM)
2. What happens first? (automated response, setter call, direct booking)
3. How are leads qualified? (application, setter call, automated scoring)
4. What is the sales event? (closer call, webinar pitch, sales page checkout)
5. What happens after the sale? (onboarding, welcome, fulfillment)
6. What happens after a loss? (nurture, re-engagement, archive)

Each real conversion event becomes a pipeline stage. No more, no less.

#### Step 2: Define Stage Architecture

Standard pipeline for high-ticket creator businesses:

| Stage | Definition | Required Fields | Max Time | Exit Action |
|-------|-----------|----------------|----------|-------------|
| New Lead | Form/DM/referral received | Name, email, source, timestamp | 1 hour | Assigned + contacted |
| Contacted | First outreach made | Contact method, timestamp | 24 hours | Response received |
| Qualified | Meets ICP criteria | Qualification score, segment, BANT | 48 hours | Call booked |
| Booked | Call scheduled | Date, time, closer, call type | Until call | Call completed |
| Showed | Attended call | Duration, disposition notes | 2 hours | Outcome logged |
| Pitched | Received offer + price | Tier presented, objections noted | 72 hours | Decision |
| Follow-Up | Needs time/more info | Follow-up date, blocking issue | 14 days | Re-engaged or lost |
| Closed Won | Purchased | Amount, tier, payment method | -- | Onboarding triggered |
| Closed Lost | Declined | Lost reason (required), notes | -- | Nurture triggered |

#### Step 3: Configure Automation Rules

**Speed-to-Lead Automations (Non-Negotiable):**

| Trigger | Action | Timing |
|---------|--------|--------|
| New lead enters pipeline | Confirmation email + SMS | Immediate (<1 min) |
| New lead enters pipeline | Notify assigned rep | Immediate |
| New lead enters pipeline | Create task: "Contact within 5 min" | Immediate |
| Lead uncontacted at 5 min | Escalate to manager | 5 min |
| Lead uncontacted at 1 hour | Reassign to available rep | 1 hour |

**Stage Transition Automations:**

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Moved to Qualified | Pre-call nurture email #1 |
| Call booked | Calendar invite + confirmation email + SMS |
| Call booked | Request call prep brief |
| 24h before call | Reminder email with value preview |
| 6h before call | Social proof reminder |
| 2-3h before call | Personal text from closer |
| 1h before call | Final reminder with link |
| No-show (10 min past) | No-show sequence triggered |
| Call complete -- interested | Move to Pitched + decision nurture |
| Call complete -- not ready | Move to Follow-Up + re-engagement |
| In Follow-Up > 14 days | Move to Lost + long-term nurture |
| Closed Won | Onboarding sequence + team notification |
| Closed Lost | Lost reason email survey + nurture |

**Engagement-Based Automations:**

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Opens 3+ emails in sequence | Lead score +10 |
| Clicks pricing page | Alert closer + score +20 |
| Watches 75%+ VSL | Tag "high-intent" + score +15 |
| Replies to email | Move to Contacted if New |
| Visits sales page 2+ times | Alert closer + score +10 |
| Downloads resource | Score +5 |
| Unsubscribes | Remove from sequences |

## Framework 4: Pipeline Analytics

### Purpose
Produce actionable analysis of sales performance with specific, prioritized optimization recommendations.

### Analysis Framework

#### Step 1: Funnel Math Audit

Map the actual conversion rates at every stage and compare to benchmarks:

```
[Impressions] -> [Clicks] -> [Page Views] -> [Opt-Ins] -> [Applications]
     CTR: X%      Load: X%     Conv: X%       Comp: X%

-> [Booked] -> [Showed] -> [Pitched] -> [Closed]
    Book: X%    Show: X%    Pitch: X%   Close: X%
```

For each stage, compare actual vs. benchmark and identify the widest gap.

#### Step 2: Bottleneck Identification

The stage with the largest gap between actual and benchmark is the primary bottleneck. Prioritize fixing the bottleneck before optimizing other stages.

| Bottleneck Stage | Likely Causes | Investigation Areas |
|-----------------|--------------|-------------------|
| Low opt-in rate | Headline/offer mismatch, wrong traffic, page load issues | A/B test headlines, check traffic quality, test page speed |
| Low application rate | VSL not converting, application too long, unclear next step | VSL retention analysis, form field audit, CTA test |
| Low book rate | Poor follow-up, no setter, scheduling friction | Speed-to-lead audit, setter script review, calendar UX |
| Low show rate | Weak confirmation sequence, low commitment, wrong timezone | Nurture sequence audit, confirmation page review, reminder timing |
| Low close rate | Script issues, poor qualification, wrong closer-lead match | Call recording review, qualification criteria audit, closer coaching |

#### Step 3: Source Performance Analysis

For each lead source, calculate:

| Metric | Formula |
|--------|---------|
| Cost per lead | Source spend / Leads generated |
| Cost per qualified lead | Source spend / Qualified leads |
| Cost per booked call | Source spend / Booked calls from source |
| Cost per acquisition | Source spend / Customers from source |
| Revenue per dollar spent | Revenue from source / Source spend |
| Time to close | Avg days from first touch to close, by source |

Rank sources by cost per acquisition, not cost per lead. A source with expensive leads that close at high rates is better than cheap leads that never convert.

#### Step 4: Closer Performance Analysis

| Metric | How to Measure | Coaching Trigger |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Close rate | Closes / Shows | Below team average by >5% |
| Average deal size | Revenue / Closes | Consistently lower tier |
| Show-up rate (their calls) | Shows / Booked for them | Below 70% (may be rapport issue) |
| Speed to follow-up | Time from call end to follow-up sent | Above 2 hours |
| Objection categories | Most common objections encountered | Concentration in one category |
| Talk ratio | Salesperson talk time / Total call time | Above 40% |

#### Step 5: Forecast Model

Pipeline-based forecast:

```
Forecast Revenue = Sum of (Opportunity Value x Stage Probability)
```

Stage probabilities (calibrated to actual data):

| Stage | Default Probability | Adjust Based On |
|-------|-------------------|-----------------|
| New Lead | 5% | Lead score |
| Qualified | 15% | Segment fit |
| Booked | 25% | Show-up history |
| Showed | 40% | Closer performance |
| Pitched | 60% | Objection profile |
| Follow-Up | 30% | Time in stage |
