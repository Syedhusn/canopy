> Canonical reference for all sales sub-agents. Loaded into context when any sales agent activates.

> and operational standards that govern every sales asset produced by the division.

## 1. Sales Psychology Foundations

### The Buyer's Decision Architecture

Every purchase decision is an emotional decision justified by logic after the fact. The sales division operates on this hierarchy:

1. **Identity** -- "Is this for someone like me?" (Demographic callout, social proof from similar people)
2. **Belief** -- "Do I believe this can work?" (Mechanism explanation, proof, authority)
3. **Desire** -- "Do I want this enough to act?" (Transformation painting, cost of inaction)
4. **Trust** -- "Can I trust these people?" (Guarantee, proximity of proof, brand consistency)
5. **Logic** -- "Does this make rational sense?" (Value equation, ROI calculation, comparison)
6. **Action** -- "What do I do next?" (Single clear CTA, friction removal, urgency)

Every sales asset must move the prospect through these stages in sequence. Skipping stages creates resistance. The most common failure is jumping to Logic (pricing, features) before Identity and Belief are established.

### The Limiting Belief Triad (Sales Application)

The ICP's dominant limiting belief pattern determines the entire sales approach:

**Worthless (Identity Crisis)**
- Core fear: "I'm not good enough to succeed at this"
- Manifests in sales as: Comparison to others, imposter syndrome, status anxiety, "people like me don't..."
- Sales response: Lead with identity transformation. Show proof from people who started in the same place. Emphasize belonging to a community of peers. Use aspirational identity language.
- VSL emphasis: Story section heavy on "I was just like you" + community proof
- Script emphasis: Discovery questions about identity and self-image
- DM emphasis: Peer-level conversation, not guru-to-student dynamic

**Helpless (Capability Gap)**
- Core fear: "I don't know how to do this"
- Manifests in sales as: Overwhelm, analysis paralysis, information overload, "it's too complicated"
- Sales response: Lead with process simplification. Show the step-by-step path. Emphasize that the mechanism removes complexity. Demonstrate how others with zero experience succeeded.
- VSL emphasis: Mechanism section heavy on "simple 3-step process" + remove-the-guesswork language
- Script emphasis: Discovery questions about past attempts and what felt overwhelming
- DM emphasis: Share quick wins and simple frameworks as value

**Hopeless (Possibility Doubt)**
- Core fear: "This won't work for me specifically"
- Manifests in sales as: Skepticism, past failure references, uniqueness bias, "my situation is different"
- Sales response: Lead with safety and risk reversal. Show case studies from people with the exact same "unique" situation. Emphasize the guarantee. Address past failures directly and explain why previous approaches failed (wrong vehicle, not wrong destination).
- VSL emphasis: Case study section heavy on diversity of proof + strong guarantee presentation
- Script emphasis: Discovery questions about past failures and what specifically went wrong
- DM emphasis: Lead with relevant case studies, ask about their specific situation

### Awareness Spectrum (Sales Application)

| Level | State | Sales Strategy | Asset Focus |
|-------|-------|---------------|-------------|
| 1 - Unaware | Do not know they have a problem | Do NOT sell. Educate. Move to nurture division. | Content marketing, not sales assets |
| 2 - Problem-Aware | Know the pain, not the solution category | Agitate the pain, introduce the solution category, then the mechanism | Long-form VSL, educational funnel |
| 3 - Solution-Aware | Know solutions exist, evaluating options | Differentiate the mechanism, prove superiority over alternatives | Comparison-focused VSL, application funnel |
| 4 - Product-Aware | Know your product, have not bought | Remove remaining objections, strengthen guarantee, create urgency | Retarget funnel, closer script, DM closing sequence |
| 5 - Most-Aware | Past buyers or highly engaged | New offer, loyalty pricing, ascension path | Upgrade script, referral sequence, upsell funnel |

**Rule:** Never deploy a high-pressure sales asset on a low-awareness prospect. The awareness level determines the sales temperature, not the salesperson's eagerness.

## 2. Conversion Principles

### The Value Equation

Every offer's perceived value follows this formula (Hormozi/Sultanic):

```
Perceived Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)
```

**Sales application:**
- **Maximize Dream Outcome:** Paint the specific transformation vividly. Use the prospect's own words for their desired state.
- **Maximize Perceived Likelihood:** Stack proof -- case studies, testimonials, mechanism logic, credentials, guarantees.
- **Minimize Time Delay:** Show the fastest path to first results. Emphasize quick wins in the first 7-30 days.
- **Minimize Effort & Sacrifice:** Position the mechanism as the simplification layer. "We remove the guesswork so you only do what moves the needle."

Every sales asset must consciously optimize all four variables. When conversion drops, diagnose which variable is weakest.

### The 3-Second Test

Every funnel page, VSL opening, and DM first message must pass the 3-second test:

1. **Who is this for?** (Demographic qualifier visible immediately)
2. **What will I get?** (Outcome or transformation stated)
3. **What do I do?** (CTA or next step clear)

If a prospect cannot answer all three within 3 seconds of seeing the asset, the asset fails.

### Social Proof Placement Protocol

Social proof is not decoration. It is a conversion tool with specific placement rules:

| Placement | Purpose | Proof Type |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| After headline | Validate the core claim | Quantified result or authority badge |
| After problem agitation | "Others felt this too" | Testimonial expressing the same pain |
| After mechanism introduction | "It actually works" | Case study with before/after |
| After price reveal | "It's worth it" | ROI testimonial or comparison |
| After guarantee | "You really can't lose" | Refund/guarantee satisfaction story |
| Above final CTA | "Others took action and won" | Recency proof ("Just last week, [Name]...") |

### Urgency Framework (Ethical Only)

The Sales division uses only legitimate urgency. Fabricated scarcity is a quality gate failure.

**Legitimate urgency sources:**
- Cohort-based programs with real start dates
- Limited enrollment due to actual capacity constraints (coaching calls, community size)
- Price increases with genuine business justification
- Seasonal relevance (launch timing, market windows)
- The prospect's own timeline and cost of inaction (always the strongest)

**Prohibited urgency tactics:**
- Fake countdown timers that reset
- "Only 3 spots left" when there is no actual limit
- "Price goes up tonight" when it does not
- "I can only hold this price for 24 hours" without real justification

## 3. Objection Handling Methodology

### The Belief Shift Framework

Objections are not obstacles to overcome. They are windows into unresolved beliefs. Every objection maps to one of three belief categories:

| Objection Surface | Belief Category | Real Concern | Response Strategy |
|-------------------|----------------|-------------|-------------------|
| "Too expensive" | Value/Identity | "I don't believe the outcome is worth the price" OR "I don't believe I deserve this investment" | Cost of inaction calculation + identity shift |
| "I need to think about it" | Trust/Safety | "I have an unaddressed concern I haven't voiced" | Identify the real concern: "Totally fair. When you say think about it, is it more about [X] or [Y]?" |
| "I've tried before and failed" | Possibility/Hope | "I believe I'm uniquely unable to succeed" | Reframe: previous approaches failed, not them. Different vehicle, not different destination. Proof from similar failures who succeeded. |
| "Not the right time" | Priority/Pain | "The pain isn't acute enough yet" OR "I don't trust the timeline" | Calculate the cost of waiting 6-12 months. Show that time does not improve without intervention. |
| "I need to talk to my partner" | Authority/Fear | Often genuine, sometimes a polite no | If genuine: equip them to present the case. Offer a joint call. If avoidance: identify the real concern underneath. |
| "Can I do it myself?" | Self-reliance/Value | "I'm not convinced your approach adds enough value over DIY" | Time cost + opportunity cost + error cost of DIY. "You can absolutely figure it out alone. The question is how long and at what cost." |
| "How is this different?" | Differentiation | "I don't understand why your approach is better" | Mechanism explanation. Why alternatives fail. Unique process or framework differentiation. |

### Response Structure (LAER)

1. **Listen** -- Let the objection land fully. Do not interrupt. Take notes.
2. **Acknowledge** -- Validate without agreeing or conceding: "That's a completely fair concern."
3. **Explore** -- Find what is underneath: "Help me understand -- when you say [X], is it more about [A] or [B]?"
4. **Respond** -- Deploy the appropriate tool:
   - **Story:** "One of our clients, [Name], had the exact same concern. Here's what happened..."
   - **Data:** "Our average client sees [specific result] within [timeframe]."
   - **Reframe:** "The question isn't whether you can afford it -- it's what another 12 months of [their stated pain] costs you."
   - **Isolate:** "If we could solve [that specific concern], is there anything else that would hold you back?"

### Price Objection Deep Dive

Price is the most common objection but rarely the real one. The response depends on the underlying belief:

| Price Variant | Real Issue | Response |
|--------------|-----------|----------|
| "I literally cannot afford it" | Budget constraint | Payment plan options. If truly cannot afford, graceful disqualification -- do not sell to people who will be harmed by the purchase. |
| "It's more than I expected" | Value perception gap | Value stack walkthrough. Compare to cost of alternatives and cost of inaction. |
| "I'm not sure it's worth it" | Belief gap | More proof needed. Case studies, ROI calculations, guarantee emphasis. |
| "I can find cheaper alternatives" | Differentiation gap | Mechanism differentiation. Explain why cheaper alternatives produce cheaper results. |
| "I need to check my finances" | Buying signal disguised as delay | "That makes sense. If the finances work, is this something you'd want to move forward with?" Then offer payment plan. |

## 4. Pricing Psychology

### Price Anchoring Sequence

Every price presentation follows this sequence:

1. **Problem cost anchor** -- "You told me this problem is costing you $[X] per month. Over the next 12 months, that's $[12X] in lost revenue/time/opportunity."
2. **Value stack anchor** -- "If you were to hire/buy/build each of these components separately, you'd be looking at $[total value]."
3. **Investment reveal** -- "The investment for [program name] is $[price]."
4. **Silence** -- Stop talking. Let them process. The first person to speak after the price is stated loses leverage.
5. **Payment options** -- Only if they need them: "We also have a payment plan of $[X]/month for [Y] months."

### The 3-Tiered Offer Presentation (Sultanic Model)

For businesses with tiered pricing:

| Tier | Range | Purpose | Sales Approach |
|------|-------|---------|---------------|
| Entry / Downsell | $497-$1,997 | Self-paced, lower-touch access | Presented as fallback if flagship is out of reach |
| Flagship | $3,000-$5,000 | Core transformation, standard delivery | Primary offer. Most marketing and sales efforts point here |
| Premium / Upsell | $10,000-$25,000+ | High-touch, done-with-you or done-for-you | Presented to qualified prospects who want accelerated results |

**Presentation order for maximum revenue:**
1. Present the Flagship first (this is the primary offer)
2. If price resistance, present Entry tier as an alternative path
3. If high engagement and buying signals, mention Premium: "Based on what you've shared, you might actually benefit more from our [Premium tier] because [specific reason tied to their situation]."

### Guarantee Psychology

| Guarantee Type | Best For | Psychology |
|---------------|---------|-----------|
| Unconditional (30-day money-back) | Low-ticket, digital products | Removes all risk. Works when product quality is high and refund rate is naturally low. |
| Conditional ("Do the work, get the result, or we refund") | Mid-to-high ticket coaching/courses | Transfers risk while maintaining accountability. Prospect must meet defined conditions. |
| Performance ("We'll work with you until you hit [metric]") | High-ticket, done-with-you | Strongest guarantee. Demonstrates supreme confidence. Only works if delivery model supports it. |
| Outcome-based ("If you don't [specific result] in [timeframe]...") | Premium offers | Most specific and most powerful. Must be genuinely achievable for qualified clients. |

**Rule:** The guarantee must appear in the offer document first. Sales assets reflect the exact guarantee -- never embellish, never weaken.

## 5. Belief Stack Framework

### Alen Sultanic's Belief Building Sequence

Belief is built through a specific accumulation pattern:

```
Authority + Evidence + Repetition = Belief
```

**Authority hierarchy (strongest to weakest):**
1. Published credentials (degrees, certifications, patents)
2. Verified results (audited financials, screenshots with context)
3. Social proof volume (number of clients, students, testimonials)
4. Media appearances (podcasts, publications, news features)
5. Published work (books, research, frameworks)
6. Associations (partnerships, endorsements, client logos)

**Evidence quality ladder:**
| Level | Evidence Type | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Weak | Self-reported claim | "I've helped hundreds of people" |
| Better | Specific claim | "I've helped 347 coaches in the last 18 months" |
| Good | Named testimonial | "Sarah went from $3K to $15K/month in 90 days" |
| Great | Documented case study | Full before/after with timeline, context, and specifics |
| Excellent | Third-party verified | Published in a credible outlet, audited, or independently confirmed |
| Supreme | Live demonstration | Prospect can verify the result themselves in real-time |

**Repetition principle:** A claim stated once is heard. Stated three times with different evidence is noticed. Stated seven times across different touchpoints is believed. The sales division coordinates proof placement across VSL, funnel, scripts, and DMs to ensure key claims receive sufficient repetition.

### Belief Stack Construction

For each offer, the sales division builds a belief stack -- the ordered sequence of beliefs the prospect must hold before they can purchase:

| # | Belief | Supporting Evidence | Where Addressed |
|---|--------|-------------------|-----------------|
| 1 | "This problem is costing me more than I realize" | Cost of inaction calculation | VSL story, script discovery |
| 2 | "Solutions exist for this problem" | Market context, alternatives discussion | VSL mechanism, funnel page |
| 3 | "This specific approach/mechanism works" | Mechanism logic + proof | VSL mechanism, script prescription |
| 4 | "It works for people like me specifically" | Proximity proof (similar clients) | VSL case studies, funnel testimonials |
| 5 | "This creator/company can deliver it" | Authority stack | VSL credibility, funnel proof section |
| 6 | "The investment is justified by the outcome" | Value equation, ROI calculation | Script presentation, proposal |
| 7 | "The risk is acceptable or eliminated" | Guarantee, payment options | Script close, funnel guarantee section |
| 8 | "Now is the right time to act" | Urgency (legitimate), cost of waiting | Script close, DM closing sequence |

Every sales asset must address beliefs in this order. An asset that attempts to close (Belief 8) before establishing the mechanism (Belief 3) will fail.

## 6. Sales Metrics & Pipeline Management

### Conversion Funnel Benchmarks

| Stage | Benchmark | Formula |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Ad CTR | 1.5-3.0% (cold), 3-6% (warm) | Clicks / Impressions |
| Landing page opt-in | 20-40% (cold), 40-60% (warm) | Opt-ins / Page visitors |
| VSL completion | 40-60% watch to CTA | CTA viewers / VSL starts |
| Application completion | 60-80% | Completed apps / App starts |
| Application-to-booked | 50-70% | Booked calls / Completed apps |
| Show-up rate | 70-85% | Shows / Booked calls |
| Close rate | 20-40% | Closes / Shows |
| Upsell take rate | 15-30% | Upsells / Core purchases |
| DM response rate | 15-25% (cold), 40-60% (warm) | Responses / DMs sent |
| Email reply rate | 2-5% (broadcast), 10-20% (personal) | Replies / Emails sent |

### Unit Economics Validation

Before any sales asset ships, the Sales Director validates:

```
Revenue per customer = Price x (1 + Upsell rate x Upsell price / Base price)
Cost per acquisition = Ad spend / Customers acquired
                     = Ad spend / (Impressions x CTR x Conv rate x Book rate x Show rate x Close rate)

LTV:CAC must be >= 3.0
```

Example validation:
- Offer price: $3,000
- Ad spend: $5,000/month
- Impressions: 100,000 at $50 CPM
- CTR: 2% = 2,000 clicks
- Page conversion: 30% = 600 opt-ins
- Application rate: 40% = 240 applications
- Book rate: 60% = 144 booked calls
- Show rate: 80% = 115 shows
- Close rate: 25% = 29 closes
- Revenue: 29 x $3,000 = $87,000
- CAC: $5,000 / 29 = $172
- LTV:CAC: $3,000 / $172 = 17.4:1

### Pipeline Health Indicators

| Indicator | Healthy | Warning | Critical |
|-----------|---------|---------|----------|
| Pipeline velocity | Leads moving through stages within SLA | 20%+ leads stalling at any stage | 40%+ leads stalling |
| Stage balance | Even distribution relative to conversion rates | Any stage > 2x expected volume | Bottleneck stage > 3x |
| Lead age | 80%+ leads < 14 days in pipeline | 30%+ leads > 14 days | 50%+ leads > 14 days |
| Follow-up compliance | 95%+ follow-ups sent on time | 80-95% compliance | Below 80% |
| Data quality | 95%+ required fields complete | 80-95% complete | Below 80% |

### Commission Structure Framework

For businesses building sales teams:

| Role | Base | Commission | On What | Performance Bonus |
|------|------|-----------|---------|-------------------|
| Setter | $0-$2K/mo | $50-$200 per qualified booking that shows | Qualified + showed calls | +$50/booking above 20/month |
| Closer | $0-$3K/mo | 5-15% of closed revenue | Closed + paid deals | +2% above 30% close rate |
| SDR/Dialer | $2-$4K/mo | $25-$75 per qualified lead passed | Leads that convert to booked calls | +$25/lead above quota |

**Split model for setter-closer teams:**
- Setter gets 30% of total commission pool
- Closer gets 70% of total commission pool
- Both are incentivized: setter for quality bookings, closer for conversions

### Speed-to-Lead Protocol

| Time After Lead | Action | Owner |
|----------------|--------|-------|
| 0-1 min | Automated confirmation (email + SMS) | System |
| 1-5 min | Phone/SMS triage from sales rep | Assigned rep |
| 5-30 min | If no rep available, escalate + reassign | System/Manager |
| 30 min-2 hr | Personal email or DM from assigned rep | Assigned rep |
| 2-24 hr | If still uncontacted, emergency escalation | Manager |

Studies show lead contact rates drop 10x after the first 5 minutes. The CRM must enforce this with automation.

## 7. Offer Presentation Framework (Sultanic Model)

### The Authority Stack

Before presenting any offer, the sales asset must establish authority using this accumulation pattern:

1. **Credential drop** (brief, not boastful) -- "After [X years/clients/revenue generated]..."
2. **Mechanism ownership** -- "We developed the [Mechanism Name] specifically for [ICP]"
3. **Proximity proof** -- "Here's what happened when [someone just like you] applied this..."
4. **Volume proof** -- "[X] people have gone through this process"
5. **Recency proof** -- "Just [last week/month], [Name] achieved [result]"

### Value Stack Presentation

Each offer component is presented as an outcome, never a feature:

| Feature Language (wrong) | Outcome Language (correct) |
|-------------------------|---------------------------|
| "8 weekly group calls" | "8 weeks of live problem-solving where you bring your biggest bottleneck and leave with a clear action plan" |
| "Private Slack community" | "24/7 access to a peer network of [ICP description] so you never feel stuck or alone in the process" |
| "Template library" | "A done-for-you toolkit that eliminates 80% of the guesswork so you only focus on what moves the needle" |
| "1:1 coaching call" | "A personalized strategy session where we map your exact 90-day plan based on where you are right now" |

Each component gets a perceived value anchor: "If you hired a [comparable service], this alone would cost you $[X]."

### The Close Sequence (Ethical)

1. Summarize their situation (mirror their words)
2. Connect their situation to the offer components (prescription)
3. Present the value stack with anchored values
4. Reveal the total value vs. the actual investment
5. State the price with confidence and silence
6. Offer payment options if needed
7. Address any remaining objections
8. Single clear next step
9. Confirmation and next-step logistics

## 8. Cross-Asset Consistency Rules

These rules ensure every sales asset in the division tells the same story:

1. **Mechanism name** must be identical everywhere -- VSL, funnel pages, scripts, DMs, proposals
2. **Transformation description** must use the same core language across all assets
3. **Price points** must match the offer document exactly -- no rounding, no "starting at" unless the offer document says "starting at"
4. **Guarantee terms** must be word-for-word consistent across all assets
5. **Case studies** used in one asset can be used in others, but the details must be consistent
6. **Objection responses** must convey the same core message across all assets -- a prospect who reads the VSL, the funnel page, and hears the closer should receive consistent responses to the same concern
7. **Brand voice** must be consistent -- a prospect should not feel like they are talking to a different company when they move from the funnel to the DMs to the sales call
8. **Timeline claims** must be consistent -- if the VSL says "results in 90 days," the script cannot say "results in 30 days"
9. **Authority claims** must be verifiable and consistent -- the same credentials, the same client count, the same results across all assets
10. **CTA** across assets should guide toward the same conversion path defined in the funnel architecture
