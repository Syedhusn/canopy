# Email Sequence Frameworks -- Complete Playbook

> Comprehensive framework for all 8 email sequence types. Each framework includes structural blueprint, email-by-email breakdown, strategic job assignments, awareness progression, timing/cadence, psychological purpose, and quality checklist.

---

## Framework 1: Welcome Sequence (5-7 Emails)

### Strategic Purpose
The welcome sequence is the most important sequence in the entire email ecosystem. It sets the relationship tone, installs initial beliefs, and creates the habit of opening emails from this creator. A well-crafted welcome sequence generates more downstream revenue than any other sequence type because it determines whether the subscriber stays engaged or ignores future emails.

### Psychological Architecture
The welcome sequence exploits the peak of subscriber curiosity -- the moment after opt-in when attention and goodwill are highest. Each email strategically deposits trust while subtly installing beliefs that prepare the subscriber for the eventual offer.

### Structural Blueprint

| Email | Day | Strategic Job | Awareness Target | Belief Installed | Copywriting Formula |
|-------|-----|---------------|-----------------|------------------|---------------------|
| 1 | 0 (immediate) | Pattern Interrupt + Delivery | Problem-Aware | "This creator is different" | Hook + value delivery |
| 2 | 1 | Origin Story | Problem-Aware | "This person understands me" | SSS (Star-Story-Solution) |
| 3 | 3 | Value Delivery | Problem to Solution-Aware | "This approach works" | PAS + teaching framework |
| 4 | 5 | Belief Installation | Solution-Aware | "The old way is broken" | PAS with mechanism introduction |
| 5 | 7 | Proof Delivery | Solution-Aware | "People like me get results" | BAB (Before-After-Bridge) |
| 6 | 10 | Desire Amplification | Solution to Product-Aware | "I want this transformation" | Future pacing + BAB |
| 7 | 14 | Soft Pitch | Product-Aware | "This offer is the next step" | AIDA with soft CTA |

### Email-by-Email Breakdown

**Email 1: The Pattern Interrupt Delivery (Day 0)**
- Subject line formula: Curiosity Gap or Specific Benefit referencing the lead magnet
- Open with a hook that surprises -- do NOT open with "Here's your download"
- Deliver the promised resource within the first 3 lines (link or attachment)
- Add one unexpected bonus insight not promised on the opt-in page
- Close with a question that opens a loop for Email 2
- CTA: Consume the lead magnet
- Psychological purpose: Exceed expectations immediately. The subscriber should think "Wow, this is more than I expected." This deposits trust and creates reciprocity.

**Email 2: The Origin Story (Day 1)**
- Subject line formula: Pattern Interrupt ("I almost quit [topic] forever")
- Open with the lowest point of the creator's journey (vulnerability hook)
- Share the turning point -- what changed, what they discovered
- Connect the story to the subscriber's current situation
- End with: "That's why I created [mechanism/offer] -- but more on that later"
- CTA: Reply with their biggest challenge (engagement + list segmentation)
- Psychological purpose: Build relatability and trust. The subscriber should think "This person was where I am. If they figured it out, maybe I can too." Installs the self-belief seed.

**Email 3: The Value Bomb (Day 3)**
- Subject line formula: Specific Benefit ("How to [outcome] in [timeframe]")
- Teach one actionable concept using the creator's unique mechanism
- Structure: Problem statement > Why conventional approach fails > The mechanism approach > Quick-start action step
- Include one mini case study or example
- CTA: Try the technique and reply with results
- Psychological purpose: Prove expertise through generosity. The subscriber should think "If the free content is this good, the paid content must be incredible." Begins the "I need more help" belief installation.

**Email 4: The Belief Shift (Day 5)**
- Subject line formula: Direct Question ("Are you making this [topic] mistake?")
- Use PAS: Name a specific mistake the ICP makes > Agitate the consequences > Introduce the mechanism as the solution
- Challenge a commonly-held belief in the ICP's world
- Present the new belief with evidence (testimonial or data)
- CTA: Read a specific blog post, watch a video, or save the framework
- Psychological purpose: Disrupt the subscriber's existing mental model and replace it with one that supports the offer. This is the pivotal belief installation email.

**Email 5: The Proof Stack (Day 7)**
- Subject line formula: Social Proof ("She went from [before] to [after]")
- Lead with a compelling transformation story (BAB format)
- Stack 2-3 additional proof points (shorter testimonials, data points)
- Strategically select proof that crushes the ICP's top objection
- CTA: Learn more about the creator's approach (link to content)
- Psychological purpose: Transfer trust from the testimonial subjects to the subscriber. "If it worked for them, maybe it works for me." Crushes skepticism.

**Email 6: The Desire Amplifier (Day 10)**
- Subject line formula: Curiosity Gap ("What nobody tells you about [desired outcome]")
- Paint a vivid picture of the "after" state in sensory detail
- Use future pacing: "Imagine waking up and [specific desired morning routine/result]"
- Contrast with the cost of staying where they are
- Mention the offer exists (first explicit mention) without hard selling
- CTA: Read a case study or watch a training
- Psychological purpose: Create emotional urgency. The subscriber should feel the gap between where they are and where they want to be, and associate the creator with the bridge.

**Email 7: The Soft Pitch (Day 14)**
- Subject line formula: Direct + Benefit ("Here's how we can work together")
- Brief recap of the value delivered in previous emails
- Introduce the offer as the natural next step
- Present the transformation promise and key value stack elements
- Include guarantee and risk reversal
- Keep it soft: "If this feels right for you" framing, not hard urgency
- CTA: Learn more about the offer (link to sales page)
- P.S.: Restate the strongest proof point
- Psychological purpose: Present the purchase as the logical conclusion of the journey so far. No pressure, but clear direction for subscribers who are ready.

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Email 1 delivers lead magnet within first 3 lines
- [ ] Email 2 includes a genuine vulnerability moment
- [ ] Emails 1-4 are 100% value with zero promotional content (40% value-first ratio exceeded)
- [ ] No two consecutive emails share the same strategic job
- [ ] Every email has exactly one CTA
- [ ] All subject lines under 50 characters
- [ ] Awareness progression is logical (no level skipping)
- [ ] Brand voice is consistent across all 7 emails
- [ ] At least one belief is explicitly installed per email
- [ ] Preview text complements (never repeats) the subject line

## Framework 2: Nurture Sequence (Ongoing)

### Strategic Purpose
The nurture sequence maintains the relationship between welcome and purchase. It delivers consistent value, continues belief installation, and keeps the creator top-of-mind. Unlike other sequences with defined endpoints, the nurture sequence runs indefinitely, requiring a sustainable content framework.

### Psychological Architecture
Nurture leverages the mere-exposure effect and the consistency principle. Subscribers who regularly receive valuable content develop a relationship with the creator that compounds over time. The key is maintaining a 3:1 value-to-pitch ratio -- three value emails for every one that mentions the offer.

### Structural Blueprint (Monthly Cycle)

| Week | Email | Strategic Job | Theme | Copywriting Formula |
|------|-------|---------------|-------|---------------------|
| 1 | Teaching email | Value Delivery | Teach a concept through the mechanism | PAS + framework |
| 1 | Story email | Belief Installation | Creator or client story that shifts a belief | SSS |
| 2 | Proof email | Proof Delivery | Case study or testimonial spotlight | BAB |
| 2 | Insight email | Value Delivery | Contrarian take or industry observation | Hook + argument + evidence |
| 3 | Teaching email | Value Delivery | Actionable framework or technique | Step-by-step with example |
| 3 | Soft pitch | Direct Pitch (soft) | Connect recent value to the offer | PASTOR |
| 4 | Community email | Belief Installation | Wins, community highlights, social proof | Roundup + celebration |
| 4 | Personal email | Origin Story variant | Behind-the-scenes, personal update, vulnerability | Personal narrative |

### Cadence: 2 emails per week or 1 email per week depending on audience tolerance.

### Content Categories with Rotation

**Category A: Teaching Emails (40% of nurture)**
- Pick one concept from the creator's methodology each email
- Structure: Problem > Why it matters > The mechanism approach > One action step
- Always end with: "Want to go deeper on this? [CTA to content or offer]"

**Category B: Story Emails (25% of nurture)**
- Client transformation stories (BAB format)
- Creator personal stories with a teaching point
- Industry stories that reinforce the mechanism belief
- "What I learned from [experience]" reflections

**Category C: Proof Emails (20% of nurture)**
- Detailed case studies with before/during/after
- Screenshot proof (revenue, results, transformations)
- "Behind the numbers" breakdowns of client results
- Social proof roundups (3-5 quick testimonials in one email)

**Category D: Pitch Emails (15% of nurture)**
- Never more than 1 in 7 emails
- Always preceded by at least 2-3 value emails
- Frame as "for those of you who are ready"
- Include fresh proof not shared before
- Use PASTOR or 4Ps formula

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Value-to-pitch ratio is 3:1 or higher
- [ ] No two consecutive emails use the same content category
- [ ] Each email teaches through the unique mechanism lens
- [ ] Subject lines vary in formula (never 3 of the same type in a row)
- [ ] At least one email per month addresses the ICP's top objection
- [ ] Brand voice is consistent but not monotonous

## Framework 3: Launch Sequence (7-14 Emails)

### Strategic Purpose
The launch sequence converts nurtured subscribers into buyers during a time-bounded campaign window. It creates urgency through real deadlines, stacks proof, handles objections, and gives subscribers every reason to buy now rather than later.

### Psychological Architecture
Launch sequences leverage urgency (deadline pressure), social proof (others are buying), loss aversion (fear of missing out), and commitment escalation (each email deepens engagement). The sequence builds emotional momentum that peaks at the deadline.

### Structural Blueprint (10-Email Launch)

| Email | Day | Strategic Job | Awareness Target | Copywriting Formula |
|-------|-----|---------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| 1 | Launch Day | Direct Pitch (announcement) | Product-Aware | AIDA |
| 2 | Day 1 | Origin Story | Product-Aware | SSS with offer context |
| 3 | Day 2 | Value Delivery | Product-Aware | Teaching + "this is inside the program" |
| 4 | Day 3 | Proof Delivery | Product-Aware | BAB -- transformation story |
| 5 | Day 4 | Objection Handling (Money) | Product-Aware | Reframe + proof + CTA |
| 6 | Day 5 | Proof Delivery (Stack) | Product-Aware | Multiple testimonials + CTA |
| 7 | Day 6 | Objection Handling (Time/Fit) | Product-Aware | Reframe + testimonial |
| 8 | Day 7 | Desire Amplification | Most-Aware | Future pacing + cost of inaction |
| 9 | Day 8 (48h) | Urgency Creation | Most-Aware | 4Ps -- 48-hour warning |
| 10 | Day 9 (final) | Urgency Creation (Final) | Most-Aware | Final CTA + last-chance proof |

### Phase Breakdown

**Phase 1: Open Cart (Emails 1-2, Days 0-1)**
- Announce the offer is available. Lead with the transformation, not features.
- Email 1: The "doors are open" email. Direct, exciting, comprehensive. AIDA format.
- Email 2: The "why I created this" email. Creator's origin story connected to the offer.
- Psychological purpose: Excitement + relatability. Capture early buyers (20-30% of launch revenue comes from the first 24-48 hours).

**Phase 2: Value + Proof (Emails 3-6, Days 2-5)**
- Alternate between teaching (demonstrating the value of what is inside) and proof (showing it works).
- Email 3: Teach one concept from the offer curriculum as a free value email, then reveal it is just Module 1 of 8.
- Email 4: Full transformation story from a past client/student. BAB format with specific numbers and timeline.
- Email 5: Address the money objection directly. Reframe the investment as cost-per-day, compare to cost of inaction, include a testimonial from someone who hesitated.
- Email 6: Stack 3-5 testimonials, each addressing a different objection (time, fit, experience level, industry).
- Psychological purpose: Build conviction that the offer works, handle the rational objections that prevent purchase.

**Phase 3: Urgency Close (Emails 7-10, Days 6-9)**
- Shift tone from educational to urgent. The deadline is real.
- Email 7: Handle the "timing" objection. "Is now the right time?" Reframe: "It's never the right time. The question is whether you're willing to start."
- Email 8: Future pace. "Imagine 90 days from now..." Paint the after-state vividly, then contrast with the cost of waiting.
- Email 9: 48-hour warning. Direct, clear, no fluff. Restate what they get, the price, the guarantee, and the deadline.
- Email 10: Final email. "Doors close tonight at midnight." Shortest email of the sequence. Subject line: "Last chance." Include final testimonial and P.S. with guarantee reminder.
- Psychological purpose: Convert fence-sitters through legitimate urgency and loss aversion.

### Launch Bonus Strategy (Integrated)
- Announce early-bird bonus in Email 1 (expires Day 3)
- Reveal a surprise bonus in Email 6 (not previously announced)
- Remind of all bonuses in Email 9 (48-hour email)
- Note which bonuses expire at close in Email 10

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] First email within 1 hour of cart opening
- [ ] No two consecutive emails share the same strategic job
- [ ] At least 3 objection-handling emails in the sequence
- [ ] At least 5 unique testimonials across the sequence
- [ ] Final 3 emails focus exclusively on urgency (no new teaching)
- [ ] Every email includes a direct link to the sales page
- [ ] P.S. lines in every email from Day 3 onward
- [ ] Subject lines escalate in urgency across the final 3 emails
- [ ] Early-bird and surprise bonus are both included
- [ ] Brand voice maintained even in urgency emails (no desperation tone)

## Framework 4: Webinar Sequence (4-6 Emails)

### Strategic Purpose
The webinar email sequence maximizes registration-to-attendance conversion. Show rates are the single biggest lever for webinar revenue -- a 10% increase in show rate can double sales. This sequence builds anticipation, reduces friction, and creates commitment before the event.

### Structural Blueprint

| Email | Timing | Strategic Job | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------------|---------|
| 1 | Immediate (registration) | Pattern Interrupt + Confirmation | Confirm, set expectations, build excitement |
| 2 | 48 hours before | Desire Amplification | Share one key takeaway, build anticipation |
| 3 | 24 hours before | Belief Installation | Social proof -- "X people registered, here's why" |
| 4 | 6 hours before | Value Delivery (teaser) | Share a quick insight to demonstrate webinar quality |
| 5 | 1 hour before | Urgency Creation | "We're about to start" with direct link |
| 6 | Post-event (2-4 hours after) | Direct Pitch | Replay link + offer CTA + deadline |

### Email-by-Email Breakdown

**Email 1: Confirmation + Excitement Builder (Immediate)**
- Subject: "You're in! [Webinar title]"
- Confirm date, time, and access method (calendar link or platform URL)
- Add to calendar CTA (iCal, Google Calendar links)
- Share one surprising thing they will learn (open loop)
- Set expectations: "This is not a lecture. You will leave with [specific outcome]."
- CTA: Add to calendar
- Psychological purpose: Reduce the chance they forget. The calendar add is the highest-leverage action for show rate.

**Email 2: Anticipation Builder (48 hours before)**
- Subject: Curiosity Gap about a specific teaching point
- Share one insight from the webinar content (enough to create desire, not enough to satisfy it)
- Include a testimonial from someone who attended a previous event
- Remind of the date/time
- CTA: "Clear your schedule and show up ready to take notes"
- Psychological purpose: Create information gap. They now know just enough to want more.

**Email 3: Social Proof Push (24 hours before)**
- Subject: Social proof ("X people are attending tomorrow")
- Share the registration count if impressive
- Include 2-3 quick testimonials or anticipated outcomes
- Remind of time and access details
- CTA: "Show up tomorrow and I'll show you [specific promise]"
- Psychological purpose: Social proof reduces the "is this worth my time?" objection.

**Email 4: Pre-Event Teaser (6 hours before)**
- Subject: Benefit teaser for what they will learn today
- Very short email. 3-5 sentences maximum.
- Share one quick tip or reframe as a preview of webinar quality
- "See you in [X] hours"
- CTA: Direct link to webinar room
- Psychological purpose: Final mental preparation. Moving the webinar from "something on my calendar" to "something happening today."

**Email 5: Starting Now (1 hour before / at start)**
- Subject: "We're live!" or "Starting in 60 minutes"
- 2-3 sentences maximum. No teaching, no stories.
- Direct link to join
- "Don't be late -- the first 10 minutes contain the most valuable framework"
- CTA: Join now link (prominent)
- Psychological purpose: Remove all friction. One click to attend.

**Email 6: Post-Event Follow-Up (2-4 hours after)**
- Subject: "Replay + special offer inside"
- Thank attendees, acknowledge those who missed it
- Share replay link (if available)
- Recap the top 3 takeaways
- Present the offer with a deadline (replay access expires, bonus expires, price increases)
- CTA: Watch replay or purchase directly
- P.S.: "The offer I shared at the end is available for [timeframe]. Here's the link: [URL]"
- Psychological purpose: Capture the post-event momentum. Attendees who felt the pitch live are most likely to buy within 24 hours.

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Calendar add link included in confirmation email
- [ ] Each email includes the event date, time, and access link
- [ ] Emails escalate in urgency from informational to "join now"
- [ ] Post-event email includes replay link and offer with deadline
- [ ] No email exceeds the subscriber's attention tolerance (emails 4-5 are under 100 words)
- [ ] Social proof is included in at least 2 pre-event emails

## Framework 5: Re-engagement Sequence (3-5 Emails)

### Strategic Purpose
The re-engagement sequence wins back subscribers who have gone cold (30-60 days of no opens/clicks). It also serves as a list hygiene tool -- subscribers who do not re-engage after this sequence are removed, improving deliverability for engaged subscribers.

### Psychological Architecture
Re-engagement leverages curiosity (what have they missed?), loss aversion (you will lose access), and direct honesty (should I remove you?). The final email uses the "break up" technique -- threatening removal is the most effective re-engagement tactic because it triggers loss aversion.

### Structural Blueprint

| Email | Day | Strategic Job | Approach |
|-------|-----|---------------|----------|
| 1 | 0 | Pattern Interrupt | Curiosity-driven "miss you" with value hook |
| 2 | 3 | Value Delivery | Best content piece -- prove what they're missing |
| 3 | 5 | Proof Delivery | Transformation story of someone who stayed engaged |
| 4 | 7 | Direct Question | "Should I keep sending you emails?" |
| 5 | 10 (optional) | Urgency Creation (removal) | "Removing you tomorrow unless you click" |

### Email-by-Email Breakdown

**Email 1: The Curiosity Hook (Day 0)**
- Subject: Pattern Interrupt ("Did I do something wrong?")
- Acknowledge the silence without being needy
- Share one new thing that has happened since they last engaged
- Open a curiosity loop about a recent result or insight
- CTA: Click to confirm they want to stay subscribed
- Tone: Warm, slightly humorous, zero guilt

**Email 2: The Value Bomb (Day 3)**
- Subject: Specific Benefit (best-performing subject line format)
- Send the single best piece of content you have
- Make it so valuable that not opening feels like a loss
- No pitch, no ask beyond "was this helpful?"
- CTA: Reply with their biggest challenge right now

**Email 3: The Proof Reminder (Day 5)**
- Subject: Social Proof ("She almost unsubscribed too")
- Tell the story of someone who was on the fence, stayed engaged, and got a result
- Contrast with what would have happened if they left
- CTA: "If you're still interested in [outcome], here's what's coming next"

**Email 4: The Direct Ask (Day 7)**
- Subject: Direct Question ("Should I remove you from this list?")
- Be completely transparent: "I only want to email people who want to hear from me"
- Give them two options: click to stay or do nothing and be removed
- No manipulation, no guilt -- genuine respect for their attention
- CTA: Single click to confirm continued subscription

**Email 5: The Removal Warning (Day 10, optional)**
- Subject: "Removing you tomorrow"
- Final notice: "If I don't hear from you by [time], I'll remove you from the list"
- Brief reminder of what they will miss
- Single CTA: Click to stay
- Psychological purpose: Loss aversion is the strongest motivator. This email consistently gets the highest open rate of any re-engagement sequence.

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Email 1 is curiosity-driven, not guilt-driven
- [ ] Email 2 contains genuinely valuable content (not a disguised pitch)
- [ ] The direct ask (Email 4) gives a clear binary choice with no manipulation
- [ ] Subscribers who do not engage after the full sequence are actually removed
- [ ] Tone throughout is respectful and mature, never passive-aggressive

## Framework 6: Application Sequence (3-5 Emails)

### Strategic Purpose
The application sequence nurtures high-ticket leads who have visited an application page but have not completed the application. It builds exclusivity perception, addresses high-ticket objections, and moves prospects from consideration to commitment.

### Structural Blueprint

| Email | Day | Strategic Job | Focus |
|-------|-----|---------------|-------|
| 1 | 0 (immediate) | Pattern Interrupt | "Noticed you started your application" + what to expect |
| 2 | 2 | Proof Delivery | Detailed transformation story of an accepted applicant |
| 3 | 4 | Objection Handling | Address "am I ready?" and "is this worth the investment?" |
| 4 | 6 | Desire Amplification | Future pace life after the program + exclusivity framing |
| 5 | 8 | Urgency Creation | "Applications close [date]" or "limited spots remaining" |

### Key Principles
- Tone is exclusive but welcoming -- "not everyone is right for this, but the fact that you started an application suggests you might be"
- Every email reinforces that this is a selective process (even if it is not heavily selective)
- Testimonials specifically from people who went through the application process and were glad they did
- Address the high-ticket-specific objections: investment anxiety, time commitment, spouse/partner conversation, "can I do this?"
- CTA is always "Complete your application" -- never "buy now"

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Exclusivity framing in every email (never desperate or salesy)
- [ ] At least one testimonial from an accepted applicant
- [ ] High-ticket objections (investment, readiness) addressed by Email 3
- [ ] CTA is always "complete your application," maintaining the qualification frame
- [ ] Tone conveys confidence: "We'd love to have you, but only if it's the right fit"

## Framework 7: Post-Purchase Sequence (5-7 Emails)

### Strategic Purpose
The post-purchase sequence reduces buyer's remorse, onboards the customer, sets expectations, delivers an early win, and seeds the next offer. Buyer's remorse peaks in the first 24-72 hours after purchase -- this sequence must meet the customer exactly in that moment with validation, excitement, and a clear path forward.

### Structural Blueprint

| Email | Day | Strategic Job | Psychological Purpose |
|-------|-----|---------------|-----------------------|
| 1 | 0 (immediate) | Pattern Interrupt (Celebration) | "You made the right decision" -- validate the purchase |
| 2 | 1 | Value Delivery (Onboarding) | "Here's exactly what to do first" -- reduce overwhelm |
| 3 | 3 | Belief Installation (Commitment) | "Here's what successful students do in Week 1" -- set the standard |
| 4 | 7 | Proof Delivery | "Meet [name], who was in your exact position 6 months ago" |
| 5 | 14 | Value Delivery (Check-in) | "How's it going? Here's a tip for where you should be" |
| 6 | 21 | Desire Amplification (Upsell seed) | "Now that you've [milestone], here's what's next" |
| 7 | 30 | Direct Pitch (Upsell/Cross-sell) | Introduce the next offer or testimonial request |

### Key Principles
- Email 1 is the most critical email in the entire customer lifecycle. It must arrive within minutes of purchase.
- Email 2 must give ONE clear first step, not a list of 20 things to do. Overwhelm causes inaction.
- The "successful students" email (Email 3) uses social proof to set behavioral expectations -- "Here's what the people who get the best results do in their first week."
- No selling until Email 6 at the earliest. The first 5 emails are 100% value and support.
- Testimonial in Email 4 should be from someone who started exactly where this buyer is starting.

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Email 1 arrives within 5 minutes of purchase
- [ ] Email 2 gives exactly ONE first step, not a content dump
- [ ] No promotional content in emails 1-5
- [ ] Buyer's remorse is specifically addressed in Email 1 ("You made the right decision because...")
- [ ] Community onboarding is included if a community exists
- [ ] Upsell seed is natural, not forced

## Framework 8: Single Broadcast Email

### Strategic Purpose
Single broadcast emails are one-off sends for promotions, educational content, announcements, or special events. Unlike sequences, they must stand alone -- the subscriber may not have context from a preceding email.

### Types and Structures

**Type A: Promotional Broadcast**
- Formula: PASTOR (Problem-Amplify-Story-Transformation-Offer-Response)
- Open with a pain point or desire hook
- Amplify with a story or data point
- Present the offer as the solution
- Single CTA with deadline or scarcity element
- P.S. with guarantee or strongest proof point

**Type B: Educational Broadcast**
- Formula: PAS + Teaching
- Open with a problem the subscriber is facing right now
- Teach one actionable concept through the mechanism lens
- End with a CTA to content (blog, video, podcast) or a soft offer mention
- No P.S. unless bridging to an offer

**Type C: Announcement Broadcast**
- Formula: Hook + News + Implication + CTA
- Lead with the most exciting or surprising element of the announcement
- Explain what it means for the subscriber specifically
- Clear CTA: What should they do about this news?
- Keep it short -- announcements lose impact with length

### Subject Line A/B Test Protocol
For every broadcast, provide two subject line options:
- Option A: Curiosity-driven
- Option B: Benefit-driven
Test with 20% of the list, send the winner to 80%.

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Subject line under 50 characters
- [ ] Single CTA
- [ ] Preview text written and complements subject
- [ ] Can be understood without context from previous emails
- [ ] Brand voice maintained
- [ ] P.S. included on promotional broadcasts
- [ ] A/B subject line options provided

## Universal Quality Standards (All Sequence Types)

### Pre-Send Audit Checklist

**Structure:**
- [ ] Every email has exactly one strategic job assigned
- [ ] No two consecutive emails share the same strategic job
- [ ] Awareness progression is logical (no level skipping)
- [ ] Value-first ratio met (first 40% is pure value)
- [ ] Each email has exactly one CTA

**Copy:**
- [ ] All subject lines under 50 characters
- [ ] All preview text written and complements subject
- [ ] No generic openings ("Hey [Name], hope you're doing well")
- [ ] Every email opens with a hook that earns the next line
- [ ] P.S. lines on all sales-focused emails
- [ ] Brand voice consistent across the entire sequence

**Strategy:**
- [ ] Beliefs installed map to the ICP's limiting belief triad
- [ ] ICP pain points and desires are referenced with specific language
- [ ] Offer details match reference/offers.md exactly
- [ ] Testimonials selected to crush specific ICP objections
- [ ] Sequence cadence matches the type specification

**Technical:**
- [ ] All links are properly formatted with placeholders
- [ ] Unsubscribe considerations noted for compliance
- [ ] Merge tags indicated where personalization is needed
- [ ] Email length appropriate for the strategic job (value emails can be longer; urgency emails should be short)
