# Lead Magnet Frameworks -- Complete Playbook

> Comprehensive framework for all 9 lead magnet types. Each framework includes structural blueprint, section-by-section breakdown, psychological purpose, belief installation strategy, and quality checklist.

---

## Universal Principles (All Lead Magnet Types)

### The Sub-Problem Selection Protocol

Before designing any lead magnet, map the paid offer's full transformation into 5 component sub-problems:

```
Full Transformation (Paid Offer): [E.g., "Build a profitable online coaching business"]
  Sub-Problem A: [Most urgent, highest pain] -- "Getting the first 3 clients"
  Sub-Problem B: [Most common, widest relevance] -- "Creating a consistent content system"
  Sub-Problem C: [Most misunderstood, belief-shifting] -- "Pricing correctly without undercharging"
  Sub-Problem D: [Easiest to solve, fastest win] -- "Writing a DM script that starts conversations"
  Sub-Problem E: [Most visible result, shareable] -- "Building a landing page that converts"
```

Select the sub-problem scoring highest on: **Urgency + Speed-to-Win + Connection-to-Offer**

### The Bridge Architecture

Every lead magnet must contain a bridge to the paid offer. The bridge is NOT a hard sell. It follows this structure:

1. **Acknowledge the win** -- "You just [achieved the quick win]. That's significant."
2. **Reveal the bigger picture** -- "But [quick win] is just one piece of the puzzle. The full transformation requires [elements from the paid offer]."
3. **Natural invitation** -- "If you want help implementing all of this with step-by-step guidance, [offer name] is where I walk you through the complete process."
4. **No-pressure close** -- "Either way, use what you learned here. It works."

### Belief Installation Protocol

Every lead magnet installs exactly 1-3 beliefs:

| Belief Slot | Purpose | Installation Method |
|-------------|---------|---------------------|
| Primary | "This approach works" | Teach through the unique mechanism, show results within the lead magnet |
| Secondary | "This creator is an expert" | Demonstrate depth and specificity only an expert would know |
| Tertiary | "I need more structured help" | Deliver a genuine win that reveals the complexity of the full transformation |

## Framework 1: Custom Lead Magnet

### When to Use
When the ICP's specific situation requires a format not covered by the standard 8 types, or when combining elements from multiple types creates a more effective resource.

### Design Protocol
1. Load all four foundation documents
2. Identify the sub-problem that standard types cannot adequately address
3. Select the delivery format that best matches the ICP's consumption preferences
4. Apply the universal principles (sub-problem selection, bridge architecture, belief installation)
5. Structure using the closest standard type as a baseline, then adapt

### Structural Blueprint
- **Opening:** Context + outcome promise (under 1 page)
- **Core content:** Adapted from the closest standard framework
- **Implementation:** Clear next steps
- **Bridge:** Natural transition to paid offer

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Justified why a custom type was needed over the standard 8
- [ ] Still solves exactly one sub-problem
- [ ] Quick win achievable in under 15 minutes
- [ ] Bridge to offer is natural, not forced
- [ ] Beliefs installed are documented

## Framework 2: PDF Guide (5-15 Pages)

### Strategic Purpose
The PDF Guide is the workhorse lead magnet -- best for topics that require structured explanation, multiple steps, or conceptual depth that cannot be compressed into a checklist. It positions the creator as a thorough authority and delivers value through frameworks, examples, and implementation guidance.

### Psychological Architecture
The PDF Guide leverages the "completeness" psychology -- people value resources that feel comprehensive enough to be a standalone solution. The guide should feel like it could be sold for $27-47, making the free price point feel like a gift.

### Structural Blueprint

**Section 1: Quick Context (0.5 pages)**
- Why this topic matters right now
- What the reader will achieve by the end
- One specific outcome promise: "By page 10, you will have [concrete result]"
- No creator biography. No lengthy introductions. Straight to value.

**Section 2: The Core Framework (2-4 pages)**
- Introduce the mechanism or methodology that solves the sub-problem
- Structure: Name the framework > Explain why it works > Show the components
- Use visual: diagram, flowchart, or matrix that summarizes the framework
- This section answers "WHAT" -- the high-level approach
- Psychological purpose: Install Belief 1 -- "This approach works and it's different from what I've tried"

**Section 3: Step-by-Step Application (3-5 pages)**
- Walk through implementation in sequential steps
- Each step includes: What to do, why it matters, common mistakes to avoid, one example
- Use numbered steps for clarity
- Include mini-exercises or "do this now" prompts
- Psychological purpose: Install Belief 2 -- "This creator knows their stuff at a level of detail I don't have"

**Section 4: Examples and Case Studies (1-2 pages)**
- 2-3 examples of the framework applied in real scenarios
- Before/after format for maximum impact
- At least one example from someone in a situation similar to the ICP
- Include specific numbers and timelines
- Psychological purpose: Install Belief 3 -- "This works for people like me"

**Section 5: Quick-Start Action Plan (1 page)**
- Exactly 3-5 things to do TODAY after reading
- Formatted as a checklist for immediate action
- Each action should be completable in under 15 minutes
- Psychological purpose: Create momentum. An engaged reader who takes action becomes a warm lead.

**Section 6: Bridge to Offer (0.5 pages)**
- Acknowledge what they just learned
- Reveal the larger picture: "You now have [framework]. But the full transformation requires [additional elements from the offer]"
- Present the paid offer as the natural next step
- Single CTA link
- No-pressure framing

### Title Formula Application
- Primary: "The [Adjective] Guide to [Desired Outcome] [Without Common Objection]"
- Example: "The Complete Guide to Landing Your First 3 Coaching Clients Without Paid Ads"

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Total length 5-15 pages (not shorter, not longer)
- [ ] Quick Context section is under 1 page (no bloated intros)
- [ ] Core framework includes a visual diagram or matrix
- [ ] Step-by-step section has at least one example per step
- [ ] Case studies include specific numbers and timelines
- [ ] Action plan has 3-5 items, each doable in under 15 minutes
- [ ] Bridge to offer is natural and under 0.5 pages
- [ ] Written in the creator's brand voice throughout
- [ ] Teaches through the unique mechanism from positioning.md

## Framework 3: Checklist (1-2 Pages)

### Strategic Purpose
The Checklist is the lowest-friction, highest-opt-in lead magnet type. It works because it converts knowledge into action -- instead of "here's what to learn," it says "here's exactly what to do." Best for process-oriented audiences who value clarity and action over theory.

### Psychological Architecture
Checklists leverage the Zeigarnik Effect -- incomplete tasks create psychological tension. A half-checked checklist creates a drive to complete it. They also reduce cognitive load: instead of deciding what to do, the reader simply checks boxes.

### Structural Blueprint

**Header Block**
- Title using formula: "The [Number]-Step [Topic] Checklist: [Outcome] in [Timeframe]"
- One-line outcome promise
- Estimated completion time: "[X] minutes to complete"

**Pre-Flight Section (Optional, 2-3 items)**
- What to have ready before starting
- "Before you begin, make sure you have: [tools, information, access]"
- Sets the reader up for success

**Checklist Body (10-25 items, grouped into 3-5 phases)**

Phase structure:
```
PHASE 1: [Phase Name] (Items 1-5)
  [ ] Item 1: [Action verb] + [specific thing to do]
      Why: [One sentence explaining why this matters]
  [ ] Item 2: [Action verb] + [specific thing to do]
      Why: [One sentence]
  ...

PHASE 2: [Phase Name] (Items 6-10)
  ...
```

Each checklist item must:
- Start with an action verb (Write, Create, Set up, Review, Send, Test)
- Be specific enough to execute without additional research
- Include a one-line "Why" explanation
- Be completable as a standalone action

**Completion Milestone**
- "When all items are checked, you will have: [specific outcome]"
- Celebrate the accomplishment
- Provide a next step

**Bridge Section**
- "This checklist covers [sub-problem]. For the complete system, including [list 2-3 additional elements from the paid offer], check out [offer name]."
- Single CTA link

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Title includes a number and outcome
- [ ] Total items between 10-25 (fewer feels incomplete, more feels overwhelming)
- [ ] Items grouped into 3-5 logical phases
- [ ] Every item starts with an action verb
- [ ] Every item is specific enough to execute without Googling
- [ ] "Why" explanations add genuine value (not just filler)
- [ ] Completion milestone is specific and motivating
- [ ] Entire checklist completable in under 15 minutes
- [ ] Bridge to offer is 2-3 sentences, not a full sales pitch

## Framework 4: Cheat Sheet (1-2 Pages)

### Strategic Purpose
The Cheat Sheet is a quick-reference resource designed to be saved, printed, and revisited repeatedly. Unlike a checklist (sequential), a cheat sheet is a reference card (non-sequential). It works best for audiences who need to remember formulas, frameworks, or decision criteria during execution.

### Psychological Architecture
Cheat sheets create ongoing engagement because they are referenced repeatedly. Every time the reader pulls up the cheat sheet, they re-encounter the creator's methodology and brand. This repeated exposure deepens the mechanism belief over time.

### Structural Blueprint

**Header**
- Title: "The [Topic] Cheat Sheet: [Category] at a Glance"
- One-line description of what it covers

**Core Content (formatted for scanning, not reading)**

Format options (choose based on content):
- **Table format:** Rows = scenarios/situations, Columns = what to do / what to avoid / example
- **Formula format:** Named formulas with components and examples
- **Decision tree:** "If [situation] then [action]" format
- **Quick-reference cards:** Category headers with 3-5 bullet points each

**Usage Instructions**
- "Keep this open while you [activity]"
- "Reference Section 2 when you encounter [specific situation]"

**Bridge**
- Brief: "Want the full methodology behind these shortcuts? [Offer name] goes deep on every one."

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Fits on 1-2 pages (can be printed and pinned to a wall)
- [ ] Scannable in under 30 seconds (no long paragraphs)
- [ ] Each section is independently useful (not sequential)
- [ ] Designed to be referenced repeatedly, not read once
- [ ] Teaches through the unique mechanism terminology
- [ ] Visual hierarchy is clear (headers, bold, spacing)

## Framework 5: Swipe File (5-20 Items)

### Strategic Purpose
The Swipe File gives the reader ready-to-use templates, examples, or scripts they can adapt immediately. It is the ultimate "done-for-you" lead magnet -- instead of teaching how to create something, you provide completed examples they can model or modify.

### Psychological Architecture
Swipe files reduce the creation burden from "create from scratch" to "adapt an existing example." This addresses the Helpless ICP pattern directly -- they do not need to figure it out, they just need to fill in the blanks.

### Structural Blueprint

**Introduction (0.5 pages)**
- What the swipe file contains and how to use it
- "Don't copy these word-for-word. Adapt them to your voice and situation."
- Quick overview of the categories included

**Swipe Items (5-20 items, grouped by category)**

For each swipe item:
```
ITEM [#]: [Descriptive Name]
Category: [category]
Best For: [when to use this]
Context: [1-2 sentences on why this works]

[THE SWIPE CONTENT]
-- Example text, template, or script --

Customization Notes: [How to adapt this for different situations]
```

**Usage Guide**
- "Start with items [X, Y, Z] if you're a beginner"
- "Mix items [A, B, C] for an advanced approach"
- Decision framework for which items to use when

**Bridge to Offer**
- "These [X] examples cover [sub-problem]. Inside [offer name], you get [larger number] templates plus the strategy behind why each one works."

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Each swipe item is genuinely usable (not just an outline)
- [ ] Items are grouped by category for easy navigation
- [ ] Customization notes explain how to adapt, not just "make it your own"
- [ ] At least 5 items minimum, 20 maximum
- [ ] Variety across items (not 10 versions of the same thing)
- [ ] "Best For" context helps the reader select the right item
- [ ] Bridge reveals that more templates exist inside the paid offer

## Framework 6: Mini Course (3-5 Lessons)

### Strategic Purpose
The Mini Course builds the deepest trust of all lead magnet types because it creates a multi-day engagement loop. Each lesson delivers value AND creates a commitment to return for the next lesson. By the end, the subscriber has invested significant time and attention, making them highly qualified leads.

### Psychological Architecture
Mini courses leverage the investment effect (the more time someone invests, the less likely they are to walk away) and progressive disclosure (each lesson reveals more of the mechanism, creating curiosity for the next). They also install beliefs sequentially, one per lesson.

### Structural Blueprint

**Lesson 1: The Quick Win (Day 1)**
- Subject: Specific Benefit -- "Let's get your first result"
- Teach the simplest, fastest technique from the methodology
- Must produce a tangible result within 15 minutes of implementation
- End with: "Tomorrow, I'll show you why this worked -- and how to make it 10x more powerful"
- Belief installed: "This approach works" (evidence: they just experienced a result)

**Lesson 2: The Why (Day 2 or 3)**
- Subject: Curiosity Gap -- "The reason behind yesterday's result"
- Explain the mechanism behind Lesson 1's quick win
- Introduce the creator's unique framework by name
- Show how the quick win is a subset of a larger system
- End with: "Now you know the framework. Tomorrow, I'll show you the mistakes that kill most people's results"
- Belief installed: "This creator understands the deeper system" (expertise proof)

**Lesson 3: The Pitfalls (Day 3 or 5)**
- Subject: Direct Question -- "Are you making these [topic] mistakes?"
- Teach the 3-5 most common mistakes people make when trying to achieve this outcome
- For each mistake: what it is, why people make it, what to do instead
- Include a testimonial from someone who was making these mistakes and overcame them
- End with: "Tomorrow, I'll show you what's possible when you avoid these mistakes"
- Belief installed: "The old way (and my past failures) had a structural flaw, not a personal flaw"

**Lesson 4: The Possibility (Day 5 or 7)**
- Subject: Social Proof -- "What [name] achieved in [timeframe]"
- Full case study of someone who applied the methodology
- Before/during/after structure with specific numbers
- Show the gap between what the mini course teaches and the full implementation
- End with: "Tomorrow, I'll share the complete implementation path"
- Belief installed: "People like me get real results with this" (self-belief)

**Lesson 5: The Implementation + Bridge (Day 7 or 10)**
- Subject: Benefit + Bridge -- "Your complete [topic] implementation plan"
- Provide a high-level implementation roadmap
- Show all the pieces needed for the full transformation (some taught in the mini course, others only available in the paid offer)
- Natural bridge: "Lessons 1-4 gave you [elements]. But the full transformation requires [additional elements]. That's exactly what [offer name] delivers."
- CTA: Learn about the offer
- P.S.: Recap the strongest proof point
- Belief installed: "I need structured support to implement the full system" (readiness for offer)

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Lesson 1 delivers a genuine quick win within 15 minutes
- [ ] Each lesson has a clear open loop to the next lesson
- [ ] Lessons are spaced to build anticipation without losing momentum
- [ ] Each lesson installs exactly one belief
- [ ] No promotional content until Lesson 5
- [ ] The bridge in Lesson 5 feels natural, not abrupt
- [ ] Total mini course could stand alone as valuable even if they never buy
- [ ] Written in the creator's brand voice for all lessons

## Framework 7: Quiz / Assessment

### Strategic Purpose
Quizzes and assessments have the highest opt-in rates (40-60%) of all lead magnet types because they offer personalized results. People are inherently curious about themselves. The quiz also serves as a powerful segmentation tool -- results data helps tailor future email sequences.

### Psychological Architecture
Quizzes leverage the Barnum Effect (people believe personalized descriptions even when they are somewhat general) and the self-assessment drive (we are compelled to understand ourselves). The results page is the highest-attention moment -- this is where belief installation is most effective.

### Structural Blueprint

**Quiz Title**
- Formula: "What's Your [Topic] Type/Score/Level?"
- Example: "What's Your Fitness Business Revenue Ceiling?"
- The title must promise self-knowledge the reader cannot get elsewhere

**Question Design (5-10 Questions)**

For each question:
```
Question [#]: [Question text]
  A) [Answer option -- maps to Result Type 1]
  B) [Answer option -- maps to Result Type 2]
  C) [Answer option -- maps to Result Type 3]

Scoring: A = [points/type], B = [points/type], C = [points/type]
Psychological purpose: This question reveals [belief or behavior pattern]
```

Question design principles:
- Questions should feel insightful, not obvious
- Each question should reveal something the reader has not consciously articulated
- Avoid binary yes/no -- use multiple choice with nuanced options
- Mix behavior questions ("What do you do when...") with belief questions ("Which statement resonates most?")

**Result Types (3-5 Types)**

For each result type:
```
RESULT: [Type Name]
Score Range: [X-Y points]
Percentage of takers who get this result: [estimated %]

Description:
[2-3 paragraphs that feel deeply personal and accurate]
- Strengths of this type
- Blind spots of this type
- Common patterns and behaviors
- What this type needs most right now

Personalized Recommendation:
[Specific action step tailored to this type]

Bridge to Offer (Type-Specific):
"Based on your result, the [specific component of offer] would be most valuable for you because [reason tied to their type]."
```

**Results Page Design**
- Lead with the type name and a positive framing ("You're a [Type Name]!")
- Description should feel like the quiz knows them personally
- Include: "Only [X]% of people score in this range"
- Personalized next step
- Bridge to offer tailored to their result type

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Quiz title promises self-knowledge (not just information)
- [ ] 5-10 questions (fewer feels superficial, more creates abandonment)
- [ ] Each question reveals a genuine insight, not filler
- [ ] 3-5 result types, each with unique descriptions and recommendations
- [ ] Results feel personalized and accurate (pass the "that's so me" test)
- [ ] Bridge to offer is tailored per result type (not generic)
- [ ] Data collected enables email segmentation by result type
- [ ] No question has an obviously "right" answer

## Framework 8: Calculator / Tool

### Strategic Purpose
Calculators and interactive tools provide quantified, personalized answers to specific questions the ICP is asking. They convert data into insights and give the prospect a number they can act on. Best for data-driven audiences who make decisions based on metrics.

### Psychological Architecture
Calculators leverage the concreteness effect -- specific numbers are more persuasive than abstract concepts. "You could earn $8,200/month" is more motivating than "You could earn more money." The calculated result creates an anchor that the paid offer can be measured against.

### Structural Blueprint

**Calculator Title**
- Formula: "[Topic] Calculator: Find Your [Metric]"
- Example: "Revenue Ceiling Calculator: Find Your Coaching Business Earning Potential"

**Input Fields (3-7 Fields)**

For each input:
```
Field [#]: [Label]
Type: [number / dropdown / slider]
Default value: [sensible default]
Help text: "[Brief explanation of what to enter]"
Impact on result: [How this input affects the output]
```

Design principles:
- Minimum inputs for maximum output (respect the user's time)
- Default values should produce a moderate result (not best-case)
- Each input should feel like it matters to the calculation
- Include one "reveal" input -- a factor the user has not considered before

**Output Display**

Primary output:
- One headline number: "Your estimated [metric]: $[number]"
- Context for the number: "This puts you in the [top/bottom X%] of [group]"

Secondary outputs:
- 2-3 supporting metrics that add depth
- Comparison: "The average [person in this situation] achieves [Y]. Your inputs suggest [Z]."

**Interpretation Section**
- What the number means for their situation
- What they should focus on based on the result
- Common mistakes people in their range make

**Bridge to Offer**
- "Based on your calculation, the biggest lever for reaching [higher target] is [element addressed by the paid offer]"
- "Inside [offer name], we specifically focus on [lever] in Module [X]"

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] 3-7 input fields (fewer feels too simple, more creates friction)
- [ ] Default values produce a reasonable mid-range result
- [ ] Primary output is one clear, motivating number
- [ ] Interpretation section adds genuine insight beyond the number
- [ ] Bridge to offer connects the calculator result to a specific offer component
- [ ] Calculator result creates a positive anchor for the offer price comparison

## Framework 9: Free Training (30-60 Minute Video)

### Strategic Purpose
Free Training is the highest-investment lead magnet for both creator (production effort) and prospect (time commitment). It delivers the deepest authority proof because the prospect watches the creator teach for 30-60 minutes, evaluating expertise, communication style, and trustworthiness in real time. Best for offers over $997 where the prospect needs high trust before considering a purchase.

### Psychological Architecture
Free trainings leverage the parasocial relationship effect -- watching someone teach for 30-60 minutes creates a feeling of knowing them personally. They also leverage reciprocity at scale: "They just gave me an hour of valuable teaching for free -- their paid content must be incredible."

### Structural Blueprint

**Pre-Training Landing Page**
- Title: "Free Training: How to [Specific Outcome] [Without Common Objection]"
- Subtitle: "In this [X]-minute training, you'll learn:"
- 3-5 bullet points of what they will learn (benefit-oriented, not topic-oriented)
- Authority markers: "[X] students, [Y] years, featured in [Z]"
- Social proof element: testimonial or results screenshot
- CTA: "Get Instant Access" (email required)

**Training Structure (30-60 minutes)**

| Section | Duration | Purpose | Content |
|---------|----------|---------|---------|
| Hook | 2-3 min | Grab attention, make a promise | Bold claim or result preview |
| Authority | 3-5 min | Build credibility | Results, testimonials, story |
| Teaching Point 1 | 8-12 min | Deliver first insight | Concept + example + application |
| Teaching Point 2 | 8-12 min | Deliver second insight | Concept + example + application |
| Teaching Point 3 | 8-12 min | Deliver third insight | Concept + example + application |
| Recap | 2-3 min | Summarize learnings | Quick recap of all 3 points |
| Bridge to Offer | 5-8 min | Natural transition to paid offer | "If you want to go deeper..." |

**Slide Design Notes (per section)**
- One key point per slide (no walls of text)
- Use visuals, diagrams, and screenshots over bullet points
- Include the framework's visual representation
- Customer result screenshots placed at high-attention moments

**Bridge to Offer (within the training)**
- "You now have the [X framework]. But implementing it fully requires [Y, Z] -- which is exactly what [offer name] gives you."
- Brief offer overview (2-3 minutes, not a full pitch)
- CTA: "Click the link below to learn more"
- This is a soft bridge, not a hard pitch -- the training must stand alone as valuable

**Post-Training Follow-Up**
- Auto-triggered email sequence (3-5 emails):
  - Email 1 (immediate): Replay link + key takeaway summary
  - Email 2 (Day 1): Additional insight that complements the training
  - Email 3 (Day 3): Testimonial from someone who applied the training's concepts
  - Email 4 (Day 5): Bridge to offer with social proof
  - Email 5 (Day 7): Direct offer presentation with deadline (if applicable)

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Training is 30-60 minutes (shorter lacks depth, longer loses attention)
- [ ] Hook captures attention in the first 60 seconds
- [ ] Each teaching point delivers a genuinely useful insight (not just teasers)
- [ ] Training stands alone as valuable even if the viewer never buys
- [ ] Bridge to offer is natural and under 5 minutes
- [ ] Follow-up email sequence is included in the deliverable
- [ ] Slide notes or outline is production-ready
- [ ] Written in the creator's brand voice and teaching style

## Landing Page Copy Framework (Universal)

Every lead magnet needs a landing page. This copy framework applies to all 9 types.

### Structure

**Above the Fold:**
- Headline: Lead magnet title (using the title formula)
- Subheadline: "Learn how to [outcome] in [timeframe] -- even if [objection]"
- Hero image: Mockup of the lead magnet (PDF cover, quiz screenshot, etc.)
- CTA Button: "Get the Free [Format]" or "Take the Quiz" or "Watch the Training"
- Supporting text: "Join [X]+ [audience members] who have already downloaded this"

**Below the Fold:**
- "Here's what you'll learn:" (3-5 benefit bullets)
- Brief creator credibility line
- 1-2 testimonials from people who used the lead magnet
- FAQ (2-3 questions addressing objections to giving email)
- Second CTA button

### Bullet Point Formula
Each bullet should use the "Feature > So That > Which Means" structure:
- "Discover the 3-step framework for [topic] so that you can [immediate benefit], which means [ultimate desire]"

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Headline matches the lead magnet title exactly
- [ ] Subheadline includes outcome, timeframe, and objection handling
- [ ] CTA button text is action-oriented (never "Submit" or "Sign Up")
- [ ] Bullets are benefit-oriented, not feature-oriented
- [ ] Social proof element is present (download count, testimonial, or authority marker)
- [ ] Page loads fast and works on mobile
