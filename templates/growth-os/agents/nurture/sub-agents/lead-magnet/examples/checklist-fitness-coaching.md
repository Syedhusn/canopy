# The 5-Step Body Recomp Checklist
## Lose Fat and Build Muscle in 40 Minutes a Day
### By Marcus Chen

**What this is:** A step-by-step checklist to set up your first week of body recomposition training and nutrition. Complete it in 15 minutes. Start seeing results by Day 7.

**What body recomposition means:** Losing fat AND building muscle at the same time. Not just "losing weight" (which usually means losing muscle along with fat and ending up smaller but still soft). Recomp means you get leaner, stronger, and more energetic simultaneously.

---

### PRE-FLIGHT: What You Need Before Starting

- [ ] Access to a gym with free weights (or a set of adjustable dumbbells at home)
- [ ] A bathroom scale (any scale works -- consistency matters more than accuracy)
- [ ] A tape measure (for waist measurement)
- [ ] A notes app or journal for tracking
- [ ] 3 hours per week available (three 40-minute sessions, scheduled in advance)

---

### STEP 1: BASELINE YOUR BODY (10 minutes)

Before you change anything, capture where you are right now. This data becomes your proof of progress.

- [ ] **Weigh yourself** first thing in the morning, after using the bathroom, before eating or drinking
      *Why: Morning weight is the most consistent. Record it. Do this daily for the next 7 days and average the number at the end of the week. The average matters -- individual days fluctuate.*

- [ ] **Measure your waist** at the navel, standing relaxed (not sucking in)
      *Why: Waist circumference is a better progress indicator than scale weight for recomposition. The scale might not move for 2-3 weeks while your waist shrinks because muscle is denser than fat.*

- [ ] **Take a front and side photo** in the same lighting, same location, same time of day
      *Why: You see yourself daily, so changes are invisible in the mirror. Photos 4 weeks apart tell the truth your mirror hides.*

- [ ] **Record your current training status:** When was your last workout? What did you do?
      *Why: Your starting point determines your starting weights and progression speed. Be honest.*

- [ ] **Record your current eating pattern:** What did you eat yesterday? No judgment -- just document.
      *Why: We're not overhauling everything on Day 1. We're making 2-3 strategic changes to what you're already doing.*

---

### STEP 2: SET UP YOUR TRAINING SCHEDULE (5 minutes)

Body recomposition requires 3 resistance training sessions per week. Not 5. Not 6. Three.

- [ ] **Choose 3 training days** and put them in your calendar right now
      *Why: If it's not scheduled, it won't happen. Treat these like meetings you can't cancel. Mon/Wed/Fri or Tue/Thu/Sat are the most common splits.*

- [ ] **Set a consistent training time** for each day
      *Why: Same time = habit formation. Morning sessions have the highest adherence rate for professionals, but any consistent time works.*

- [ ] **Identify your training location** (gym, home gym, hotel gym if traveling this week)
      *Why: Removing the "where do I go?" decision eliminates a friction point that kills consistency.*

- [ ] **Set a 40-minute timer for each session** -- when it rings, you're done
      *Why: Parkinson's Law applies to training. If you give yourself 90 minutes, you'll take 90 minutes and rest too long between sets. 40 focused minutes outperforms 90 unfocused minutes.*

---

### STEP 3: LEARN THE FOUNDATION FOUR (The Exercises That Matter)

Your first week uses only 4 exercises. These compound movements train 85% of your muscle mass.

- [ ] **Exercise 1: Trap Bar Deadlift** (or Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift if no trap bar)
      *Why: Trains your entire posterior chain -- back, glutes, hamstrings -- and has the strongest hormonal response of any exercise. This is the #1 ROI exercise for professionals. 3 sets of 5 reps.*

- [ ] **Exercise 2: Dumbbell Bench Press** (or Push-Ups if no bench available)
      *Why: Trains chest, shoulders, and triceps. The dumbbell version is safer for shoulders than the barbell version and builds more stability. 3 sets of 8 reps.*

- [ ] **Exercise 3: Dumbbell Row** (one arm at a time, knee on bench)
      *Why: Trains your entire back and biceps. Balances out the pressing from Exercise 2 and fixes the rounded posture that desk work creates. 3 sets of 8 reps per side.*

- [ ] **Exercise 4: Goblet Squat** (hold a dumbbell at your chest)
      *Why: Trains your quads, glutes, and core. The goblet position is the safest squat variation for beginners and actually teaches better squat mechanics than a barbell. 3 sets of 10 reps.*

- [ ] **Watch a form video for each exercise** (search "[exercise name] proper form" on YouTube -- pick a video from a certified trainer, not a random influencer)
      *Why: Form prevents injury and maximizes muscle activation. 5 minutes of video-watching saves weeks of pain.*

- [ ] **Start with a weight you can do 2-3 more reps than required** -- if the set calls for 8 reps, choose a weight where you could do 10-11
      *Why: The first week is about learning movement patterns, not testing your max. Starting conservative prevents soreness that kills motivation and injury that kills progress.*

---

### STEP 4: MAKE 3 NUTRITION CHANGES (Not 30)

Forget the meal plans. Forget the macros. For Week 1, make exactly three changes to your current eating:

- [ ] **Change 1: Eat 30g of protein within 1 hour of waking**
      *Why: Morning protein stabilizes blood sugar, reduces cortisol (the stress hormone that promotes belly fat), and keeps you full through lunch. Examples: 4 eggs, a protein shake, Greek yogurt with nuts.*

- [ ] **Change 2: Eat a palm-sized portion of protein at every meal**
      *Why: Most professionals under-eat protein by 40-60%. Protein preserves muscle during fat loss and has the highest thermic effect (your body burns 25-30% of protein calories just digesting it). You don't need to count grams yet -- a palm-sized portion is roughly 25-30g.*

- [ ] **Change 3: Stop eating 2 hours before bed**
      *Why: Late-night eating disrupts sleep quality, which disrupts hormone production, which disrupts body recomposition. This single change improves sleep quality within 3-4 days for most people.*

- [ ] **Keep eating everything else the same** for Week 1
      *Why: Overhauling your entire diet on Day 1 is the fastest path to quitting by Day 5. Three targeted changes create measurable results without the willpower drain of a complete diet overhaul.*

---

### STEP 5: TRACK AND ASSESS AFTER 7 DAYS

After completing your first week (3 training sessions + 3 nutrition changes), measure your results:

- [ ] **Average your daily weigh-ins** from the full week
      *Why: Day-to-day weight fluctuates by 2-4 lbs based on water, salt, and digestion. The weekly average reveals the actual trend.*

- [ ] **Re-measure your waist** at the navel, same conditions as Day 1
      *Why: Even if the scale hasn't moved, your waist measurement may have changed. This is recomposition in action -- fat lost, muscle gained, scale stays the same.*

- [ ] **Note your energy levels at 2pm** compared to before
      *Why: The protein and sleep timing changes typically improve afternoon energy within 5-7 days. If you notice a difference, that's your metabolism responding.*

- [ ] **Record your training weights** for all 4 exercises
      *Why: These become your baseline. Next week, you'll add 5 lbs to each exercise. This progressive overload is the engine of body recomposition.*

- [ ] **Ask yourself: Was this sustainable?** If yes, you're on the right track. If something felt too hard, note what -- that's data, not failure.
      *Why: The best program is the one you'll actually follow. Sustainability beats intensity every time.*

---

### COMPLETION MILESTONE

When all items above are checked, you will have:
- Baseline body composition data (weight, waist, photos)
- A 3-day training schedule locked in your calendar
- One full week of the Foundation Four workout completed
- Three strategic nutrition changes implemented
- Week 1 progress data to compare against

That's more structured progress than most people make in 3 months of "trying to get in shape."
