# Webinar & Challenge Frameworks -- Complete Playbook

> Comprehensive framework for all 6 event types. Each framework includes complete structural blueprint, section-by-section scripting guidelines, timing, psychological purpose of each element, and quality checklist.

---

## Universal Principles (All Event Types)

### The Belief Installation Sequence

Every webinar or challenge must install these beliefs in this order before the pitch:

| Belief | When Installed | How Verified |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| 1. Authority belief | Introduction (authority positioning) | Attendee stays past minute 15 |
| 2. Mechanism belief | Introduction (unique mechanism) + Teaching | Attendee types "yes" in chat during mechanism reveal |
| 3. Problem belief | Teaching Point 1 | Attendee agrees the problem is real and structural |
| 4. Solution belief | Teaching Points 2-3 | Attendee sees the framework as credible |
| 5. Proof belief | Testimonials throughout | Attendee accepts that results are real |
| 6. Self belief | Testimonials from "people like me" | Attendee believes it could work for them |
| 7. Urgency belief | Transition (crossroads close) | Attendee feels the cost of inaction |

If any belief is missing or installed out of order, conversion suffers.

### The Micro-Commitment Protocol

Micro-commitments are moments where attendees say "yes" (in chat, by nodding, or by taking an action). Research shows that after 20+ micro-commitments, the final "yes" (purchase) feels like a natural continuation rather than a new decision.

Types of micro-commitments:
- Chat engagement: "Type YES if you agree"
- Agreement questions: "Does that make sense?"
- Belief checks: "Can you see why [old way] doesn't work?"
- Value acknowledgment: "Was that helpful?"
- Identity alignment: "Are you the kind of person who [positive trait]?"
- Excitement building: "Are you starting to see what's possible?"
- Action prompts: "Screenshot this slide -- you'll want to reference it"

Target: 20+ micro-commitments before the pitch section begins.

### The Open Loop System

Open loops create unresolved information gaps that keep attendees watching. Plant 3-5 loops in the introduction, close them strategically throughout.

| Loop Type | Opening | Closing Point | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|--------------|---------|
| Story loop | "I'll tell you about the student who tried this and..." | Teaching Point 2 | Keeps attention through content |
| Bonus loop | "There's a tool I'll give you later that makes this 10x easier" | Bonus reveal in pitch | Creates anticipation for pitch section |
| Results loop | "One of today's teaching points generates 80% of my revenue" | Teaching Point 3 | Creates curiosity hierarchy |
| Secret loop | "I've never shared this publicly before..." | Content section | Creates exclusivity feeling |
| Objection loop | "If you're thinking 'this won't work for me,' hold that thought" | Teaching Point 4 or Q&A | Prevents premature exit |

## Framework 1: Live Webinar (60-120 Minutes)

### Strategic Purpose
The Live Webinar is the foundational conversion event. It compresses the entire awareness journey (Problem-Aware to Most-Aware) into a single high-intensity session. The live element creates urgency, social proof (through chat activity), and the energy of a shared experience.

### Psychological Architecture
Live webinars leverage social facilitation (people are more engaged in groups), urgency (the event is happening now), and the parasocial relationship (60-90 minutes of face time builds significant trust). The chat activity creates a bandwagon effect during the pitch.

### Complete Section-by-Section Blueprint

#### SECTION 1: INTRODUCTION (15-30 Minutes)

**1A: Opening Hook -- Pattern Interrupt (2-5 min)**

The first 60 seconds determine whether attendees stay or leave. Choose one:

*Option A: Live Proof*
- Show real results on screen (Stripe dashboard, analytics, student results)
- "Before we start, let me show you what happened this morning..."
- Works best when the proof is genuinely impressive and verifiable
- Psychological purpose: Immediate credibility shock

*Option B: Contrarian Belief*
- Challenge a widely-held assumption
- "Everything you've been told about [topic] is wrong, and in the next 60 minutes I'm going to prove it"
- Works best when the contrarian angle is defensible and surprising
- Psychological purpose: Curiosity and pattern disruption

*Option C: Story Interrupt*
- Begin a compelling story mid-action, then stop
- "At 3am last Tuesday, I got a message that changed everything. But I'll tell you what it said in a minute."
- Works best when the story has genuine emotional resonance
- Psychological purpose: Open loop that demands resolution

*Option D: Shocking Stat*
- Lead with a data point that reframes the problem
- "97% of people who try [conventional approach] fail within 6 months. I know because I was one of them."
- Works best when the stat is verifiable and relevant
- Psychological purpose: Problem awareness amplification

**1B: Authority Positioning (5-10 min)**

Layer authority in this exact order:

1. **Results Display** (2-3 min)
   - Revenue screenshots, client count, transformation timeline
   - "Come-up story": where they started vs. where they are now
   - Media features, speaking engagements, recognitions
   - Scripting: "In the last [timeframe], I've helped [X] people achieve [outcome]. Here's what that looks like..."

2. **Testimonial Block** (2-3 min)
   Three testimonials in this order:
   - Expert endorsement (respected figure vouching)
   - Dream result testimonial (aspirational outcome)
   - Initial win testimonial (quick early result -- relatable)
   - Each testimonial displayed on screen with photo, name, and result

3. **Credibility Humanizer** (1-2 min)
   - Brief vulnerability moment
   - "I didn't always know this. I failed at [thing] for [time] before figuring it out."
   - Psychological purpose: Make authority relatable, not intimidating

**1C: Sell the Unique Mechanism (5-8 min)**

Two-track argument:

*Logical Track:*
1. Name the conventional approach everyone tries
2. Explain the structural flaw in that approach (why it fails)
3. Introduce the mechanism by name
4. Explain why it works (tie to a verifiable principle)

*Emotional Track:*
1. Paint the pain of the old way (sensory detail)
2. Paint the relief of the new way (vivid future)
3. Anchor to identity: "This is for people who are serious about [identity]"

**1D: Create Commitments (3-5 min)**

Script:
```
"I'm about to share something I normally only teach inside [program name].
This alone is worth [price anchor]. But before I do, I need something from you.

If you're committed to getting [outcome] and you're ready to take notes,
type YES in the chat right now.

[Wait for chat responses]

[Read a few names] -- I see you, [Name]. [Name], let's go. [Name], welcome.

And at the end, for those of you who want my help implementing all of this,
I'll share how we can work together. But first -- the training."
```

- Count commitment responses. Target: 50%+ of attendees type YES.
- This is the most underrated section. Skipping it drops conversion by 30-50%.

**1E: Open Loops (Woven Throughout Introduction)**

Plant 3-5 loops during the introduction:
- "I'm going to show you the exact framework that generated $[X] in [timeframe]. But first..."
- "There's a bonus I'll share later that makes implementation 10x faster"
- "One of the 3 things I'm teaching today is responsible for 80% of our results. Can you guess which one?"
- "If you're thinking 'this sounds great but my situation is different,' I'll address that directly"

Track each loop and annotate where it closes in the script.

#### SECTION 2: CONTENT (30-45 Minutes, 3-5 Teaching Points)

**Teaching Point Structure (repeat for each point):**

```
CONTEXT (2-3 min):
- "The [ordinal] thing you need to understand about [topic] is..."
- Why this point matters
- What most people get wrong about this
- Connect to a specific ICP pain point from icp.md

TEACHING (5-8 min):
- Present the high-level framework or concept
- One concrete example or case study
- Show the principle in action (NOT step-by-step implementation)
- Use visual aids: diagram, matrix, or screenshot
- CRITICAL: Teach WHAT, not HOW. The webinar shows the map; the offer gives the GPS.

TESTIMONIAL (1-2 min):
- Specific testimonial that proves this teaching point works
- Format: Name, starting point, result, timeframe
- Strategically selected to crush a specific ICP objection
- Display on screen: photo, name, quote, result

MICRO-COMMITMENT (30 sec):
- "Does that make sense? Type YES in the chat."
- "Can you see how this changes things? Drop a [emoji] if you're with me."
- "Are you starting to see the picture?"
```

**Bonus Setup During Teaching:**

During content, plant seeds for bonuses:
- Teaching Point 2: "This framework takes about 3 hours to build. I created a template that does it in 15 minutes." (This becomes a bonus)
- Teaching Point 3: "The hardest part is [thing]. That's why I built a [tool]." (This becomes a bonus)

When the bonus is revealed during the pitch, attendees think "I was hoping they'd include that."

**Objection Integration:**

Map ICP objections to teaching points:

| Objection | Integration Point | Method |
|-----------|------------------|--------|
| "I don't have time" | Teaching Point 1 testimonial | Feature someone who did it with a full-time job |
| "I'm not experienced" | Teaching Point 2 testimonial | Feature a complete beginner who succeeded |
| "My situation is different" | Teaching Point 3 testimonial | Feature someone in an unusual niche |
| "I've tried other things" | Teaching Point context | Explain structural difference from competitors |

#### SECTION 3: TRANSITION (5-10 Minutes)

**Step 1: Content Recap (2 min)**
```
"Let me quickly recap what we covered:
- Point 1: [one sentence summary]
- Point 2: [one sentence summary]
- Point 3: [one sentence summary]
You now have a complete framework for [outcome]."
```

**Step 2: Rapid Yes Questions (2 min)**
```
"Quick check-in:
1. Did you learn at least one thing today that could change your [area]?
2. Can you see how [mechanism] is different from what you've tried before?
3. Do you believe [outcome] is possible for someone like you?
4. Would having a guide who's done this speed things up?
5. Are you the kind of person who takes action on what they learn?"
```

**Step 3: Crossroads Close (3-5 min)**

This is the single most important transition in the entire webinar.

```
PATH A (Inaction):
"You could take everything I taught today and implement it yourself.
Some of you will. And honestly, that's fine.
But here's what I've seen happen over and over:
[Describe the specific failure pattern of going alone]
[Paint the emotional cost: frustration, wasted months, staying stuck]
Six months from now, you're still [current painful state]."

PATH B (Action):
"Or you could decide right now that you're done figuring this out alone.
That you want [outcome] in [timeframe] instead of [longer timeframe].
That you want someone who's already [achieved result] to show you the exact path.
[Paint the emotional reward: confidence, momentum, support]"
```

**Step 4: Permission to Pitch (30 sec)**
```
"For those of you who want my help implementing everything I just taught
and more, I'd love to show you how we can work together. Is that okay?
[Wait for chat confirmation]
Awesome. Here's what I've got for you."
```

#### SECTION 4: PITCH (15-25 Minutes)

**4A: Core Offer Presentation (3-5 min)**
- Name the program
- What is included (modules, community, coaching)
- Who it is for (reinforce ICP identity)
- Who it is NOT for (exclusivity signal)
- The transformation: "You walk in as [before]. You walk out as [after]."

**4B: Price Anchor (2-3 min)**

For mid-ticket ($497-$2,000):
```
"The total value of everything I described is $[3-5x price].
But I'm not going to charge you that.
I'm not even going to charge $[2x price].
Your investment today is $[price]."
```

For high-ticket ($2,000-$10,000):
```
"This is a serious investment. I won't pretend it isn't.
But let me put it in context: [ROI calculation].
If this program helps you [achieve even a fraction of the outcome],
it pays for itself [X] times over."
```

**4C: Bonus Stack (5-10 min)**

Order: Second-best bonus first, best bonus last.

For each bonus:
1. Name it
2. Explain what it does (connect to a problem surfaced during teaching)
3. Show standalone value
4. Reveal it is included free
5. State the specific objection this bonus crushes

**4D: Guarantee (2-3 min)**
- Name the guarantee (brand it if possible)
- Explain exactly what it covers
- Frame as confidence: "I offer this because in [X] years, [Y]% of people who do the work get results"

**4E: Final CTA (2 min)**
- Clear action: "Click the link below" / "Go to [URL]"
- Restate what they get (core + bonuses)
- Restate the guarantee
- Add urgency: bonus expires, price increases, limited spots
- "I'll see you inside"

#### SECTION 5: Q&A (30+ Minutes)

**Scripted Objection Destruction (first 15-20 min):**

Script 6+ objections from icp.md:

For each:
```
"I know some of you are thinking: '[Objection]'
[Validate]: 'I completely understand. [Name] thought the exact same thing.'
[Reframe]: 'But here's what [Name] realized...'
[Proof]: '[Testimonial from someone who had this objection and overcame it]'
[CTA]: 'If that resonates with you, [action step]. The link is [URL].'"
```

**Money objection responses:**
- "I can't afford it" -> ROI reframe + payment plan mention + cost of inaction
- "What if it doesn't work?" -> Guarantee + proof of results + risk reversal
- "I need to ask my spouse" -> "Bring them to the replay" + frame as investment conversation

**Time objection responses:**
- "I don't have time" -> Time-to-results framework + testimonial from busy person
- "When will I see results?" -> Typical timeline + "first win by [milestone]"

**Product objection responses:**
- "How is this different?" -> Mechanism differentiation + competitor comparison (without naming)
- "Will it work for my situation?" -> Diverse testimonials + customization element of offer

**Live Q&A (remaining time):**
- Take genuine audience questions
- Every answer connects back to the offer's value
- End EVERY answer with a CTA reference

**Final CTA (absolute last 60 seconds):**
```
"If you're ready to [transformation], here's your next step:
Go to [URL]. Pick your payment option. And I'll see you inside [program name].
[Urgency element]. This is your moment. [URL] -- go there now."
```

### Timing Guide

| Section | Minimum | Standard | Extended |
|---------|---------|----------|----------|
| Introduction | 12 min | 20 min | 30 min |
| Content | 25 min | 35 min | 45 min |
| Transition | 5 min | 8 min | 12 min |
| Pitch | 12 min | 18 min | 25 min |
| Q&A | 15 min | 30 min | 45+ min |

- [ ] All 4 foundation documents loaded before scripting
- [ ] Opening hook captures attention in first 60 seconds
- [ ] Authority positioning includes results, testimonials, and humanizer
- [ ] Unique mechanism presented with both logical and emotional arguments
- [ ] Commitment section included with chat engagement prompt
- [ ] 3-5 open loops planted in introduction with closure points annotated
- [ ] 3-5 teaching points each follow Context > Teaching > Testimonial > Micro-Commitment
- [ ] Teaches WHAT, not HOW
- [ ] Bonus values set up during teaching section
- [ ] 20+ micro-commitments tracked before pitch
- [ ] Crossroads close presents two clear paths with emotional weight
- [ ] Permission to pitch obtained before offer presentation
- [ ] Bonuses presented second-best first, best last
- [ ] Guarantee named and framed as confidence
- [ ] 6+ scripted objections with validate > reframe > proof > CTA

- [ ] All ICP objections tracked to where they are handled

## Framework 2: Evergreen Webinar (60-90 Minutes)

### Strategic Purpose
The Evergreen Webinar runs on autopilot as part of a sales funnel. It must feel live without relying on live-only elements (real-time chat, current events). The key adaptation: replace live urgency with deadline-funnel urgency (personalized deadlines based on when the viewer watches).

### Adaptations from Live Webinar Framework

| Element | Live Version | Evergreen Adaptation |
|---------|-------------|---------------------|
| Opening hook | "Let me show you what happened this morning" | "Let me show you what happened last month" (evergreen-safe) |
| Chat engagement | "Type YES in the chat" | "Write this down" or "Pause and think about this" |
| Social proof during event | "I see 500 of you here" | "Over [X] people have watched this training" |
| Q&A section | Live questions | Scripted FAQ section (no live Q&A) |
| Urgency | "Offer expires tonight" | "Your exclusive access expires in [X] hours/days" (deadline funnel) |
| Bonuses | "Available only today" | "Available for the next [X] hours" (personalized deadline) |

### Evergreen-Specific Scripting Rules
- No time-of-day references ("Good evening" -- could be watched at any time)
- No current event references (nothing that dates the recording)
- No "live chat" references (use internal dialogue prompts instead)
- Deadline language must be relative ("within the next 48 hours") not absolute ("by Friday")

### Quality Checklist (in addition to Live Webinar checklist)
- [ ] No time-specific or date-specific references in the script
- [ ] All chat engagement prompts replaced with internal dialogue prompts
- [ ] Deadline language is relative, not absolute
- [ ] Q&A section is fully scripted (no live question period)
- [ ] Total runtime between 60-90 minutes (shorter than live -- no live Q&A padding)
- [ ] Recording instructions included (camera angle, lighting, energy notes)

## Framework 3: 3-Day Challenge

### Strategic Purpose
The 3-Day Challenge creates deep engagement and rapid belief installation over a compressed timeframe. Each day serves a distinct strategic function, with the pitch on Day 3 after two full days of value delivery.

### Day-by-Day Architecture

**Day 1: Hook + Authority + Quick Win (60-90 min)**

| Section | Duration | Content |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Opening hook | 5 min | Pattern interrupt, challenge overview, what they will achieve by Day 3 |
| Authority positioning | 10 min | Results, testimonials, origin story |
| Teaching Point 1 | 20 min | Core concept + example + quick-win exercise |
| Teaching Point 2 | 15 min | Supporting concept + testimonial |
| Day 1 Homework | 5 min | Specific action to complete before Day 2 |
| Preview of Day 2 | 3 min | Open loop for what is coming tomorrow |

- Belief installed: "This person is credible and this approach produces results"
- Pitch element: NONE. Day 1 is 100% value.
- Homework: One action that takes 15-30 minutes and produces a tangible first result

**Day 2: Deepen + Mechanism + Belief Shift (60-90 min)**

| Section | Duration | Content |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Day 1 recap + homework review | 10 min | Celebrate homework completers, share results |
| Teaching Point 3 | 20 min | Mechanism deep-dive, why this approach is different |
| Teaching Point 4 | 20 min | Advanced application + case study |
| Community moment | 10 min | Q&A, chat engagement, member sharing |
| Day 2 Homework | 5 min | Build on Day 1 homework with deeper application |
| Preview of Day 3 | 3 min | "Tomorrow, everything comes together" |

- Belief installed: "This mechanism is structurally different and addresses why other things failed"
- Pitch element: Soft mention: "If you're enjoying this free training, wait until you see what's inside [program name]"
- Homework: Builds on Day 1 work, requires 20-30 minutes

**Day 3: Proof + Implementation + Pitch (75-120 min)**

| Section | Duration | Content |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Day 1-2 recap + homework review | 10 min | Celebrate progress, showcase results |
| Teaching Point 5 | 15 min | Final concept + heavy testimonials |
| Implementation roadmap | 10 min | Show the complete path from where they are to full transformation |
| Transition (Crossroads Close) | 8 min | Two paths: DIY vs. guided implementation |
| Pitch | 15 min | Full offer presentation with bonus stack |
| Q&A / Objection handling | 20-30 min | Scripted objections + live questions |
| Final CTA | 5 min | Deadline, last call, action step |

- Belief installed: "I need structured support to implement the full system, and now is the time"
- Pitch element: Full pitch with bonuses, guarantee, and urgency

### Challenge Email/SMS Support Sequence

| Timing | Channel | Message |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Day 0 (registration) | Email | Challenge welcome, expectations, access details |
| Day 1 morning | Email + SMS | "Day 1 starts in 1 hour" with link |
| Day 1 evening | Email | Homework reminder + Day 1 replay |
| Day 2 morning | Email + SMS | "Day 2 in 1 hour" + Day 1 recap |
| Day 2 evening | Email | Homework reminder + Day 2 replay |
| Day 3 morning | Email + SMS | "Final day! Don't miss this." |
| Day 3 evening | Email | Replay + offer details + deadline |
| Day 4 | Email | "Doors closing" + final testimonial + CTA |
| Day 5 | Email | "Last chance" -- deadline enforcement |

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Day 1 is 100% value (no pitch, no offer mention)
- [ ] Each day installs a distinct belief
- [ ] Homework assignments build on each other progressively
- [ ] Day 2 includes a soft offer mention (not ignored until Day 3)
- [ ] Day 3 crossroads close is fully scripted
- [ ] Supporting email/SMS sequence is included
- [ ] Challenge completion rate target: 50%+

## Framework 4: 5-Day Challenge

### Strategic Purpose
The 5-Day Challenge maximizes trust-building through extended engagement. The longer format allows for deeper teaching, more belief installation, community building between participants, and a more gradual transition to the pitch.

### Day-by-Day Architecture

| Day | Title | Duration | Belief Installed | Pitch Element |
|-----|-------|----------|------------------|---------------|
| 1 | Foundation | 60-75 min | Authority + Problem belief | None |
| 2 | Framework | 60-75 min | Mechanism belief (positive) | None |
| 3 | Application | 60-75 min | Evidence belief | Soft mention |
| 4 | Advanced | 45-60 min | Self belief | Clear bridge + offer preview |
| 5 | Mastery + Close | 75-120 min | Urgency belief | Full pitch + Q&A |

**Day 1: Foundation ("Why Everything You've Tried Has Failed")**
- Heavy authority positioning
- Teaching Points 1-2: Name the structural problem
- Homework: Diagnostic exercise (assess their current state)
- Community prompt: "Share your diagnostic results in the challenge chat"
- Belief: "The problem is structural, not personal. There is a better way."

**Day 2: Framework ("The [Mechanism Name] Approach")**
- Introduce the complete framework overview
- Teaching Points 3-4: Core methodology components
- Homework: Apply one component of the framework
- Community prompt: "Share your first attempt"
- Belief: "This framework addresses the structural problem in a way others don't."

**Day 3: Application ("See It Work")**
- Live demonstration or detailed case study walkthrough
- Teaching Point 5: Watch the framework applied in real-time
- Homework: Apply the framework to their own situation
- Community prompt: "Share your results"
- Soft pitch: "You're doing in 3 days what [program] systematizes over 12 weeks"
- Belief: "This works. I've seen the evidence."

**Day 4: Advanced ("Going Beyond the Basics")**
- Advanced applications and edge cases
- Q&A-style format addressing common challenges from Days 1-3
- Address the top 3 objections from icp.md
- Homework: Refine and improve Day 3 work
- Bridge: "What you've built is Module 1. Here's what Modules 2-8 cover..."
- Belief: "I'm capable of doing this, and there's more to learn."

**Day 5: Mastery + Close ("Your Path Forward")**
- Recap of Days 1-4 progress
- Final teaching point: The implementation roadmap
- Crossroads close (full script)
- Complete pitch with bonus stack, guarantee, and deadline
- Scripted Q&A with objection handling
- Final CTA
- Belief: "Now is the time. The opportunity cost of waiting is too high."

### Community Integration (5-Day Challenge Specific)

5-day challenges benefit from a dedicated challenge community space:

| Day | Community Element | Purpose |
|-----|------------------|---------|
| Pre-Day 1 | Introduction thread | Build connections before challenge starts |
| Day 1-5 | Daily homework sharing thread | Accountability and peer learning |
| Day 2-4 | Buddy system pairing | Personal accountability partner |
| Day 3-5 | Daily wins celebration | Social proof from peers |
| Post-challenge | "What's next" discussion | Maintain momentum |

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Days 1-2 are 100% value
- [ ] Day 3 soft mention is natural, not forced
- [ ] Day 4 bridges to the offer through advanced content revelation
- [ ] Day 5 includes full crossroads close and pitch
- [ ] Community integration plan is included
- [ ] Supporting email/SMS sequence covers all 5 days + 2 post-challenge emails
- [ ] Each day has specific homework with sharing prompt
- [ ] Day-over-day retention target: 65%+

## Framework 5: 7-Day Challenge

### Strategic Purpose
The 7-Day Challenge creates the deepest engagement and highest commitment. It is best suited for high-ticket offers ($2,000-$25,000) where the prospect needs extensive trust-building. The extended format allows for community formation, peer accountability, and implementation-level engagement.

### Day-by-Day Architecture

| Day | Theme | Focus | Pitch Level |
|-----|-------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Foundation | Problem + authority + quick win | None |
| 2 | Framework | Mechanism introduction | None |
| 3 | Application | Framework in action + first results | None |
| 4 | Depth | Advanced concepts + community deep-dive | Soft seed |
| 5 | Breakthrough | Overcoming obstacles + diverse proof | Bridge to offer |
| 6 | Implementation | Workshop-style hands-on session | Offer preview + FAQ |
| 7 | Mastery + Close | Final teaching + full pitch + Q&A | Full pitch |

### Key Differences from 5-Day

1. **Days 1-3 are completely pitch-free.** The extended format earns more trust before any commercial signal.
2. **Day 4 is a community-focused day.** Guest expert, member panel, or collaborative exercise. This builds peer bonds that increase completion.
3. **Day 6 is a workshop.** Participants work on their actual implementation with creator guidance. This creates the IKEA effect (they value what they build) and reveals the complexity gap.
4. **Day 7 pitch is more expansive.** The longer relationship justifies a longer pitch section with more bonus reveals and deeper objection handling.

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Days 1-3 are completely free of any offer mention
- [ ] Day 4 includes a community-building element (not just teaching)
- [ ] Day 6 is implementation-focused (participants do real work)
- [ ] Total daily time commitment does not exceed 60 minutes for participants
- [ ] Community space is active with daily interaction from the creator
- [ ] Day 7 pitch includes a "challenge alumni" bonus exclusive to completers
- [ ] Post-challenge follow-up extends for 7 additional days (total 14-day experience)

## Framework 6: Workshop (2-4 Hours)

### Strategic Purpose
The Workshop combines intensive teaching with guided implementation. Unlike webinars (which teach the WHAT), workshops teach the WHAT AND guide the HOW -- participants leave with actual work product, not just knowledge. The pitch comes at the end, positioned as "continue this implementation with ongoing support."

### Psychological Architecture
Workshops leverage the sunk cost effect (participants who invest 2-4 hours are highly committed), the implementation gap (they see how hard DIY is), and the IKEA effect (they value their work product and want to complete it). The pitch is positioned as "you've started building this -- let me help you finish."

### Workshop Structure

| Section | Duration | Content |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Opening + Context | 15-20 min | Hook, authority, workshop objectives, what they will build |
| Teaching Block 1 | 30-40 min | Concept + demonstration + guided practice |
| Implementation Sprint 1 | 20-30 min | Participants work on their own, creator available for Q&A |
| Share + Feedback | 15-20 min | 3-5 participants share, creator gives feedback |
| Teaching Block 2 | 30-40 min | Next concept + demonstration + guided practice |
| Implementation Sprint 2 | 20-30 min | Participants continue building |
| Share + Feedback | 15-20 min | More sharing, more feedback |
| Recap + Implementation Roadmap | 10-15 min | What they built, what remains, the full path |
| Pitch | 15-20 min | "Continue building with support inside [program]" |
| Q&A | 20-30 min | Scripted objections + live questions |

### Workshop-Specific Scripting Guidelines

**Implementation Sprint Instructions:**
```
"For the next 20 minutes, I want you to [specific task].
Use the framework I just showed you.
Don't worry about perfection -- we'll refine it in the share session.

Here's what to do:
Step 1: [Action]
Step 2: [Action]
Step 3: [Action]

I'll be right here answering questions.
Put 'DONE' in the chat when you finish.
If you get stuck, type 'HELP' and I'll come to you.

Your timer starts... now."
```

**Share Session Script:**
```
"Time's up. Who wants to share what they built?
[Take 3-5 volunteers]
[For each]: 'Tell us what you created and what was hardest.'
[Creator gives specific, genuine feedback]
[End with]: 'Look at what you built in 20 minutes.
Imagine what you could build with 12 weeks of guided support.'"
```

### Workshop Pitch Positioning

The workshop pitch is uniquely powerful because participants have just experienced the value AND the difficulty of implementation. The pitch is:

"You've just built [deliverable]. In [X] hours, you accomplished what most people spend weeks struggling with alone. But here's the truth: [deliverable] is Module 1 of an [X]-module system. The remaining modules are [list]. Inside [program name], we build all of them together, with my step-by-step guidance, templates that eliminate the guesswork, and a community of people building alongside you."

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Workshop produces a tangible deliverable participants can use immediately
- [ ] Implementation sprints have clear instructions and time limits

## Universal Post-Event Protocol

Regardless of event type, the following happens after every event:

| Timing | Action | Channel |
|--------|--------|---------|
| 0-2 hours | Thank-you email + replay (if applicable) | Email |
| 2-6 hours | SMS reminder of offer/deadline | SMS |
| 24 hours | "Did you watch the replay?" + offer reminder | Email |
| 48 hours | Testimonial + objection handling | Email |
| 72 hours | "Last chance" or "Doors closing" | Email + SMS |
| 96 hours | Final email: deadline enforcement or waitlist | Email |
| 7 days | Non-buyer survey: "What held you back?" | Email |

### Quality Checklist for Post-Event
- [ ] Thank-you email sent within 2 hours of event end
- [ ] Replay available within 4 hours (if applicable)
- [ ] Deadline is enforced (no silent extensions)
- [ ] Non-buyer survey sent to gather objection intelligence
- [ ] All post-event emails match the event's brand voice and energy
