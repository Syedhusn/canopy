# Foundations Shared Knowledge Base

> Shared domain knowledge for all Foundations sub-agents. This document contains the conceptual frameworks, mental models, and validated principles that govern every decision made during the Foundations phase. Both the Market Research & ICP sub-agent and the Offer Architecture sub-agent must internalize this knowledge before producing any artifact.

---

## Domain 1: The Impact Distribution Principle

The single most important principle in the entire Growth OS. It determines where effort should be allocated and where diagnostic attention should be directed when results underperform.

### The Formula

```
Results = 40% Audience Quality + 40% Offer Strength + 20% Copy/Messaging
```

### What This Means in Practice

| Component | Impact | Where Creators Actually Spend Time | The Gap |
|-----------|--------|-----------------------------------|---------|
| Audience quality (right person, right pain, right awareness level) | 40% of results | ~10% of their time | 30% underinvested |
| Offer strength (right transformation, right price, right guarantee) | 40% of results | ~10% of their time | 30% underinvested |
| Copy and messaging (words, hooks, subject lines, scripts) | 20% of results | ~80% of their time | 60% overinvested |

### The Diagnostic Rule

When a campaign, launch, or funnel underperforms:

1. **First:** Audit the audience. Is the ICP correct? Are we reaching the right people? Is the awareness level diagnosis accurate?
2. **Second:** Audit the offer. Is the transformation clear? Is the price anchored correctly? Does the guarantee address the real objection?
3. **Third -- and only third:** Audit the copy. Are we using the buyer's language? Is the hook strong enough? Is the belief stack installed?

### The Most Common Error

80% of a creator's time goes to copy (20% of impact) while they ignore audience and offer (80% of impact combined). This is why creators who rewrite their sales page five times see no improvement -- the page was never the problem. The audience or the offer was.

### The Hard Rule

**Never optimize copy before the audience is validated and the offer is proven.** A strong offer to the right audience converts with mediocre copy. Great copy to the wrong audience or with a weak offer always loses. Always. Without exception.

## Domain 2: The Awareness Spectrum

Every individual in a market sits at one of five awareness levels. This classification determines the length, tone, proof density, and belief-building strategy of every piece of content, every ad, every email, and every sales page that targets them.

### The Five Levels

| Level | Name | % of Market | Copy Length Required | Belief Building Strategy | What They Need to Hear |
|-------|------|-------------|---------------------|-------------------------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Unaware** | ~5% | Very long | Indirect, heavy social proof, story-driven | They do not know they have a problem. You must show them the problem before you can offer a solution. |
| 2 | **Problem Aware** | ~15% | Long | Rational, education-heavy | They know something is wrong but do not know solutions exist. Validate the pain, name the problem, introduce the category. |
| 3 | **Solution Aware** | ~30% | Medium | Evidence-based, comparison-ready | They know solutions exist and are evaluating options. Differentiate on mechanism, not features. |
| 4 | **Product Aware** | ~40% | Medium-short | Direct proof, testimonials, specifics | They know your product/creator exists. They need proof it works, urgency to act, and the right offer. |
| 5 | **Most Aware** | ~10% | Short | Just present the offer | They are ready to buy. They trust the creator. Just make the offer clear and frictionless. |

### Strategic Implications

- **Cold traffic ads** target Levels 1-3 and must be longer, more educational, more proof-heavy.
- **Retargeting** targets Levels 3-4 and can be shorter, more direct, more offer-focused.
- **Email sequences** move people UP the awareness levels over time (2 --> 3 --> 4 --> 5).
- **Sales pages** must be designed for a specific awareness level -- a page that tries to serve all five levels serves none well.
- **The ICP's dominant awareness level** determines the default content strategy for the entire business.

## Domain 3: Market Maturity Stages

Markets evolve through four stages, and the stage determines everything: messaging strategy, proof requirements, pricing power, and differentiation intensity. Misdiagnosing the market stage is one of the most expensive errors in the foundations phase.

### The Four Stages

| Stage | Name | Market Characteristics | Messaging Strategy | Pricing Power | Proof Requirements |
|-------|------|----------------------|-------------------|---------------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Naive** | Few competitors, low search volume, no established language, audience does not know solutions exist | Direct promises: "Do X, get Y" | Low-mid (market is price-sensitive because value is unproven) | Minimal -- the promise itself is enough because no one has broken promises yet |
| 2 | **Aware** | Growing search volume, 5-15 competitors, emerging category language, audience knows options exist | Mechanism-driven: "Do X *this way* to get Y" | Mid-tier (can charge for differentiated approach) | Moderate -- need to show why your way is different/better |
| 3 | **Skeptical** | High search volume, 15+ competitors, audience has tried and failed, trust is eroded | Mechanism + identity + heavy proof: "The reason X hasn't worked is Z. Here's proof Y works differently." | Mid-high (premium for proven results) | Heavy -- mechanism must be proven, not just claimed. Testimonials, case studies, before/after required. |
| 4 | **Exhausted** | Declining engagement, cynicism dominant, "I've tried everything" mentality, audience actively resists marketing | Full paradigm shift: "Everything you've been told about X is wrong. Here's an entirely new category." | Premium (only those willing to pay for a paradigm shift remain) | Maximum -- must demolish old paradigm AND prove new one simultaneously |

### Diagnosis Method

To diagnose market maturity, assess:
- Number of competitors actively advertising
- Search volume trends (growing, stable, declining)
- Audience sentiment (hopeful, cautious, cynical, exhausted)
- Average price points (rising = early stages, compressing = late stages)
- Content saturation (how much free content exists on the topic)

### Stage Transition Indicators

Markets move from 1 --> 2 when competitors enter. From 2 --> 3 when audiences start failing with existing solutions. From 3 --> 4 when cynicism becomes the default. Markets rarely move backward, though individual sub-niches can be at different stages.

## Domain 4: The Limiting Belief Triad

Every ICP has a dominant limiting belief that prevents them from taking action. This is not a minor psychological note -- it is the single most strategically important diagnosis in the entire foundations phase. The limiting belief determines mechanism design, guarantee structure, proof strategy, and offer architecture.

### The Three Beliefs

| Belief | Core Fear | Internal Narrative | Evidence Patterns to Look For |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|------------------------------|
| **WORTHLESS** | "I'm not good enough to succeed" | "People like me don't make it. I'm not smart enough, experienced enough, connected enough. Who am I to charge for this?" | Comparison language, imposter syndrome references, "people like me don't...", self-deprecation, envy of others' success, status anxiety |
| **HELPLESS** | "I don't know how and can't figure it out" | "There's too much information. I don't know where to start. Every time I try to learn, I get more confused. I need someone to just tell me what to do." | Overwhelm language, "there's too much info," analysis paralysis, information hoarding, course-jumping, "I don't know where to start," asking the same question in multiple places |
| **HOPELESS** | "Nothing will work for me specifically" | "I've tried everything. My situation is different. Nothing works for people like me. I'm the exception to every rule." | Past failure references, uniqueness claims, "I've tried everything," cynicism toward all solutions, "my situation is different," skepticism even toward free advice |

### Belief --> Strategy Mapping

| Dominant Belief | Mechanism Focus | Proof Strategy | Guarantee Type |
|----------------|-----------------|---------------|----------------|
| Worthless | **Status mechanisms** -- identity shift, authority positioning, "become the person who..." | Social proof, transformation stories, "people just like you" testimonials, identity-shift case studies | Performance guarantee: "If you don't [identity shift result] in [time], [remedy]" |
| Helpless | **Process mechanisms** -- step-by-step systems, decision elimination, "just follow this..." | Step-by-step methodology proof, simplicity demonstrations, "here's exactly what to do" breakdowns | Completion guarantee: "Follow the system for [time]. If you don't get [result], [remedy]" |
| Hopeless | **Safety mechanisms** -- risk reversal, uniqueness accommodation, "this works even if..." | Uniqueness proof, "why this is different" explanations, proof from people who failed at everything else | ROI/reversal guarantee: "If [mechanism] doesn't produce [result] for your specific situation, [remedy]" |

### Diagnosis Requirements

A limiting belief diagnosis is NOT a guess. It requires 3-5 distinct evidence patterns from the ICP's actual language. One quote is not enough. The pattern must be observable across multiple sources (Reddit, YouTube comments, reviews, forums).

## Domain 5: Market Hierarchy

The Market Hierarchy defines the diagnostic order for any business problem. It is the master framework that governs all other frameworks.

```
Market State --> Audience --> Offer --> Copy
```

### How to Use It

When anything in a creator business is underperforming, diagnose in this exact order:

1. **Market State:** Is there a viable market? Is it growing, stable, or contracting? What maturity stage is it in?
2. **Audience:** Are we targeting the right person? Is the ICP accurate? Is the awareness level correct?
3. **Offer:** Is the transformation clear? Is the price right? Does the guarantee address the real objection? Do the economics work?
4. **Copy:** Are we using the right words? Is the hook compelling? Is the belief stack installed correctly?

**Never diagnose or fix a downstream element when an upstream element is broken.** You cannot fix bad copy when the audience is wrong. You cannot fix a weak offer when the market does not exist. Always start at the top.

## Domain 6: The Evidence Hierarchy

Not all evidence is equal. What people DO outweighs what people SAY. This hierarchy determines the weight assigned to any finding during research and ICP construction.

### The Six Ranks

| Rank | Evidence Type | Description | Example | Reliability |
|------|-------------|-------------|---------|-------------|
| 1 | **Paid** | Money exchanged for a solution | Course purchases, tool subscriptions, coaching payments | Highest -- money is the ultimate signal of value |
| 2 | **Spent** | Significant time/effort invested seeking a solution | Long-form content consumption, detailed forum posts, attending free workshops | Very high -- time is the second-most valuable resource |
| 3 | **Said Unprompted** | Voluntarily expressed pain, desire, or opinion | Reddit rants, YouTube comments, unsolicited reviews, DM conversations | High -- unprompted = genuine |
| 4 | **Searched** | Actively looked for answers or solutions | Google search volume, autocomplete queries, "how to" searches | Medium-high -- intent is present but passive |
| 5 | **Engaged** | Interacted with related content | Likes, shares, saves, comments on posts | Medium -- engagement does not equal intent to buy |
| 6 | **Surveyed** | Responded when directly asked | Poll responses, survey data, direct questions | Lowest -- people lie in surveys, especially about willingness to pay |

### Application Rule

Higher-ranked evidence overrides lower-ranked evidence when they conflict. If survey data says people will pay $2,000 for a course but no one in the market is actually paying more than $500 (Paid evidence), the Paid evidence wins.

## Domain 7: The Offer Value Equation

Every offer's perceived value is determined by a four-variable equation. This is not metaphorical -- it is the literal framework used to design, evaluate, and optimize every offer in the Growth OS.

### The Formula

```
                  Dream Outcome  x  Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
Perceived Value = --------------------------------------------------------
                     Time Delay  x  Effort & Sacrifice Required
```

### How to Maximize Value

| Variable | To Increase Value... | Tactical Levers |
|----------|---------------------|-----------------|
| **Dream Outcome** (numerator) | Make the outcome bigger, more specific, more desirable | Name a specific result. Quantify it. Connect it to identity, not just utility. |
| **Perceived Likelihood** (numerator) | Make the buyer believe this will actually work for them | Stronger proof, better guarantee, mechanism clarity, "people like me" testimonials |
| **Time Delay** (denominator) | Reduce the time between purchase and first result | Quick wins, momentum loops, 72-hour first-result design, fast-action bonuses |
| **Effort & Sacrifice** (denominator) | Reduce the work the buyer must do | Done-for-you elements, templates, automation, decision elimination, step reduction |

### The Key Insight

Most creators try to increase value by making the dream outcome bigger ("make more money! be more successful!"). But the fastest way to increase perceived value is to reduce the denominator -- make it faster and easier. A $5,000 course that promises $100K in revenue is less compelling than a $5,000 course that promises $50K in revenue within 90 days with a 3-step system. The second offer has a smaller dream outcome but a dramatically lower denominator.

## Domain 8: Sequential Dependency

The mandatory build order for the Foundations phase. This is not a suggestion. It is a hard dependency chain where each step consumes the output of the previous step. Skipping or reordering creates cascading errors that corrupt every downstream artifact.

### The Chain

```
Market Research --> ICP --> Positioning --> Offer --> Brand Voice
     (1)            (2)       (3)           (4)         (5)
```

### Why Each Step Depends on the Previous

| Step | Consumes | Produces | Why It Cannot Be Skipped |
|------|----------|----------|--------------------------|
| 1. Market Research | Creator context, external data | Viability score, market maturity, competitive landscape, demand signals | Without research, the ICP is built on assumptions. Everything downstream inherits those assumptions. |
| 2. ICP | Market Research findings | 13-section audience profile, limiting belief diagnosis, awareness level, pain architecture, language patterns | Without a validated ICP, positioning has no target. The mechanism addresses no one's specific belief. |
| 3. Positioning | ICP + Market Research | Old Vehicle Autopsy, mechanism design, Core Belief Statement, narrative architecture | Without positioning, the offer is a collection of features with no strategic differentiation. It competes on price. |
| 4. Offer | Positioning + ICP + Market Research | Offer tiers, value stack, guarantee, economics validation, belief stack | Without a structured offer, there is nothing to sell. Brand voice is extracted to serve the offer. |
| 5. Brand Voice | All prior artifacts + creator content samples | Voice profile, tone spectrum, anti-patterns, language level, example rewrites | Without brand voice, all downstream copy sounds generic. The creator's audience does not recognize it as authentic. |

### What Happens When Steps Are Skipped

| Skipped Step | Cascading Error |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Market Research | ICP built on assumptions --> positioning addresses wrong beliefs --> offer priced incorrectly --> brand voice misaligned with market |
| ICP | Positioning has no target --> mechanism addresses generic pain --> offer does not map to real objections --> copy uses wrong language |
| Positioning | Offer has no differentiation --> competes on price --> economics fail --> cannot sustain acquisition costs |
| Offer | No transformation to sell --> brand voice has no commercial anchor --> marketing produces content with no conversion path |
| Brand Voice | All downstream copy sounds like a different person --> audience trust erodes --> conversion drops regardless of offer quality |

### The Exception

Creators who already have an existing business with paying customers may enter at Step 2 (using their existing data as market research) or Step 3 (if they have validated audience data). However, this must be explicitly validated -- an existing business is NOT automatically a validated market. The Foundations Director verifies this before allowing a skip.

## Cross-Domain Integration

These eight domains are not independent. They interact as a system:

1. **Impact Distribution** tells you WHERE to focus (audience and offer before copy).
2. **Market Hierarchy** tells you the diagnostic ORDER (market --> audience --> offer --> copy).
3. **Market Maturity** tells you HOW the market currently behaves (and what messaging strategy to use).
4. **Awareness Spectrum** tells you WHERE the individual sits (and how to reach them).
5. **Limiting Belief Triad** tells you WHAT blocks the individual (and what mechanism to build).
6. **Evidence Hierarchy** tells you HOW to validate any claim (and what evidence to trust).
7. **Value Equation** tells you HOW to design the offer (maximize numerator, minimize denominator).
8. **Sequential Dependency** tells you the ORDER to build everything (and why skipping breaks the chain).

Every sub-agent must understand all eight domains. Every artifact must be consistent across all eight domains. A positioning document that contradicts the market maturity diagnosis, or an offer that ignores the limiting belief, fails the cross-document coherence check regardless of its individual quality.
