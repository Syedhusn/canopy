# Launch Planning Frameworks

> Complete frameworks for all five launch types. Each framework includes strategy, day-by-day timeline, email schedule, content plan, milestone targets, and contingency protocols.

---

## Framework 1: Live Launch

### Strategy Overview

The Live Launch is the highest-revenue, highest-coordination launch type. It creates a real event with real energy, real deadlines, and real scarcity. The emotional arc follows: curiosity > education > desire > urgency > decision.

**When to use:** Proven offer, engaged audience of 1,000+ email subscribers or 5,000+ social followers, creator can commit 4-6 weeks of focused execution.

**Revenue expectation:** 60-80% of total revenue arrives in the final 72 hours. This is structural, not a sign of failure. The pre-launch and early window build pressure; the close converts it.

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks -4 to -3)

**Objective:** Ensure all assets are complete and the audience is warming.

| Day | Actions | Owner | Success Metric |
|-----|---------|-------|---------------|
| -28 | Confirm all sales assets complete (VSL, funnel pages, emails) | Launch Planner | Asset checklist 100% |
| -27 | Set up payment processing, CRM automations, calendar booking | Operations | All systems tested and live |
| -26 | Build waitlist/interest capture landing page | Funnel Architect | Page live, tracking confirmed |
| -25 | Segment warm leads for DM outreach during launch | Sales Team | Segment list created |
| -24 | Begin audience warming: increase content frequency by 50% | Content Manager | Content calendar updated |
| -23 | Share behind-the-scenes teasers (working on something new) | Creator | 3+ teaser posts published |
| -22 | Collect and organize social proof: testimonials, case studies, results | Case Study Writer | Proof portfolio updated |
| -21 | Review ICP objection map -- ensure launch content addresses top 5 | Launch Planner | Objection-content map created |

### Phase 2: Runway (Weeks -2 to -1)

**Objective:** Build anticipation, install beliefs, and activate urgency.

| Day | Actions | Owner | Success Metric |
|-----|---------|-------|---------------|
| -14 | Announcement sequence: reveal what is coming across all platforms | Creator + Marketing | Announcement reach > 50% of audience |
| -13 | Email 1 (pre-launch): "Something big is coming" -- teaser with waitlist CTA | Email Strategist | Open rate > 30% |
| -12 | Content: Address ICP objection #1 through educational post | Content Manager | Published on 2+ platforms |
| -11 | Story sequence: Behind-the-scenes of offer creation | Story Producer | 5+ stories posted |
| -10 | Email 2 (pre-launch): "Here's why I built this" -- origin story + waitlist | Email Strategist | Waitlist additions tracked |
| -9 | Content: Address ICP objection #2 through social proof/case study | Content Manager | Published with testimonial |
| -8 | Live video/webinar: Free training that demonstrates mechanism | Creator | Registrations > 200 or 10% of list |
| -7 | Email 3 (pre-launch): "What you'll get" -- offer peek + waitlist | Email Strategist | Waitlist at 3x+ target units |
| -6 | Content: Address ICP objection #3 through FAQ-style content | Content Manager | Published with engagement CTA |
| -5 | Story sequence: Countdown activation (5 days until doors open) | Story Producer | Daily countdown begins |
| -4 | Email 4 (pre-launch): Early bird/VIP access announcement | Email Strategist | Segment engaged list for early access |
| -3 | Content: "Who this is for and who this is NOT for" -- filtering post | Content Manager | High engagement (comments/saves) |
| -2 | Technical dry run: Test every link, automation, payment flow | Operations | All systems verified |
| -1 | Email 5 (pre-launch): "Doors open tomorrow" -- final anticipation | Email Strategist | Open rate > 35% |

### Phase 3: Launch Window (Days 1-7 for 7-day window)

| Day | Email | Social | Ads | DMs | Daily Target |
|-----|-------|--------|-----|-----|-------------|
| 1 | "It's here" -- Announcement + offer overview + CTA | 5 stories + 1 feed post + go live | Launch retarget campaign | Top 50-100 warm leads | 15-25% of total target |
| 2 | "The transformation" -- Client story + specific results | 3 stories (testimonial focus) + 1 reel | Scale winning ad set | Follow up Day 1 non-responders | Cumulative 25-35% |
| 3 | "Your questions answered" -- FAQ + objection handling | 5 stories (FAQ) + 1 feed post | Add cold audience campaign | Re-engage email clickers | Cumulative 35-45% |
| 4 | "Why this works" -- Mechanism deep-dive + proof | 3 stories + 1 reel (mechanism demo) | Optimize by creative performance | New warm leads identified | Cumulative 45-55% |
| 5 | "Inside look" -- What's included walkthrough | 5 stories (program walkthrough) + live event | Increase spend on winners | Final outreach to fence-sitters | Cumulative 55-65% |
| 6 | "48 hours left" -- Deadline reminder + best testimonial | Countdown stories + urgency reel | Final push budget increase | Last chance DMs | Cumulative 65-75% |
| 7 | "Final call" -- Last chance + fast-action bonus (AM) + "Doors closing" (PM) | Non-stop stories + final live | All budget on retarget | Emergency outreach | 100% of target |

### Phase 4: Close Sequence (Final 48 Hours Detail)

| Hour | Email | Social | Action |
|------|-------|--------|--------|
| T-48 | "48 hours left" -- Deadline + strongest testimonial | Story: countdown timer graphic | Increase ad retarget budget 50% |

| Day | Action | Owner |
|-----|--------|-------|

## Framework 2: Evergreen Launch

### Strategy Overview

An automated funnel that runs continuously, triggered by individual opt-ins. Mimics the urgency of a Live Launch through deadline funnels with unique per-lead countdowns.

**When to use:** Offer is proven (validated through at least one Live or Beta Launch), paid traffic budget is available, creator wants passive revenue.

**Revenue expectation:** Steady daily conversions, no dramatic spikes. Typical: 1-5 sales per day depending on traffic volume and price point.

### System Architecture

```
Traffic Source (Ads/Content/Referrals)
    │
    ▼
Lead Magnet / Webinar Registration (Opt-in Page)
    │
    ▼
Thank You Page + Content Delivery
    │
    ▼
Automated Email Sequence (5-7 emails over 5-7 days)
    │
    ▼
Evergreen Webinar / VSL (Pre-recorded)
    │
    ▼
Sales Page with Deadline Funnel (48-72hr unique timer)
    │
    ▼
Checkout Page
    │
    ▼
Retargeting Ads (non-converters)
    │
    ▼
Follow-up Sequence (post-deadline)
```

### Setup Timeline (Weeks 1-3)

| Week | Actions | Owner |
|------|---------|-------|
| 1 | Record evergreen webinar or VSL. Build opt-in page and thank you page. | Creator + Funnel Architect |
| 1 | Write 7-email nurture-to-pitch sequence. | Email Strategist |
| 2 | Build sales page with deadline funnel integration. Set up payment processing. | Funnel Architect + Operations |
| 2 | Create 3-5 ad creatives for cold traffic. Set up retargeting pixel. | Ad Creative Director |
| 3 | Test entire funnel end-to-end (3 test leads through complete journey). | Operations |
| 3 | Launch ads at $50-100/day test budget. Monitor for 7 days before scaling. | Marketing Manager |

### Email Sequence Architecture

| Email | Day | Subject Angle | Purpose | CTA |
|-------|-----|--------------|---------|-----|
| 1 | 0 | Welcome + lead magnet delivery | Deliver value, set expectations | Consume the lead magnet |
| 2 | 1 | Story: "How I discovered this" | Build creator credibility + mechanism intro | Watch webinar/VSL |
| 3 | 2 | Teaching: Core framework or insight | Demonstrate expertise + install beliefs | Watch webinar/VSL |
| 4 | 3 | Social proof: Client transformation | Install "it works for people like me" belief | Watch webinar/VSL |
| 5 | 4 | The offer: Full pitch + value stack | Present the offer clearly | Sales page (deadline starts) |
| 6 | 5 | Objection handling: Top 3 objections | Remove purchase barriers | Sales page |
| 7 | 6 | Deadline: "Your access expires tomorrow" | Create urgency to decide | Sales page (deadline closing) |

### Optimization Cadence

| Frequency | Action | Metric to Watch |
|-----------|--------|----------------|
| Daily | Monitor ad spend, CPA, and ROAS | CPA below target threshold |
| Weekly | Review email sequence performance (per-email metrics) | Open rate, click rate, unsubscribe rate |
| Bi-weekly | Refresh ad creatives (creative fatigue sets in at 14-21 days) | CTR declining below 1% |
| Monthly | Full funnel conversion audit (stage-by-stage) | Overall funnel conversion rate |
| Quarterly | Major refresh: new webinar angle, updated testimonials, price review | Revenue per lead trending |

## Framework 3: Rolling Launch

### Strategy Overview

Continuous enrollment with periodic cohort starts, combining the always-on nature of Evergreen with the community energy of Live.

**When to use:** Cohort-based programs, group coaching, or memberships with structured onboarding.

**Revenue expectation:** Predictable monthly baseline with peaks around cohort close dates.

### Cohort Cycle (6-Week Example)

| Week | Phase | Activities |
|------|-------|-----------|
| 1-2 | Open Enrollment | Accept applications/registrations. Content highlights current cohort results. |
| 3 | Early Bird Close | Early pricing deadline. Email push to registered-not-enrolled. |
| 4 | Final Enrollment | Final push. Scarcity: "Cohort starts next week, X spots remain." |
| 5-6 | Cohort Active | Deliver program. Collect testimonials. Begin marketing next cohort. |

### Continuous Content Calendar

| Week of Cycle | Content Theme | Purpose |
|--------------|--------------|---------|
| 1 | "Inside the program" -- current cohort updates | Social proof generation |
| 2 | Educational content using the mechanism | Belief installation |
| 3 | Case studies from recent graduates | "People like me" proof |
| 4 | "Who this is for" + FAQ content | Objection handling |
| 5 | Behind-the-scenes of cohort delivery | Trust building |
| 6 | Results recap + "next cohort starts soon" | Anticipation building |

### Email Cadence

| Trigger | Email | Purpose |
|---------|-------|---------|
| Application submitted | Welcome + expectations | Set the frame |
| 7 days after application | "Still thinking about it?" + FAQ | Address hesitation |
| Early bird deadline -3 days | "Early pricing ends Friday" | Create deadline urgency |
| Early bird deadline | "Last chance for early pricing" | Final early push |
| Final enrollment -7 days | "Cohort starts in 7 days" | Countdown begins |
| Final enrollment -3 days | "3 spots remaining" (if real) | Scarcity activation |
| Final enrollment | "Doors are closing tonight" | Final push |
| Post-close | "Cohort is full -- join the waitlist for next round" | Waitlist for next cycle |

## Framework 4: Flash Sale

### Strategy Overview

A short, intense promotion for existing customers or warm audiences. Price-driven, not value-driven. Maximum 2 per year to avoid training the audience to wait for discounts.

**When to use:** Existing customer list of 500+, seasonal moment (Black Friday, New Year), or clearing inventory of a retiring product.

**Revenue expectation:** 90%+ of revenue within the 24-72 hour window. Often the highest per-day revenue of any launch type.

### Timeline (72-Hour Flash Sale)

**Prep Phase (Days -14 to -1)**

| Day | Action | Owner |
|-----|--------|-------|
| -14 | Select offer and discount structure. Set revenue target. | Launch Planner |
| -10 | Update sales page with flash sale pricing and countdown timer | Funnel Architect |
| -7 | Write 5-email flash sale sequence | Email Strategist |
| -5 | Create 3 ad creatives (retarget only -- no cold traffic for flash sales) | Ad Creative Director |
| -3 | Schedule all emails, social posts, and ad campaigns | Marketing Coordinator |
| -1 | Technical test: discount codes, payment links, automation triggers | Operations |

**Execution Phase (72 Hours)**

| Hour | Email | Social | Target |
|------|-------|--------|--------|
| 0 | "Flash Sale LIVE: [X]% off for 72 hours only" | Feed post + 5 stories: announcement | 20% of target in first 12 hours |
| 12 | -- | Stories: "Already X people grabbed this" | -- |
| 24 | "Still time: 48 hours left" + best testimonial | Feed post: social proof + deal | 40% of target by hour 24 |
| 36 | -- | Stories: FAQ + objection handling | -- |
| 48 | "24 hours left" + fast-action bonus | Reel: urgent CTA | 65% of target by hour 48 |
| 60 | "12 hours left -- this won't happen again" | Stories: countdown | -- |
| 66 | "6 hours left" -- final email | Stories: real-time countdown | 85% of target by hour 66 |
| 71 | "1 hour left" -- absolute final push | Story: last chance | 100% target |
| 72 | "It's over" -- door closed + non-buyer nurture | Story: "Flash sale is closed" | Tally results |

### Flash Sale Pricing Guidelines

| Original Price | Recommended Discount | Rationale |
|---------------|---------------------|-----------|
| Under $100 | 30-50% | Low-ticket needs significant discount to motivate impulse |
| $100-$500 | 20-30% | Mid-ticket responds to meaningful but not desperate discounts |
| $500-$2,000 | 10-20% or bonus stack | High-ticket buyers respond more to added value than price cuts |
| Over $2,000 | Bonus stack only (no discount) | Discounting premium offers damages positioning |

## Framework 5: Beta Launch

### Strategy Overview

Early access pricing with a structured feedback loop. The primary goal is learning and testimonial generation, not maximum revenue. Beta participants become the proof portfolio for the full launch.

**When to use:** New offer needing validation, first-ever offer, or significant offer redesign.

**Revenue expectation:** Modest. Beta pricing is typically 40-60% below full price in exchange for feedback commitment.

### Timeline (3-Week Runway + 7-Day Window)

**Pre-Launch (Weeks -3 to -1)**

| Day | Action | Owner |
|-----|--------|-------|
| -21 | Define beta offer: what's included, beta price, feedback requirements | Launch Planner |
| -18 | Build simple sales page (does not need to be polished -- "beta" framing) | Funnel Architect |
| -15 | Announce beta opportunity to engaged audience (not full list) | Creator |
| -12 | Email 1: "I'm building something new and I need your help" | Email Strategist |
| -10 | Content: Share the problem you're solving and why existing solutions fail | Content Manager |
| -7 | Email 2: "Here's what the beta includes and what I need from you" | Email Strategist |
| -5 | 1-on-1 DMs to ideal beta candidates (most engaged followers) | Creator |
| -3 | Email 3: "Beta spots are limited to [10-25] people -- here's why" | Email Strategist |
| -1 | Final prep: payment link, onboarding sequence, feedback forms ready | Operations |

**Launch Window (Days 1-7)**

| Day | Action | Target |
|-----|--------|--------|
| 1 | Cart open email + social announcement. DM all pre-qualified leads. | 40-50% of spots filled |
| 2 | Follow-up: "Here's who's already in" (social proof from Day 1 sign-ups) | -- |
| 3 | "Why I'm running a beta and what makes this different" -- content | 60-70% filled |
| 4 | DM follow-up to interested-not-enrolled leads | -- |
| 5 | "Last few spots" -- if genuinely close to capacity | 80-90% filled |
| 6 | "Closing enrollment tomorrow" -- final push | -- |
| 7 | "Beta is closed" -- cart close + thank you to enrollees | 100% filled |

**Post-Beta (Ongoing)**

| Milestone | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Week 1 | Kickoff call + structured feedback form #1 |
| Week 2 | Mid-program check-in + feedback form #2 |
| Week 4 | Results check-in + testimonial interview |
| Program end | Final feedback form + case study interview |
| +2 weeks | Graduates invited to refer friends for next cohort |

### Beta Feedback Collection Framework

| Checkpoint | Questions to Ask |
|-----------|-----------------|
| After onboarding | "What was confusing? What was missing? How was the first experience?" |
| After first milestone | "What clicked? What did not? Would you recommend this so far?" |
| At midpoint | "Are you getting the results you expected? What would you change?" |
| At completion | "What was the biggest transformation? Would you pay full price? What was missing?" |
| 30 days post | "Looking back, what was the most valuable part? Can we share your story?" |

## Cross-Launch Frameworks

### The Belief Installation Timeline (Applies to All Launch Types)

Every launch must install these beliefs in this order during the pre-launch phase:

| Belief | Install During | Method |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| 1. "This problem is real and costly" | Weeks -4 to -3 | Pain agitation content, statistics, personal stories |
| 2. "A solution exists" | Weeks -3 to -2 | Educational content demonstrating the framework |
| 3. "This mechanism is different" | Weeks -2 to -1 | Mechanism demonstration, "why other approaches fail" |
| 4. "This creator is credible" | Weeks -2 to -1 | Social proof cascade, credentials, case studies |
| 5. "I can do this" | Week -1 | Relatable case studies, "people like me" stories |
| 6. "Now is the time" | Week -1 to Day 1 | Urgency cues, opportunity cost, scarcity elements |

If any belief is missing from the pre-launch plan, the launch will underperform. Belief 5 is the most commonly skipped and the most important for high-ticket offers.

### The Urgency Architecture (Applies to All Timed Launches)

All urgency elements must be genuine. The framework:

| Urgency Element | Implementation | Verification |
|----------------|---------------|-------------|
| Cart deadline | Hard close date, no extensions | Payment links disabled at deadline |
| Bonus expiration | Specific bonuses removed at specific times | Bonus access revoked after deadline |
| Limited spots | Actual enrollment cap | Enrollment disabled at cap |
| Price increase | Price goes up after deadline | New price implemented |
| Cohort start | Program begins on specific date | Late enrollees miss content |

**Rule:** Never use urgency you cannot or will not enforce. Extending a deadline or "reopening" a cart after announcing closure permanently damages launch credibility.

### The DM Outreach Protocol (Applies to All Launch Types)

DM outreach consistently outperforms passive content for conversion. The protocol:

| Phase | Segment | Message Template |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| Pre-launch | Engaged followers (liked/commented 3+ times in 30 days) | "Hey [name], I noticed you've been engaging with my content about [topic]. I'm building something that might be exactly what you need. Want me to send you the details when it's ready?" |
| Cart open | Waitlist members | "Hey [name], just wanted to let you know [offer] is officially open. Here's the link: [URL]. Any questions, I'm here." |
| Mid-launch | Email clickers who did not purchase | "Hey [name], I saw you checked out [offer]. Was there something holding you back? Happy to answer any questions." |
| Final push | All engaged-not-purchased | "Hey [name], just a heads up -- [offer] closes in [X hours]. If you've been thinking about it, now's the time. What questions do you have?" |

### The Post-Launch Nurture Pivot (Applies to All Launch Types)

After every launch close, non-buyers must be handled with care:

| Timing | Email | Purpose |
|--------|-------|---------|
| +1 day | "Thank you for being here" -- value-first, no pitch | Preserve relationship |
| +3 days | Educational content (no sell) | Rebuild trust |
| +7 days | "Here's what our new members are experiencing" | Soft social proof |
| +14 days | Return to regular nurture sequence | Normal content cadence |
| +30 days | "We're opening doors again soon" (if applicable) | Warm for next launch |

**Rule:** Never make non-buyers feel punished for not purchasing. They are future buyers if treated well, and lost forever if treated poorly.
