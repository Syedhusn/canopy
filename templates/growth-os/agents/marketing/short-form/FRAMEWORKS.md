# Short-Form Video Frameworks & Playbooks

> Complete structural blueprints for all 9 short-form video types. Each framework produces scripts ready for the creator to film.

---

## The Viral Short-Form Formula

Every high-performing short-form video follows this master structure, adapted per type:

```
Negative Hook --> Alternative Option(s) --> Promise --> Solution(s) --> Call to Action
```

**Why this works:** Negative content extracts more emotion than positive content. Starting with a bold negative statement creates a villain, positions the creator as the hero, and compresses a full problem-to-solution arc into 20-60 seconds.

## Framework 1: Storytelling Reel

### Purpose
Emotional connection and relatability. The viewer should think "that's exactly my experience."

### Structural Blueprint

```
HOOK (0-3 sec)
[VISUAL: Close-up face, serious expression, direct eye contact]
[TEXT OVERLAY: Bold statement about the struggle/tension]
"[Tension-creating opening line that starts mid-story]"

Example hooks:
- "Nobody tells you this part about [building a business / getting in shape / etc.]"
- "I almost quit [thing] last [timeframe]. Here's what happened."
- "The moment I realized [painful truth]..."

SETUP (3-10 sec)
[VISUAL: Shift to wider shot or B-roll of relatable scenario]
Brief context -- paint the "before" scene in 2-3 sentences.
Use ICP language patterns verbatim from reference/icp.md.

CONFLICT (10-25 sec)
[VISUAL: Shift -- screen recording, text on screen, or dramatic B-roll]
The moment of tension, failure, or realization.
This is the emotional peak. Lean into it.
"And then [the thing that changed/broke/clicked]..."

RESOLUTION (25-40 sec)
[VISUAL: Return to face, slightly lighter expression]
The insight, shift, or lesson. Not the full framework -- just the principle.
"What I learned was [one clear insight]."
Connect to the mechanism subtly if appropriate.

CTA (40-50 sec)
[VISUAL: Direct to camera, friendly]
[TEXT OVERLAY: CTA text]
"Follow if you want more [category of content]."
```

**Length:** 30-60 seconds | **Beats:** 5-6 | **Words:** 80-130

### Platform Adjustments
- **Instagram:** Polish the visuals, use trending audio if relevant, focus on saves
- **TikTok:** Keep it raw, lean into the emotion, comment-bait the resolution
- **YouTube Shorts:** Make it educational-leaning, bridge to a long-form video

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Hook creates immediate emotional tension
- [ ] Story mirrors ICP's current reality
- [ ] Resolution delivers genuine insight (not platitude)
- [ ] Brand voice maintained in compressed format
- [ ] Visual shifts at each beat transition
- [ ] Text overlay on first frame

## Framework 2: Lifestyle Reel

### Purpose
Create aspiration. The viewer should think "I want that life" and associate the lifestyle with the creator's mechanism/offer.

### Structural Blueprint

```
HOOK (0-3 sec)
[VISUAL: Aspirational moment -- morning routine, workspace, travel, results]
[TEXT OVERLAY: One-line identity statement or lifestyle marker]
No spoken words -- let the visual hook. Music carries this one.

MONTAGE (3-15 sec)
[VISUAL: 3-5 quick cuts showing different aspects of the aspirational life]
Each clip: 2-3 seconds max.
Diversity of scenes: work, play, environment, relationships.
[MUSIC: Trending or aspirational audio track]

MECHANISM WHISPER (15-20 sec)
[VISUAL: Brief talking-head or text card]
One sentence connecting the lifestyle to the mechanism.
"This is what happens when you [mechanism principle]."
OR text overlay only: "[Mechanism principle] changed everything."

IDENTITY CLOSE (20-25 sec)
[VISUAL: Final aspirational shot]
[TEXT OVERLAY: Identity statement -- "Built different." / "This is the [mechanism] life."]
OR brief voice: "And you can build this too."

CTA (final 3-5 sec)
[TEXT OVERLAY: "Follow for more"]
```

**Length:** 15-30 seconds | **Beats:** 3-4 | **Words:** 15-40 (mostly visual)

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Visuals are genuinely aspirational (not staged or inauthentic)
- [ ] Music/audio matches the mood
- [ ] Mechanism connection is subtle (1 sentence max)
- [ ] Identity markers match ICP's desired identity from icp.md
- [ ] No hard selling -- pure aspiration

## Framework 3: Lead Magnet Reel

### Purpose
Drive opt-ins to a specific lead magnet. The reel must create enough desire for the resource that the viewer takes action.

### Structural Blueprint

```
HOOK (0-3 sec)
[VISUAL: Face to camera, urgent/excited expression]
[TEXT OVERLAY: "FREE [resource type]" or problem statement]
"If you're struggling with [specific pain], I made something for you."

PAIN AGITATION (3-10 sec)
[VISUAL: Talking head or text cards showing pain points]
"You've probably tried [common approach] and [common approach]..."
"And you're still stuck at [specific stuck point]."
Validate their frustration. Use ICP language.

SOLUTION INTRO (10-20 sec)
[VISUAL: Screen recording showing the lead magnet, or mockup image]
"So I created [Lead Magnet Name] -- a [format] that [specific promise]."
"Inside you'll find:"

VALUE PREVIEW (20-35 sec)
[VISUAL: Quick flashes of the resource OR text cards for each point]
"1. [Specific thing they'll learn/get]"
"2. [Specific thing]"
"3. [Specific thing]"
Highlight the most tangible, immediate-result element.

CTA (35-45 sec)
[VISUAL: Direct to camera, clear instruction]
[TEXT OVERLAY: "Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll send it to you" OR "Link in bio"]
"Comment [keyword] below and I'll DM it to you right now."
OR "Tap the link in my bio -- it's completely free."
```

**Length:** 20-45 seconds | **Beats:** 5 | **Words:** 70-110

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Lead magnet promise is specific and tangible
- [ ] Pain agitation uses exact ICP language
- [ ] Value preview shows 3 specific deliverables (not vague promises)
- [ ] CTA is crystal clear (one action, one step)
- [ ] Comment keyword is simple and memorable

## Framework 4: Hook + Value

### Purpose
Deliver one actionable insight that builds authority. This is the workhorse reel type -- high volume, consistent value, steady follower growth.

### Structural Blueprint

```
HOOK (0-3 sec)
[VISUAL: Face to camera, confident expression]
[TEXT OVERLAY: Bold claim or the insight itself]
Pattern: "[Negative/Contrarian statement]. Here's what to do instead."
OR: "[X] [thing] that [surprising outcome]. Number [X] is the game-changer."
OR: "The [thing] that [result] isn't what you think."

CONTEXT (3-8 sec)
[VISUAL: Same shot or slight angle change]
Why this matters. What the viewer loses by not knowing this.
"Most [ICP role] get this wrong because [common misconception]."

THE INSIGHT (8-25 sec)
[VISUAL: Talking head + text overlays summarizing key points]
The actual value. One clear, actionable insight.
Be specific: include numbers, steps, or a framework.
"Instead, do [this]. Here's why: [reason]."
"Step 1: [specific]. Step 2: [specific]. Step 3: [specific]."

PROOF/EXAMPLE (25-35 sec, optional)
[VISUAL: Screen recording, result, or quick example]
One quick proof point: "When I did this, [specific result]."
OR: "My client [name/description] used this and [result]."

CTA (35-45 sec)
[VISUAL: Direct to camera]
[TEXT OVERLAY: "Save this" or "Follow for more [topic]"]
"Save this for later -- you're going to need it."
OR: "Follow me for more [category] tips."
```

**Length:** 15-45 seconds | **Beats:** 4-5 | **Words:** 50-110

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Hook makes a specific, bold claim
- [ ] Insight is genuinely actionable (viewer can implement today)
- [ ] One idea only -- no cramming multiple concepts
- [ ] Includes a "save trigger" (something so specific it demands saving)
- [ ] Proof point is specific (not "it works great")

## Framework 5: Behind The Scenes

### Purpose
Build parasocial connection through authenticity. The viewer should feel like they are getting exclusive access to the creator's real life/process.

### Structural Blueprint

```
HOOK (0-3 sec)
[VISUAL: Raw, unpolished moment -- messy desk, mid-process, casual setting]
[TEXT OVERLAY: "You never see this part" or "POV: [real moment]"]
"Nobody shows you this, but [real thing happening]..."

RAW MOMENT (3-15 sec)
[VISUAL: Actual behind-the-scenes footage, minimal editing]
Show the real thing: content creation process, workspace tour, client call prep, morning routine reality.
Include imperfections -- they are the point.

CONTEXT (15-22 sec)
[VISUAL: Talking head, casual]
Brief explanation of what they are seeing and why it matters.
"This is what [X hours of content creation / running a business / coaching] actually looks like."

PERSONALITY MOMENT (22-28 sec)
[VISUAL: Candid moment]
Humor, self-deprecation, or genuine reaction.
This is the most shareable moment -- design for screenshots.

CTA (28-30 sec)
[TEXT OVERLAY: "Follow for more BTS"]
```

**Length:** 15-30 seconds | **Beats:** 4 | **Words:** 30-70

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Footage feels genuinely unpolished (not "styled casual")
- [ ] Includes a human/funny/real moment
- [ ] Brief enough to not overstay its welcome
- [ ] Personality shines through

## Framework 6: Quick Tips

### Purpose
Pure value delivery. Designed for maximum save rate. Each tip should be immediately actionable.

### Structural Blueprint

```
HOOK (0-3 sec)
[VISUAL: Face to camera, energetic]
[TEXT OVERLAY: "[X] [things] about [topic] you need to know"]
"[X] [things] I wish I knew about [topic] sooner."
OR: "Quick tips for [ICP role] that actually work."

TIP 1 (3-8 sec)
[VISUAL: Text card or talking head with text overlay]
[TEXT OVERLAY: "1. [Tip name]"]
"First: [Concise tip with specific detail]."

TIP 2 (8-14 sec)
[VISUAL: New angle or text card]
[TEXT OVERLAY: "2. [Tip name]"]
"Second: [Concise tip]."

TIP 3 (14-22 sec)
[VISUAL: New angle or text card]
[TEXT OVERLAY: "3. [Tip name]"]
"Third -- and this is the big one: [Best tip saved for last]."

CLOSE (22-30 sec)
[VISUAL: Direct to camera]
[TEXT OVERLAY: "Save this"]
"Save this -- you'll need it. Follow for more."
```

**Length:** 15-45 seconds | **Beats:** 5-7 | **Words:** 50-100

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Each tip is specific enough to act on immediately
- [ ] Best tip saved for last (retention pull)
- [ ] Tips build on each other or cover different aspects
- [ ] Save trigger explicit ("save this for later")
- [ ] Text overlays clearly label each tip number

## Framework 7: Transformation

### Purpose
Social proof through visible change. Create desire by showing what is possible.

### Structural Blueprint

```
HOOK (0-3 sec)
[VISUAL: "After" state -- result, lifestyle, or metric]
[TEXT OVERLAY: Specific result headline]
"[X] months ago I/they [was/were] [before state]. Now [after state]."

BEFORE STATE (3-10 sec)
[VISUAL: Relatable "before" imagery -- old photos, lower lifestyle, struggle footage]
[TEXT OVERLAY: "Before [Mechanism Name]"]
Paint the before picture. Use ICP language for the struggles.

THE SHIFT (10-18 sec)
[VISUAL: Transition effect or montage showing the change process]
"Then [what changed -- mechanism reference]."
Brief, not detailed. The mystery is the point.

AFTER STATE (18-28 sec)
[VISUAL: Current state -- results, lifestyle, confidence]
[TEXT OVERLAY: Specific metrics or outcomes]
Show the transformation with specifics.
"Now: [specific result], [specific result], [specific result]."

CTA (28-35 sec)
[VISUAL: Direct to camera or text card]
"Link in bio if you want to see how."
OR: "DM me [keyword] if this is what you want."
```

**Length:** 15-45 seconds | **Beats:** 5 | **Words:** 40-80

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Before/after contrast is stark and specific
- [ ] Results are measurable (not vague "changed my life")
- [ ] Mechanism is mentioned but not explained (creates curiosity)
- [ ] CTA drives to profile or offer

## Framework 8: Testimonial

### Purpose
Third-party social proof. Strongest when the testimonial-giver mirrors the ICP's current state.

### Structural Blueprint

```
HOOK (0-3 sec)
[VISUAL: Client/student face or result screenshot]
[TEXT OVERLAY: Headline result -- "[Client] went from [X] to [Y]"]
"[Client name]: '[Specific result quote].'"

BEFORE STATE (3-10 sec)
[VISUAL: Client sharing or creator narrating over footage]
"[Client] came to me [situation]. They were [struggling with ICP pain]."
Mirror the ICP's current reality through the client's story.

WHAT THEY DID (10-20 sec)
[VISUAL: Client sharing or process footage]
"They [applied mechanism / joined offer / implemented system]."
Brief mention of the approach -- not a tutorial, just credibility.

RESULTS (20-35 sec)
[VISUAL: Result screenshots, client celebration, metrics]
[TEXT OVERLAY: Key numbers]
"In [timeframe]: [specific result], [specific result], [specific result]."
Include both tangible and intangible results.

CTA (35-45 sec)
[VISUAL: Creator to camera or text card]
"Link in bio to [start your transformation / apply / learn more]."
```

**Length:** 20-45 seconds | **Beats:** 5 | **Words:** 60-100

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Client mirrors the ICP demographic
- [ ] Results are specific and measurable
- [ ] Before state matches ICP's current reality
- [ ] Mechanism/offer credited (not just generic "hard work")
- [ ] Permission obtained for testimonial use

## Framework 9: Split Screen

### Purpose
React to content, challenge ideas, or present contrasts. High engagement through the duet/reaction format.

### Structural Blueprint

```
HOOK (0-3 sec)
[VISUAL: Split screen -- original content on one side, creator reaction on other]
[TEXT OVERLAY: Creator's take -- "This is wrong" or "Let me explain why"]
Creator's immediate reaction -- facial expression first, then words.

REACTION/COMMENTARY (3-20 sec)
[VISUAL: Split screen continues]
"Okay so [person] is saying [their point]..."
"Here's the thing nobody mentions: [creator's contrarian insight]."
Break down why the original content is incomplete, wrong, or needs context.

KEY INSIGHT (20-35 sec)
[VISUAL: May shift to full-screen creator]
"What actually works is [alternative approach]."
Deliver the corrected or enhanced perspective.
Connect to the mechanism or positioning if natural.

CTA (35-45 sec)
[VISUAL: Full screen creator]
"Follow for more [hot takes / real talk / truth about X]."
```

**Length:** 15-45 seconds | **Beats:** 4 | **Words:** 50-100

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Original content is recognizable and relevant to ICP
- [ ] Creator adds genuine value beyond disagreement
- [ ] Key insight is specific and actionable
- [ ] Tone is confident, not petty or dismissive
- [ ] Reaction feels authentic, not scripted
