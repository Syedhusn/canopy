# LinkedIn Frameworks & Playbooks

> Complete structural blueprints for all 6 LinkedIn post types. Each framework produces publish-ready posts with strategic context.

---

## LinkedIn Writing Principles

1. **The first 2 lines are everything.** ~210 characters before the "see more" fold. If these two lines do not compel the click, the post is dead.
2. **Line breaks are oxygen.** One sentence, then a line break. Two sentences maximum per paragraph. LinkedIn is read on mobile.
3. **First person singular.** "I" not "we." Personal posts outperform company posts 3:1 on LinkedIn.
4. **Specific numbers beat vague claims.** "$47K" beats "significant revenue." "3 months" beats "recently."
5. **Professional does not mean corporate.** LinkedIn rewards authenticity, vulnerability, and strong opinions -- delivered with professional framing.
6. **End with genuine curiosity.** "What's your experience with this?" beats "Agree?" or "Thoughts?"
7. **Three hashtags maximum.** #Industry #Subtopic #BrandedHashtag. That is it.
8. **No emoji bullets.** Professional tone allows occasional contextual emoji, but emoji-as-formatting signals amateur LinkedIn.

## Framework 1: Authority Post

### Purpose
Establish domain expertise. Designed to be saved, shared, and referenced. Positions the creator as the expert to follow in their category.

### Structural Blueprint

```
LINE 1-2 (THE "SEE MORE" HOOK -- under 210 characters):
[Bold claim or counterintuitive insight that challenges the reader's current thinking.]
[Second line that escalates or adds specificity.]

---fold---

CONTEXT (2-3 short paragraphs):
Why this matters in the industry right now.
What most people believe vs. what is actually true.
Brief personal credibility marker (not bragging -- evidence).

FRAMEWORK/KEY POINTS (3-5 points):
[Point 1]: [One-sentence explanation]

[Point 2]: [One-sentence explanation]

[Point 3]: [One-sentence explanation]

[Point 4 (optional)]: [One-sentence explanation]

[Point 5 (optional)]: [One-sentence explanation]

PROOF:
When I applied this: [specific, measurable result].
OR: In working with [X] [clients/companies]: [pattern observed].

TAKEAWAY:
[One sentence -- what the reader should do with this information.]

CONVERSATION PROMPT:
[Genuine question that invites thoughtful responses, not "agree?"]

#Hashtag1 #Hashtag2 #Hashtag3
```

### Hook Formulas for Authority Posts
| Formula | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Pattern Recognition | "After coaching 200 founders, I see the same mistake 80% make." |
| Counterintuitive Claim | "The most successful [role] I know never [common practice]." |
| Specific Number + Insight | "One framework generated $340K in 90 days. Here's what it is." |
| Industry Challenge | "Our industry has a problem nobody wants to discuss." |

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Hook creates curiosity before the "see more" fold
- [ ] Points are formatted with line breaks between each
- [ ] Proof is specific (numbers, timeframe, scope)
- [ ] Conversation prompt is genuine and answerable
- [ ] Length: 800-1300 characters total
- [ ] Maximum 3 hashtags

## Framework 2: Story Post

### Purpose
Build personal connection through professional vulnerability. LinkedIn stories perform best when they blend personal and professional -- the reader sees both the human and the expert.

### Structural Blueprint

```
LINE 1-2 (HOOK -- moment of tension or revelation):
[Start in the middle of the story's most intense moment.]
[Add a detail that makes the reader need to know more.]

---fold---

BACKSTORY (2-3 short paragraphs):
[Who you were. Where you were. What you believed.]
[Set the professional context so the reader understands the stakes.]

THE STRUGGLE (2-3 paragraphs):
[What went wrong or felt impossibly hard.]
[Specific details -- not generalities.]
[The emotional reality of the moment.]

THE SHIFT (1-2 paragraphs):
[The conversation, event, or realization that changed your perspective.]
[What clicked. What you suddenly understood.]

THE LESSON (1 paragraph):
[The universally applicable insight extracted from the personal experience.]
[Frame it as something the reader can apply to their own professional life.]

THE INVITATION (1 line):
[Genuine question: "Has anyone else experienced this?" or "What would you have done?"]

#Hashtag1 #Hashtag2 #Hashtag3
```

### Hook Formulas for Story Posts
| Formula | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Vulnerable Admission | "I was fired from my first leadership role. Here's what they didn't tell me." |
| Timeline Contrast | "3 years ago: burnt out, broke, and questioning everything. Today: [result]." |
| "I stopped" | "I stopped chasing clients 6 months ago. Here's what happened next." |
| The Moment | "The email that changed my entire business strategy arrived at 2am on a Tuesday." |

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Story has a clear arc (setup > struggle > shift > lesson)
- [ ] Vulnerability is genuine but professionally appropriate
- [ ] Lesson is universally applicable
- [ ] Details are specific (dates, numbers, places, names when appropriate)
- [ ] Length: 1000-1500 characters

## Framework 3: Framework Post

### Purpose
Deliver a reusable mental model that readers screenshot, save, and share. Framework posts position the creator as an original thinker, not just an information regurgitator.

### Structural Blueprint

```
LINE 1-2 (HOOK):
[Framework name + bold claim about its impact.]
[Why existing approaches fall short.]

---fold---

THE PROBLEM (1-2 paragraphs):
Why the common approach to [topic] fails.
What most [ICP role] do wrong.

THE FRAMEWORK (3-5 named components):
The [Framework Name]:

Step 1: [Name]
[One-line explanation of what to do and why]

Step 2: [Name]
[One-line explanation]

Step 3: [Name]
[One-line explanation]

Step 4: [Name] (optional)
[One-line explanation]

Step 5: [Name] (optional)
[One-line explanation]

APPLICATION (1 paragraph):
How I use this: [brief personal example with result].
OR: When I applied this with [client type]: [result].

INVITATION:
Save this and try it this week.
Let me know which step was the game-changer for you.

#Hashtag1 #Hashtag2 #Hashtag3
```

### Framework Naming Rules
- Name it (gives it authority and makes it shareable)
- Use 2-3 words maximum
- Examples: "The 3-Layer Stack," "The Clarity Funnel," "The 80/20 Sprint"

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Framework has a memorable name
- [ ] Each step is actionable (not just conceptual)
- [ ] Steps are in logical order
- [ ] Personal application demonstrates it works
- [ ] Length: 800-1200 characters
- [ ] Designed to be screenshotted

## Framework 4: Contrarian Take

### Purpose
Spark discussion, establish intellectual positioning, and attract aligned audience. The best contrarian takes on LinkedIn generate hundreds of comments because they force reflection.

### Structural Blueprint

```
LINE 1-2 (HOOK -- the hot take):
[Directly challenge conventional wisdom. Be specific.]
[Add the "and here's why" tease.]

---fold---

WHY EVERYONE BELIEVES THE CONVENTIONAL THING (1-2 paragraphs):
[Name the conventional wisdom specifically.]
[Explain why it became conventional -- it's not that people are dumb.]

THE EVIDENCE IT'S WRONG (2-3 paragraphs):
[Personal experience, data, or observation.]
[Why the conventional approach fails or is incomplete.]
[What the contrarian view explains that the conventional one cannot.]

WHAT ACTUALLY WORKS (1-2 paragraphs):
[The alternative. Your positioning.]
[Brief proof or example.]

THE NUANCE (1 paragraph):
[When IS the conventional wisdom right? What context does it serve?]
[This nuance is what separates a hot take from a bad take.]

YOUR STANCE (1 line):
[Where you land and why. Clear, definitive.]

INVITATION:
[Genuine invitation to disagree or add perspective.]
"Where do you fall on this? I'm curious."

#Hashtag1 #Hashtag2 #Hashtag3
```

### Contrarian Take Generation

| Pattern | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| "The best X never Y" | "The best consultants I know never send proposals." |
| "X is overrated" | "Consistency is overrated. Here's what actually matters." |
| "We have it backwards" | "We teach lead generation backwards. Here's the fix." |
| "The real problem" | "The problem isn't [surface issue]. It's [deeper issue]." |
| "I was wrong about" | "I was wrong about [thing] for 5 years. Here's what I learned." |

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Take is defensible (not just provocative for clicks)
- [ ] Nuance section included (shows intellectual honesty)
- [ ] Evidence is specific (not just "in my experience")
- [ ] Your alternative is clearly stated
- [ ] Length: 600-1000 characters
- [ ] Invites genuine discussion, not tribalism

## Framework 5: Case Study Post

### Purpose
Demonstrate specific results with enough detail to build credibility and create desire. Case studies on LinkedIn drive the most DMs and inbound inquiries.

### Structural Blueprint

```
LINE 1-2 (HOOK -- headline result):
[Specific outcome + context that makes it impressive.]
[The "how" tease.]

---fold---

THE CONTEXT (1-2 paragraphs):
Who: [Client description -- role, industry, company size]
Situation: [Where they started -- specific, measurable]
Challenge: [What made this difficult]
"They came to me because [specific trigger that prompted action]."

THE APPROACH (2-3 paragraphs):
What we did differently: [Mechanism or methodology applied]
Key decision #1: [Choice and reasoning]
Key decision #2: [Choice and reasoning]
"The counterintuitive move was [thing that seemed risky]."

THE RESULTS (1-2 paragraphs):
"In [timeframe]:"
- [Metric 1]: [Specific number]
- [Metric 2]: [Specific number]
- [Metric 3]: [Specific number]
"But the real change: [Intangible result -- confidence, clarity, freedom]."

THE LESSON (1 paragraph):
[What others can take from this case study.]
[What it reveals about [broader topic] that applies universally.]

CTA:
"If you're in a similar situation, DM me. Happy to share what might work for you."
OR: "Anyone else seen this pattern? What's your experience?"

#Hashtag1 #Hashtag2 #Hashtag3
```

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] All numbers are specific and verifiable
- [ ] Client description matches ICP (reader sees themselves)
- [ ] Approach references the mechanism or methodology
- [ ] Results include both tangible and intangible outcomes
- [ ] CTA drives to DM or conversation
- [ ] Length: 1000-1500 characters
- [ ] Client privacy respected

## Framework 6: Engagement Post

### Purpose
Generate comments to fuel algorithmic distribution. Engagement posts are the lightest format but critical for maintaining visibility between heavier posts.

### Structural Blueprint

```
LINE 1-2 (HOOK):
[Observation or question that resonates broadly with ICP.]
[Enough context to prompt a thoughtful response.]

---fold---

PERSONAL PERSPECTIVE (1-2 paragraphs):
[Your take on the observation or question.]
[Brief enough to leave room for others' perspectives.]

INVITATION (1 line):
[Specific, easy-to-answer question.]
```

### Engagement Post Patterns

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| "What's the one..." | "What's the one piece of advice you'd give your 25-year-old professional self?" |
| "Am I the only one..." | "Am I the only one who thinks [common practice] is wildly overrated?" |
| "Two types of..." | "There are two types of [ICP role]: those who [A] and those who [B]. Which are you?" |
| "Biggest lesson from..." | "Biggest professional lesson from the last 12 months? Mine: [brief answer]." |
| "Fill in the blank" | "The most underrated skill in [industry] is ________." |
| "This or that" | "[Option A] or [Option B]? And why?" |

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Question is genuinely interesting (not performative)
- [ ] Easy to answer (low friction to comment)
- [ ] Creator shares their perspective first (models the expected response)
- [ ] Length: 300-600 characters
- [ ] Not engagement bait ("like if you agree")

## LinkedIn Content Week Template

### Recommended Weekly Rhythm

| Day | Post Type | Purpose | Target Metric |
|-----|-----------|---------|---------------|
| Monday | Framework or Authority | Start week with value | Saves, shares |
| Tuesday | Story Post | Build personal connection | Comments, profile visits |
| Wednesday | Contrarian Take | Spark discussion | Comments, quote posts |
| Thursday | Case Study | Demonstrate results | DMs, profile visits |
| Friday | Engagement Post | Light conversation, algorithm fuel | Comment volume |

### First Comment Strategy

Every post should have a pre-written first comment:
- For Authority/Framework posts: Ask a follow-up question or share an additional resource
- For Story posts: Share a related micro-story or ask what the reader's experience is
- For Contrarian posts: Acknowledge the strongest counter-argument preemptively
- For Case Study posts: Share one more data point or behind-the-scenes detail
- For Engagement posts: Answer your own question in depth

### Reply Strategy (First 60 Minutes)

- Reply to every comment within the first hour
- Ask follow-up questions (drives comment depth, which drives algorithm)
- Like and reply to every comment on the first 10
- Pin the most substantive comment to the top
- Do NOT reply with one-word answers ("Thanks!" does nothing for the algorithm)

## Document/Carousel Post Framework (Bonus)

LinkedIn document posts (carousels) are the highest-performing format for saves and shares.

### Structure
```
Slide 1: Bold hook + visual branding (title slide)
Slide 2: The problem (why this matters)
Slides 3-7: One point per slide (framework steps, insights, tips)
Slide 8: Summary or TL;DR
Slide 9: CTA + where to find more
Slide 10: About the creator (mini-bio + follow CTA)
```

### Design Rules
- 1080x1350 pixel format (portrait)
- Large font -- readable without zooming
- Consistent color scheme matching brand
- One idea per slide (never crowd a slide)
- Use the creator's face on slides 1 and 10
