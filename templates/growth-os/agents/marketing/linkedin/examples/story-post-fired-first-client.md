## POST COPY

My first paying client fired me after 6 weeks.

She told me: "I followed everything you gave me and I'm in a worse place than when I started."

She was right.

---fold---

In 2018, I was 3 months into my coaching business.

I was doing what every other trainer does.

Gave her a meal plan. Gave her a training split. Told her to stay consistent.

She was consistent. She tracked every meal. She showed up to every session.

And after 6 weeks, she had lost 4 pounds on the scale but looked the same. Her energy was destroyed. She was waking up at 3 AM every night. She was miserable.

I had done to her exactly what I now teach coaches NOT to do.

I put her in an aggressive deficit without monitoring her metabolic signals. I pushed her training volume too high without programming recovery. I treated her body like a math equation instead of a biological system.

When she fired me, I felt two things:

Shame. And clarity.

The shame passed. The clarity changed everything.

I spent the next year studying metabolic adaptation, hormonal regulation, and periodization science. Not from fitness influencers. From endocrinologists, sports scientists, and researchers.

What I learned rewrote everything I thought I knew about body transformation.

Two years later, that former client reached out. She had seen my content evolve. She wanted to try again.

This time, I started by assessing her metabolic state -- not her macros. I increased her food before I decreased it. I programmed 4 training days instead of 6. I tracked her sleep and stress alongside her body composition.

In 12 weeks, she achieved the transformation she had been chasing for 3 years.

Not because I worked her harder.

Because I finally understood that transformation is not about willpower. It is about biology.

That failure in 2018 is the foundation of everything I do today. Every framework I use, every protocol I build, every client I coach -- it all traces back to the moment I realized I was part of the problem.

If you are a coach: your worst client outcome is your best teacher.

If you are looking for a coach: ask them about their failures. The ones who have been humbled by a bad outcome and changed their approach are the ones worth trusting.

Has a professional failure ever fundamentally changed how you work? I would genuinely like to hear.
