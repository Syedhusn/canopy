# Twitter/X Frameworks & Playbooks

> Complete structural blueprints for all 7 Twitter content types. Each framework produces publish-ready tweets and threads.

---

## Twitter Writing Principles

Before any template, internalize these:

1. **One idea per tweet.** If you need a comma to add a second thought, it is a second tweet.
2. **Specificity is authority.** "$47K in 90 days" beats "significant revenue quickly."
3. **Periods, not ellipses.** End statements with conviction. Ellipses signal uncertainty.
4. **Remove every word that does not add meaning.** Read the tweet, delete a word, re-read. If it still works, keep deleting.
5. **Write for the quote tweet.** The best tweets are ones people screenshot or add their own commentary to.
6. **No hashtags.** On Twitter, hashtags signal amateur accounts.

### The 3-Layer Tweet Formula
Every strong tweet operates on three layers:
- **Surface:** Immediately understandable on first read
- **Insight:** Reveals a deeper truth on reflection
- **Action:** Implies what the reader should do differently

## Framework 1: Value Thread

### Purpose
Teach a complete concept and deliver actionable insight. The reader should walk away with something they can implement immediately.

### Structural Blueprint

**Tweet 1 -- HOOK (under 280 characters)**
Pattern options:
- Cost Revelation: "I spent $[X] on [thing]. Sharing what I learned for free:"
- Result Tease: "[Specific result] in [timeframe]. Here's the full breakdown:"
- Authority: "After [experience/credential], here's what I know for sure about [topic]:"
- Direct Challenge: "Stop [common mistake]. Here's what to do instead (thread):"

Rules:
- Must standalone as a compelling tweet even if nobody clicks "show more"
- Must contain a specific number, result, or claim
- Must create an open loop that the thread closes

**Tweet 2 -- CONTEXT**
"[Why this matters. Set the stakes.]"
"Most [ICP role] [do the common thing] and wonder why [pain persists]."
"The truth is: [brief reframe that makes the reader need the rest]."

**Tweets 3-7 -- BODY (one insight per tweet)**

Structure each body tweet:
```
[Tweet number]/

[Bold opening statement for this point.]

[1-2 sentences of explanation.]

[Specific example or proof.]
```

Rules:
- Each tweet starts with its strongest line
- Each tweet ends with a micro-hook to the next ("But that's not the hard part.")
- Include one "bookmark trigger" tweet -- so tactical the reader saves it
- Vary rhythm: short punchy tweet, then longer explanatory tweet, then short again

**Tweet 8 -- PROOF**
"[Specific result that validates the thread's claims.]"
"When I applied this: [measurable outcome]."
OR: "[X] people have used this. The pattern: [consistent result]."

**Tweet 9 -- SUMMARY**
"TL;DR:"
"1. [Key point 1]"
"2. [Key point 2]"
"3. [Key point 3]"
"[One-sentence transformation statement]"

**Tweet 10 -- CTA**
"If this was valuable:"
"1. Follow @[handle] for more threads like this"
"2. RT the first tweet to help others find it"
"3. Reply with your biggest takeaway"

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Hook creates an open loop with a specific claim
- [ ] Each body tweet delivers one standalone insight
- [ ] At least one "bookmark trigger" tweet included
- [ ] Proof tweet has specific numbers/results
- [ ] Thread length: 8-12 tweets
- [ ] CTA asks for specific actions (follow, RT, reply)

## Framework 2: Story Thread

### Purpose
Tell a narrative-driven story that extracts a universal lesson. Emotional engagement first, intellectual insight second.

### Structural Blueprint

**Tweet 1 -- HOOK**
"[Year], I [relatable low point]. Today, I [aspirational result]. Here's what happened in between:"
OR: "The story of how [unexpected event] changed everything about [topic] for me:"

**Tweet 2 -- BACKSTORY**
"It started when [relatable starting point]."
"I was [specific situation that mirrors ICP's current state]."
"Like most [ICP role], I believed [common limiting belief]."

**Tweets 3-5 -- RISING ACTION**
Build tension with each tweet:
- Tweet 3: The first attempt and its failure
- Tweet 4: The escalation -- things getting worse
- Tweet 5: The lowest point or breaking moment

Structure: Each tweet ends with a hook that pulls to the next. Use short sentences. Let the reader feel the emotion.

**Tweets 6-7 -- CLIMAX**
"Then [the thing that changed everything] happened."
"I realized: [the insight that shifted the paradigm]."

This is where the mechanism's principle appears -- not named, but demonstrated through the story.

**Tweets 8-9 -- RESOLUTION**
The journey of implementing the new insight.
Specific results and milestones.
"Within [timeframe]: [result 1], [result 2], [result 3]."

**Tweet 10 -- LESSON**
"The lesson: [Universal truth extracted from the story]."
"If you're where I was in [year], here's what I'd tell you: [actionable advice]."

**Tweet 11 -- CTA**
"This thread took me [X hours/years of experience] to write."
"If it resonated, follow @[handle] for more stories like this."
"RT the first tweet if someone you know needs to hear it."

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Story has a clear arc (setup > conflict > climax > resolution)
- [ ] Reader can see themselves in the "before" state
- [ ] Vulnerability is specific and genuine
- [ ] Lesson is universally applicable
- [ ] Thread length: 8-15 tweets
- [ ] Emotional beats clearly marked

## Framework 3: Hot Take

### Purpose
Provoke discussion, establish intellectual positioning, and attract aligned followers. The take should be contrarian but defensible.

### Structural Blueprint

**Single Tweet Format (strongest):**
```
[Common belief] is wrong.

[What actually works in 1-2 sentences.]

[Why this matters.]
```

**Mini-Thread Format (2-3 tweets):**
```
Tweet 1: [Hot take -- bold, specific, contrarian]

Tweet 2: [The evidence or reasoning that supports the take]

Tweet 3: [The nuance -- when the conventional wisdom IS right, and when it isn't]
```

### Hot Take Generation Framework

| Pattern | Formula | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| "X is actually Y" | "[Thing everyone thinks is good] is actually [damaging/wrong/overrated]." | "Posting every day is actually hurting your business." |
| "The real problem is..." | "Everyone is focused on [surface issue]. The real problem is [deeper issue]." | "Everyone is focused on funnels. The real problem is the offer." |
| "Unpopular opinion" | "Unpopular opinion: [Thing most people in industry believe is true] is [wrong/overrated/incomplete]." | "Unpopular opinion: engagement rate is a vanity metric." |
| "I stopped X and Y happened" | "I stopped [common practice] and [positive result]. Here's why." | "I stopped sending daily emails and my revenue went up 40%." |
| "The best [X] never [Y]" | "The best [role/people] I know never [common behavior]." | "The best coaches I know never post motivational quotes." |

### Rules
- Must be defensible, not just inflammatory
- Must reveal genuine insight, not just contrarianism for clicks
- The take should make the right people nod and the wrong people argue
- Never punch down. Punch at bad ideas, not at people

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Take is specific enough to agree or disagree with
- [ ] Evidence or reasoning is included (not just assertion)
- [ ] Take reflects the creator's genuine positioning
- [ ] Not mean-spirited or attacking individuals
- [ ] Under 280 characters for single-tweet format

## Framework 4: How-To Thread

### Purpose
Step-by-step tactical guide the reader can implement immediately. The most bookmarked thread type.

### Structural Blueprint

**Tweet 1 -- HOOK**
"How to [specific result] in [timeframe or steps] (even if [common objection]):"
OR: "The exact [process/system/method] I use to [result]. Step by step:"

**Tweet 2 -- CONTEXT (optional)**
"Before we start: this works for [ICP description]. If you're a [qualifier], keep reading."

**Tweets 3-8 -- STEPS**

Each step tweet:
```
Step [X]: [Action Name]

[What to do -- specific, actionable, one clear instruction]

[Why this step matters -- 1 sentence]

[Pro tip or common mistake to avoid]
```

Rules:
- Each step must be specific enough to do without additional context
- Steps build on each other logically
- Include "pro tips" that show deep expertise
- Use line breaks between each element

**Tweet 9 -- BONUS TIP**
"Bonus: [The insider tip that separates good from great]."
"Most people skip this. Don't."

**Tweet 10 -- CTA**
"Save this thread. You'll need it."
"Follow @[handle] for more playbooks like this."
"Drop a [emoji] if you're implementing this today."

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Each step is actionable without additional resources
- [ ] Steps are in logical order (can't do step 3 without step 2)
- [ ] At least one "pro tip" shows insider expertise
- [ ] Bonus tip adds unexpected extra value
- [ ] Thread length: 7-10 tweets

## Framework 5: Listicle Thread

### Purpose
Curated list that positions the creator as a knowledgeable resource. Items build in value -- best saved for last.

### Structural Blueprint

**Tweet 1 -- HOOK**
"[X] [things/lessons/mistakes/truths] about [topic] that [changed/cost/taught] me [result]:"
OR: "[X] [things] every [ICP role] should [know/avoid/do] in [year/context]:"

**Tweets 2-X -- LIST ITEMS**

Each item tweet:
```
[Number]. [Item name/title]

[1-2 sentence explanation or insight]

[Optional: brief example or proof]
```

Rules:
- Items 1-3: Good (warm up the reader)
- Items 4-6: Better (reader is committed, deliver stronger value)
- Items 7+: Best (most powerful items last -- reward for reading the full thread)
- Each item must standalone (reader might screenshot just one)

**Final Tweet -- CTA**
"Which one hit different for you? Reply with the number."
"Follow for more lists like this."
"RT the first tweet to help someone who needs it."

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Items build in value (best last)
- [ ] Each item is independently valuable (screenshot-worthy)
- [ ] No filler items -- every entry earns its place
- [ ] Thread length: 7-15 tweets
- [ ] CTA asks which item resonated (drives replies)

## Framework 6: Case Study Thread

### Purpose
Deep-dive on a specific result with enough detail to build credibility. Specificity is everything.

### Structural Blueprint

**Tweet 1 -- HOOK**
"[Headline result] in [timeframe]."
"Here's the full breakdown (numbers, strategy, and lessons):"

**Tweet 2 -- CONTEXT**
"The situation:"
"[Who]: [Description]"
"[Starting point]: [Specific metric or state]"
"[Goal]: [What they wanted to achieve]"
"[Challenge]: [What made this hard]"

**Tweets 3-5 -- THE APPROACH**
"Here's what we did:"
- Tweet 3: Strategy overview (the big idea)
- Tweet 4: Key tactical decisions (the specifics)
- Tweet 5: What was different from the conventional approach (mechanism differentiation)

**Tweets 6-7 -- THE CHALLENGES**
"It wasn't all smooth."
"[Specific challenge that came up]"
"How we solved it: [Solution]"
(Include challenges for credibility -- perfection is not believable)

**Tweets 8-9 -- THE RESULTS**
"The numbers:"
"- [Metric 1]: [Before] -> [After]"
"- [Metric 2]: [Before] -> [After]"
"- [Metric 3]: [Before] -> [After]"
"- Timeline: [Specific duration]"

"But the real win was [intangible result -- confidence, freedom, clarity]."

**Tweets 10-12 -- THE LESSONS**
"3 lessons from this case study:"
"1. [Transferable lesson with brief explanation]"
"2. [Transferable lesson]"
"3. [Transferable lesson]"

**Tweet 13 -- CTA**
"If you're in a similar situation, DM me [keyword]."
"Follow @[handle] for more breakdowns like this."

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] All numbers are specific (not "significant improvement")
- [ ] Before/after metrics clearly stated
- [ ] Challenges included for authenticity
- [ ] Lessons are transferable to the reader's situation
- [ ] Thread length: 10-15 tweets
- [ ] Client privacy respected (anonymize if needed)

## Framework 7: Daily Tweet Batch

### Purpose
Maintain timeline presence between threads. Each tweet stands alone. The batch covers multiple angles to keep the feed dynamic.

### Structural Blueprint (5-7 tweets per batch)

**Tweet A -- Insight**
"[Observation about the industry that shows pattern recognition]."
Pattern: "[Thing] is not about [surface interpretation]. It's about [deeper truth]."

**Tweet B -- Hot Take**
"[Contrarian one-liner that challenges conventional thinking]."
Pattern: "The [ICP role] who [counterintuitive behavior] always [positive outcome]."

**Tweet C -- Personal Story (1-2 sentences)**
"[Brief personal moment with a lesson embedded]."
Pattern: "[Specific moment] taught me [insight]. Changed how I [behavior]."

**Tweet D -- Engagement Question**
"[Question that invites replies from the ICP]."
Pattern: "What's the [one thing/biggest mistake/best advice] about [topic] that [qualifier]?"

**Tweet E -- Mechanism Reference**
"[Statement that subtly references the mechanism or unique approach]."
Pattern: "The difference between [ICP who succeeds] and [ICP who struggles]: [mechanism principle]."

**Tweet F -- Reframe**
"[Common belief restated from a new angle]."
Pattern: "[Thing ICP fears] is actually [reframed as positive/opportunity]."

**Tweet G -- Quotable**
"[Line designed to be screenshotted or bookmarked]."
Pattern: "[Short, punchy, wisdom-dense statement that reads like an aphorism]."

### Scheduling
- Spread across the day: 8am, 10am, 12pm, 2pm, 5pm, 7pm, 9pm
- No back-to-back tweets within 90 minutes
- Engagement question during peak hours (10am-2pm)

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Each tweet stands completely alone (no context needed)
- [ ] Mix of formats: insight, opinion, personal, engagement, mechanism
- [ ] All under 280 characters
- [ ] At least one designed for bookmarks/screenshots
- [ ] ICP language from reference/icp.md used naturally

## Thread Publishing Best Practices

### Formatting Rules
- Number tweets (1/, 2/, etc.) for threads > 5 tweets
- Use a narrative flow (not numbering) for story threads
- Start each tweet as if someone might see ONLY that tweet
- End each tweet with a micro-hook to the next
- Short "spacer" tweets between dense analytical tweets

### Engagement Amplification
- Pin the thread to your profile for 24-48 hours
- Reply to the first 10-20 responses within 30 minutes
- Quote-tweet your own thread with a different angle hook
- Post a screenshot of the best tweet to Instagram Stories
