---

### Tweet 2

First, let's kill the biggest myth:

Plateaus are not caused by lack of discipline.

If you're training 4-5 days/week and tracking macros, discipline is not your problem.

Your body is doing exactly what it's designed to do: adapt.

---

### Tweet 3

Here's what's actually happening:

After 6-10 weeks of a caloric deficit, your body downregulates.

- Metabolism slows
- NEAT drops 10-15% (you move less without noticing)
- Cortisol rises
- Hunger hormones spike

Your body thinks there's a famine. It's protecting you.

---

### Tweet 4

The 3 warning signals your body sends BEFORE the plateau fully sets in:

1. Sleep disruption (waking up at 3-4 AM)
2. Hunger spikes (ravenous after dinner)
3. Performance drops (weights feel heavier)

If you've got 2 of 3, you're 10 days from a full plateau.

---

### Tweet 5

The mistake 90% of people make:

They see the plateau forming and CUT HARDER.

Less food. More cardio. Extra sessions.

This confirms your body's fear. It conserves even harder.

You lose muscle, preserve fat, and end up with a worse metabolism than when you started.

---

### Tweet 6

The fix is counterintuitive:

Eat MORE. Train LESS. For 7-14 days.

Increase calories to maintenance (especially carbs). Reduce training volume 30%. Prioritize sleep.

Your body gets the signal: "The famine is over."

Leptin rises. Cortisol drops. Metabolism reactivates.

---

### Tweet 7

After the reset, reintroduce a MODERATE deficit.

Not the 1,800 calories you were starving on.

Your body responds to it like it's new -- because metabolically, it is.

I've seen clients break 3-month plateaus in 10-14 days using this exact approach.

---

### Tweet 8

The results from people who've used this:

- Sarah: broke a 4-month plateau, lost 22 lbs in 12 weeks
- Marcus: gained 8 lbs muscle, lost 4 inches off waist
- Jamie: achieved best body composition at 41 years old

All of them were told to "just be more consistent." None of them needed more discipline.

---

### Tweet 9

TL;DR:

1. Plateaus = metabolic adaptation, not laziness
2. Watch for: sleep disruption, hunger spikes, performance drops
3. Don't cut harder -- that's the trap
4. Strategic metabolic reset (eat more, train less, 7-14 days)
5. Re-enter a moderate deficit after the reset

Your body isn't your enemy. Stop fighting it.
