# STORY SEQUENCE: The Money Objection

**Type:** Objection Breaking
**Total Slides:** 6
**Purpose:** Dissolve the "$3,000 is too expensive" objection without being salesy
**Buyer Stage:** Product-Aware
**ICP Pain Point:** Wants transformation but hesitates on investment
**Runs on:** Week 4 of content arc (Offer Window), Day 6

---

## Slide 1 -- HOOK

**Visual:** Solid black background with white text. Clean, minimal.
**Text:**
"I need to be honest about something people DM me."
**Sticker/Element:** None
**Design Notes:** Large font, centered. The simplicity signals seriousness.
**Purpose:** Create intrigue. Signal that something real is about to be shared.

---

## Slide 2 -- NAME THE OBJECTION

**Visual:** Text card -- dark navy background, white text.
**Text:**
"A lot of you have told me:

'I want to do the program, but $3,000 is a lot of money right now.'"
**Sticker/Element:** None
**Design Notes:** The quote is in slightly larger font. Italicized if possible. Feels like a direct DM quote.
**Purpose:** Name the objection in their exact words. Show that you hear them without defensiveness.

---

## Slide 3 -- VALIDATE

**Visual:** Selfie -- creator looking directly at camera. Natural lighting. Warm expression. Casual setting (couch, kitchen, car).
**Text overlay:** "I get it. 100%."
**Sticker/Element:** None
**Design Notes:** Photo feels like a FaceTime still. Text overlay is small, bottom-center.
**Purpose:** Validate without dismissing. Remove defensiveness. Creator is on their side.

**Caption/voiceover (if video clip, 8 sec):**
"I get it. And honestly, I would have said the same thing two years ago. I used to think coaching was an expense I couldn't justify. Here's what changed my mind."

---

## Slide 4 -- REFRAME WITH STORY + MATH

**Visual:** Text card -- white background, black text. Clean.
**Text:**
"I spent $4,200 in one year on:

- A gym membership I underused ($600)
- Supplements that didn't work ($1,200)
- 3 different programs I quit ($900)
- A meal delivery service ($1,500)

Total results: I was in the same place I started."
**Sticker/Element:** None
**Design Notes:** Bullet points. Numbers are bold. The punchline ("same place I started") is separated by a line break.
**Purpose:** Reframe $3,000 against the invisible costs they are already paying. Not a guilt trip -- a genuine accounting exercise.

---

## Slide 5 -- THE SHIFT + PROOF

**Visual:** Screenshot of a client DM (with permission). The DM says something like: "I was so nervous about the investment. 6 weeks in and I've already gotten more results than 3 years of doing it alone. Best money I've ever spent."
**Text overlay:** "This is what the other side looks like."
**Sticker/Element:** Poll -- "Does this change how you think about it?"
  - Option A: "Yeah, actually"
  - Option B: "Still thinking"
**Design Notes:** DM screenshot is centered with slight rotation for casual feel. Poll is below.
**Purpose:** Third-party proof that the investment pays off. Poll creates engagement and gives data on who shifted.

---

## Slide 6 -- BRIDGE (NOT HARD SELL)

**Visual:** Selfie or short video clip (5 sec). Creator looking directly at camera, relaxed.
**Text overlay:** "No pressure. Just math."
**Sticker/Element:** None (or small DM button prompt)
**Design Notes:** Warm, casual. The "no pressure" positioning is critical -- it removes sales energy.
**Purpose:** Close without closing. The math speaks for itself. Those who shifted can tap the DM or bio link on their own.

**Caption/voiceover (if video clip):**
"If the math makes sense to you and you want to talk about it, just DM me 'blueprint.' No pitch. I'll just answer your questions."

---

## SEQUENCE STRATEGY NOTES

- **Thread to content:** This runs on Day 6 of the Week 4 launch arc, after the offer has been revealed (Day 5). It addresses the #1 objection after people have seen the price.
- **Reply handling:** Every reply gets a personal response. For "still thinking" poll respondents, do NOT follow up aggressively. Post a follow-up story the next day with a different angle (testimonial-heavy). For "yeah, actually" respondents, a subtle DM can be sent: "Noticed you're thinking about it -- any questions I can answer?"
- **Data to collect:** Poll results tell us what percentage of engaged viewers are blocked by price vs. other objections. If "still thinking" is 60%+, the next day's stories should hit a different objection angle.
- **Follow-up sequence:** Day 7 runs the Final CTA sequence with urgency + last testimonial + deadline. For those who engaged with this objection-breaking sequence but did not convert, add to a warm retargeting audience for a testimonial ad within 48 hours.
