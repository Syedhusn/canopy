# Instagram/Facebook Stories Frameworks & Playbooks

> Complete structural blueprints for all 8 story sequence types. Each framework produces production-ready slide-by-slide sequences.

---

## Story Psychology Principles

Stories operate in a different psychological space than feed content:
- **Viewer intent:** They chose to tap in. They are leaning forward, not scrolling past.
- **Expectation:** Personal, real-time, casual. Not polished, not produced.
- **Consumption pattern:** Sequential. Each slide must earn the next tap.
- **Engagement tools:** Polls, questions, sliders, and countdowns are not decorations -- they are data-collection mechanisms disguised as engagement.
- **Slide economy:** Maximum 30 words per text slide. One idea per slide. Respect the tap.

## Framework 1: Launch Sequence (5-7 Day Arc)

### Purpose
Build anticipation for an offer, event, or content drop across 5-7 days. Each day's story sequence feeds the next, creating a narrative arc that peaks at the offer reveal.

### Day-by-Day Blueprint

#### Day 1: SEED THE PROBLEM (5 slides)

**Slide 1 -- Hook**
- Visual: Solid color background with bold text
- Text: "Can I be honest about something?"
- Sticker: None
- Purpose: Create intrigue, signal something real is coming

**Slide 2 -- Pain Observation**
- Visual: Text card
- Text: "I've been watching [ICP group] struggle with [specific pain] for too long."
- Purpose: Name the problem, create resonance

**Slide 3 -- Agitation**
- Visual: Text card or personal photo
- Text: "The worst part? Most of the advice out there is making it worse."
- Purpose: Position conventional solutions as inadequate

**Slide 4 -- Poll**
- Visual: Branded background
- Text: "Be honest -- are you dealing with [pain]?"
- Sticker: Poll: "Yes, constantly" / "Not really"
- Purpose: Collect data, create investment through interaction

**Slide 5 -- Tease**
- Visual: Text card
- Text: "I've been working on something. More soon."
- Purpose: Open a loop that pulls them back tomorrow

#### Day 2: PERSONAL STORY (6 slides)

**Slide 1:** "Let me tell you about the time I [relatable struggle]..."
**Slide 2-3:** The personal story -- vulnerability, specifics, emotion
**Slide 4:** "That experience is exactly why I [do what I do now]."
**Slide 5:** Question box: "Have you ever felt like this? Tell me."
**Slide 6:** "Tomorrow I'm sharing something that would have changed everything for me back then."

#### Day 3: HINT AT SOLUTION (5 slides)

**Slide 1:** "Remember what I said yesterday?"
**Slide 2:** "I've found something that actually works for [pain]."
**Slide 3:** "It's not [common approach]. It's not [other common approach]."
**Slide 4:** Slider sticker: "How badly do you want to solve [pain]?" (1-10 scale)
**Slide 5:** "Big announcement coming in [X] days. Stay tuned."

#### Day 4: SOCIAL PROOF (7 slides)

**Slide 1:** "[Specific result number]. That's what [client type] achieved."
**Slide 2-4:** Three testimonial screenshots with brief annotation
**Slide 5:** "Notice the pattern? They all [common element that ties to mechanism]."
**Slide 6:** Poll: "Which result surprised you most?" [Option A / Option B]
**Slide 7:** "Imagine having a system that makes this predictable. Almost ready to share."

#### Day 5: THE REVEAL (8 slides)

**Slide 1:** Countdown sticker or "IT'S HERE" bold text
**Slide 2:** "Introducing [Offer Name]."
**Slide 3-4:** What it is, who it's for, what it does (high level)
**Slide 5-6:** Value stack -- 2 slides showing what's included
**Slide 7:** "Early access: [incentive] for the first [X] people."
**Slide 8:** Link sticker: "Tap here to [join / apply / learn more]"

#### Day 6: OBJECTION HANDLING (6 slides)

**Slide 1:** "I know what you're thinking..."
**Slide 2:** "[Objection #1]. Here's the truth: [reframe]."
**Slide 3:** "[Objection #2]. Let me show you why that's not the case: [proof]."
**Slide 4:** "[Objection #3]. [Client story that dissolves this objection]."
**Slide 5:** Question box: "What's holding you back? Ask me anything."
**Slide 6:** Link sticker with reminder of the deadline/incentive.

#### Day 7: FINAL CTA (5 slides)

**Slide 1:** "[X] people have already joined. Here's what they're saying: [quick testimonial]."
**Slide 2:** "This closes [tonight / tomorrow / in X hours]."
**Slide 3:** "If you're still on the fence: [strongest single proof point]."
**Slide 4:** "You can keep [doing the painful thing] or you can [take action]."
**Slide 5:** Link sticker: "Last chance. Tap to [join]." + Countdown sticker to close.

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Each day's sequence opens a loop that pulls to the next day
- [ ] Day 1-3 focus on problem and anticipation (no selling)
- [ ] Day 5 reveal is clear and exciting
- [ ] At least 1 interactive element per day
- [ ] Urgency in Day 7 is real (actual deadline or limit)

## Framework 2: Proof from Many

### Purpose
Overwhelm with volume of social proof. The sheer quantity of results creates belief that the mechanism works reliably.

### Structural Blueprint (7-10 slides)

**Slide 1 -- Bold Result Claim**
- Text: "[X]+ [people/clients/students] have [achieved result]. Here's what they're saying."
- Purpose: Volume claim creates initial curiosity

**Slides 2-4 -- Testimonial Screenshots (3 different)**
- Visual: DM screenshot or testimonial image with brief annotation
- Text: "[Name]: '[Key quote].' -- [Context: who they are and their result]"
- Purpose: Each testimonial should mirror a different ICP segment

**Slide 5 -- Pattern Recognition**
- Text: "Notice what they all have in common? [Common element tied to mechanism]."
- Purpose: Shift from individual proof to systemic proof

**Slide 6 -- Mechanism Credit**
- Text: "This isn't luck. It's [Mechanism Name]. And it works because [1-sentence principle]."
- Purpose: Connect the results to the mechanism

**Slide 7 -- Poll**
- Sticker: Poll -- "Which result resonates most?" [Option from testimonials]
- Purpose: Engagement + data on which results attract the audience

**Slide 8 (optional) -- Additional Testimonials**
- Visual: Grid/collage of multiple testimonial screenshots
- Purpose: Volume overwhelm

**Slide 9 -- CTA**
- Text: "Ready for your own result? [Link / DM keyword]"
- Link sticker or DM prompt

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Minimum 3 distinct testimonials
- [ ] Testimonials represent different ICP segments
- [ ] Pattern recognition slide connects results to mechanism
- [ ] Permission obtained for all testimonials
- [ ] Results are specific (numbers, timeframes, outcomes)

## Framework 3: Objection Breaking

### Purpose
Identify and dissolve the #1 buying objection without feeling like a sales pitch. The sequence should feel like honest reflection, not persuasion.

### Structural Blueprint (5-7 slides)

**Slide 1 -- Name the Objection**
- Text: "I need to address something. A lot of you have told me: '[Exact objection in their words].'"
- Purpose: Honesty signals trust. Name it before they think it.

**Slide 2 -- Validate**
- Text: "And honestly? I get it. I used to think the exact same thing."
- Purpose: Remove defensiveness. You are on their side.

**Slide 3 -- Personal Story/Reframe**
- Visual: Personal photo or video clip
- Text: "Here's what changed my mind: [Brief personal story of holding and then overcoming this same belief]."
- Purpose: Model the belief shift through story

**Slide 4 -- New Perspective + Proof**
- Text: "The reality is: [Reframed truth]. And here's the proof: [Specific data point or client example]."
- Purpose: Logical reframe backed by evidence

**Slide 5 -- Poll**
- Sticker: Poll -- "Did this shift your thinking?" [Yes / Still not sure]
- Purpose: Create commitment through interaction + gather data

**Slide 6 (if "still not sure" option gets traction) -- Deeper Reframe**
- Text: "For those who said 'still not sure' -- here's another way to think about it: [Alternative angle]."

**Slide 7 -- Bridge**
- Text: "If you want to see how [Mechanism/Offer] addresses this directly, [link / DM me]."

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Objection is stated in the audience's exact words
- [ ] Validation is genuine (not dismissive)
- [ ] Reframe uses story or proof (not just assertion)
- [ ] Poll collects data on belief shift
- [ ] No hard selling -- bridge to action, not push to action

## Framework 4: Education/Differentiation

### Purpose
Teach something valuable while positioning the mechanism as fundamentally different from (and better than) the conventional approach.

### Structural Blueprint (5-8 slides)

**Slide 1 -- Surprising Insight**
- Text: "Most [ICP role] don't know this about [topic], but [surprising fact]."
- Purpose: Create curiosity and signal insider knowledge

**Slide 2 -- The Common Approach**
- Text: "What most people do: [Describe the conventional approach]."
- Visual: Simple text card

**Slide 3 -- Why It Fails**
- Text: "Why it doesn't work: [Specific reason, backed by logic or data]."
- Visual: Comparison graphic or text with strike-through

**Slide 4-5 -- The Better Way**
- Text: "What works instead: [Mechanism principle applied to this topic]."
- Break into 2 slides if the explanation needs space
- Purpose: Position mechanism as the superior alternative

**Slide 6 -- Quick Proof**
- Text: "[Specific example or result that proves the better way works]."
- Visual: Screenshot, metric, or brief client reference

**Slide 7 -- Save/Share CTA**
- Text: "Share this with a [friend/colleague] who needs to hear it."
- Sticker: Share prompt or "Save this" reminder

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Teaching is genuine (viewer learns something)
- [ ] Differentiation feels natural (not a sales pitch)
- [ ] Mechanism is positioned as the "better way" through logic
- [ ] One specific proof point included
- [ ] Saves and shares are the target action

## Framework 5: FAQ/Clarity

### Purpose
Answer the most common questions the audience has, reducing friction to purchase and building trust through transparency.

### Structural Blueprint (5-7 slides)

**Slide 1 -- Opener**
- Text: "I keep getting asked the same questions. Let me clear everything up."
- Purpose: Signal helpfulness and transparency

**Slide 2 -- Q&A #1**
- Text: "Q: [Most common question]\nA: [Direct, clear answer in 2 sentences max]."
- Visual: Text card with Q in bold

**Slide 3 -- Q&A #2**
- Same format, different question
- Pick questions that address hidden objections

**Slide 4 -- Q&A #3**
- Same format
- This one should address the question that is most connected to a buying decision

**Slide 5 -- Video Response (optional)**
- Visual: 10-15 second talking head video answering a bonus question
- Purpose: Personal delivery increases trust

**Slide 6 -- Question Box**
- Sticker: Question box -- "What else do you want to know?"
- Purpose: Collect new FAQs and create engagement signal

**Slide 7 (optional) -- Bridge**
- Text: "I answer all of these in depth inside [Offer/Resource]. [Link]."

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Questions are the actual questions people ask (not fabricated)
- [ ] Answers are direct and clear (no hedge language)
- [ ] At least one question addresses a hidden objection
- [ ] Question box collects new questions for future sequences

## Framework 6: Personal Connection

### Purpose
Pure relationship building. No selling, no mechanism mention, no strategy. Just human connection that makes the creator feel real and accessible.

### Structural Blueprint (3-6 slides)

**Slide 1 -- Morning/Current Moment**
- Visual: Selfie or candid photo of current moment
- Text: "[Casual opening -- what they're doing, where they are, how they're feeling]"
- Purpose: Invite the viewer into the creator's real life

**Slide 2 -- Story/Reflection**
- Visual: Photo or text card
- Text: "[Personal story, reflection, or vulnerable share. Something real.]"
- Purpose: Deepen connection through shared humanity

**Slide 3 -- Lighter Moment**
- Visual: Funny moment, pet, food, random observation
- Text: "[Humor or light commentary]"
- Purpose: Balance vulnerability with personality

**Slide 4 -- Audience Question**
- Sticker: Question sticker -- "What about you? [Relevant question]"
- Purpose: Turn monologue into dialogue

**Slide 5 (optional) -- Closing Thought**
- Text: "[Brief reflection or gratitude]"
- Purpose: Close with warmth

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Zero selling or mechanism mention
- [ ] Genuine vulnerability or personality moment
- [ ] Question sticker invites real responses
- [ ] Feels like a text to a friend, not content creation

## Framework 7: Skepticism Removal

### Purpose
Directly address the "this seems too good to be true" resistance. Use specificity, logic, and verifiable proof to build credibility.

### Structural Blueprint (6-8 slides)

**Slide 1 -- Acknowledge Directly**
- Text: "I know some of you think: 'This sounds too good to be true.' Let me address that."
- Purpose: Naming the skepticism disarms it

**Slide 2 -- "I Was the Biggest Skeptic"**
- Text: "Honestly? I was the most skeptical person about [this approach]. Here's why..."
- Visual: Personal photo from "before" era
- Purpose: Establish credibility through shared skepticism

**Slide 3-4 -- The Moment of Proof**
- Text: "Then [specific verifiable event] happened. [Specific numbers/dates/names]."
- Visual: Screenshot, data, or evidence
- Purpose: Specificity destroys skepticism

**Slide 5 -- Deep-Dive on One Result**
- Text: "Let me break down one example: [detailed walkthrough of a single result]."
- Purpose: One deeply examined result is more convincing than ten surface-level ones

**Slide 6 -- Logical Explanation**
- Text: "Here's why it works (the boring version): [logical/mechanical explanation]."
- Purpose: Give the analytical mind something to hold onto

**Slide 7 -- Invitation to Verify**
- Text: "Don't take my word for it. [Where to verify -- reviews, case studies, DM a client]."
- Purpose: Confidence in transparency

**Slide 8 -- CTA**
- Text: "Still skeptical? DM me. I'll answer any question you have."
- Purpose: Move skeptics into conversation

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Skepticism named explicitly, not danced around
- [ ] Creator's own skepticism journey shared authentically
- [ ] Proof is specific, verifiable, and detailed
- [ ] Logical explanation satisfies the analytical mind
- [ ] Invitation to verify shows confidence, not defensiveness

## Framework 8: Mission/Values

### Purpose
Attract values-aligned followers and repel wrong-fit audiences. Polarizing by design -- the right people lean in harder, the wrong people self-select out.

### Structural Blueprint (5-7 slides)

**Slide 1 -- Bold Mission Statement**
- Text: "Here's what I believe: [Core belief from positioning.md]."
- Visual: Bold text on strong color background
- Purpose: Plant the flag

**Slide 2 -- Why This Matters**
- Text: "[Why this belief matters to the creator personally -- brief origin]."
- Purpose: Connect the mission to personal conviction

**Slide 3 -- The Industry Problem**
- Text: "The [industry] has a problem: [What the creator stands against]."
- Purpose: Create a shared enemy / villain

**Slide 4 -- "What We Stand For Instead"**
- Text: "What I/we stand for: [2-3 values, clearly stated]."
- Purpose: Define the tribe's identity

**Slide 5 -- Proof of Living the Values**
- Visual: Evidence of living these values (action, not just words)
- Text: "[Specific example of the creator living these values]."
- Purpose: Actions > words

**Slide 6 -- Polarizing Invitation**
- Text: "If this resonates, you're in the right place. If it doesn't, no hard feelings -- this isn't for everyone."
- Purpose: Strengthen commitment from the right audience by acknowledging it's not for all

**Slide 7 (optional) -- Community Signal**
- Sticker: Poll -- "Does this resonate?" [Absolutely / Not for me]
- Purpose: Let the audience self-identify

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Mission is specific and potentially polarizing (not "be your best self")
- [ ] Industry problem is clearly named
- [ ] Values backed by evidence (not just stated)
- [ ] Polarizing invitation is respectful but clear
- [ ] Aligns with positioning.md core belief

## Slide Design Reference

### Rhythm Alternation Pattern

For any sequence longer than 4 slides, alternate slide types:

```
Slide 1: Text card (hook)
Slide 2: Photo + caption
Slide 3: Text card (key point)
Slide 4: Video clip or screenshot
Slide 5: Interactive (poll/question)
Slide 6: Text card (insight)
Slide 7: CTA with link/action sticker
```

### Safe Zones
- Top 15%: Reserved for username (do not place critical text here)
- Bottom 15%: Reserved for reply bar
- Sides 10%: Buffer zone
- Usable area: Center 70% of the screen

### Text Rules
- Maximum 30 words per slide
- Minimum font size: readable at arm's length
- High contrast (dark on light or light on dark)
- One idea per slide, always
