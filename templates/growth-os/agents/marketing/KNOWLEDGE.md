## 1. Content Strategy Fundamentals

### The 70/20/10 Content Mix

Every content calendar follows this ratio across all platforms combined:

| Category | Ratio | Purpose | Examples |
|----------|-------|---------|----------|
| Value | 70% | Build trust, demonstrate expertise, deliver transformation | Tutorials, frameworks, how-tos, educational threads, deep-dive videos |
| Engagement | 20% | Build relationship, create conversation, gather data | Polls, questions, hot takes, personal stories, BTS content |
| Promotional | 10% | Drive revenue, present offers, create urgency | Direct offers, launch content, testimonials with CTAs, ad creative |

**Rule:** Promotional content is earned through value. Never promote before you have deposited enough value to justify the ask. The minimum deposit-to-withdrawal ratio is 7:1 (7 value/engagement pieces before 1 promotional piece).

### Content Pillar Mapping

Content pillars are derived from the intersection of ICP pain points and the offer transformation. Every pillar addresses a specific pain and points toward the mechanism as the solution.

**Pillar Derivation Formula:**
```
Pillar = ICP Pain Point + Offer Mechanism Application + Buyer Journey Stage
```

**Standard Pillar Structure (4-5 pillars per brand):**
1. **Problem Awareness Pillar** -- Surface the pain the ICP does not fully recognize yet
2. **Education Pillar** -- Teach the new framework/mechanism that solves the problem
3. **Proof Pillar** -- Demonstrate results through case studies, transformations, data
4. **Lifestyle Pillar** -- Show the aspirational outcome of applying the mechanism
5. **Community Pillar** -- Build belonging and identity around shared values

### Platform Selection Matrix

Not every creator needs every platform. Selection depends on ICP behavior and offer type.

| Platform | Best For | ICP Signal | Content Depth | Conversion Path |
|----------|----------|------------|---------------|-----------------|
| YouTube | High-ticket, education-heavy offers | ICP researches before buying | Deep (10-60 min) | VSSL > Link in description > Sales page |
| Instagram | Lifestyle/aspiration brands, DM-close offers | ICP scrolls for inspiration | Shallow-Medium (15-60 sec) | Reel > Profile > Link in bio > DM |
| TikTok | Younger demographics, virality-dependent growth | ICP discovers through trends | Shallow (15-60 sec) | Video > Profile > Link in bio |
| Twitter/X | Thought leadership, B2B-adjacent offers | ICP consumes text-based insight | Medium (threads 2-5 min) | Thread > Profile > Link/DM |
| LinkedIn | B2B offers, corporate coaching, consulting | ICP is a professional/decision-maker | Medium (1-3 min reads) | Post > Profile > Connection > DM/Inquiry |
| Podcast | Relationship-deep offers, trust-heavy niches | ICP consumes audio during commute/gym | Deep (20-60 min) | Episode > Show notes > Lead magnet |
| Blog/SEO | Evergreen discovery, course/digital-product offers | ICP searches Google for solutions | Deep (5-15 min reads) | Search > Blog > Lead magnet > Funnel |
| Paid Ads | Scaling any of the above | ICP is reachable through ad platforms | Varies | Ad > Landing page > Offer |

## 2. Buyer Journey Mapping

### The 5 Awareness Levels

Every piece of content targets a specific awareness level. Mismatching awareness level to content approach is the #1 cause of content that "feels off."

| Level | What They Know | Content Approach | Content Types |
|-------|---------------|------------------|---------------|
| **Unaware** | Does not know they have a problem | Lifestyle content, aspiration, identity-based content | Lifestyle reels, vlog-style YouTube, story posts |
| **Problem-Aware** | Knows the pain but not the solution | Agitate the pain, validate the frustration, build resonance | Educational videos, pain-point threads, storytelling reels |
| **Solution-Aware** | Knows solutions exist but not yours | Differentiate your mechanism, explain why other approaches fail | Framework posts, how-to content, mechanism reveal videos |
| **Product-Aware** | Knows your offer but has not bought | Handle objections, stack proof, demonstrate transformation | Case studies, testimonials, FAQ stories, comparison content |
| **Most-Aware** | Ready to buy, needs the push | Direct CTA, urgency, final objection handling, offer stack | Launch sequences, direct offer ads, VSSL, retargeting |

## 3. Brand Voice Application Rules

Brand voice is sovereign. Once extracted, it governs every word of customer-facing content. Sub-agents must internalize these rules:

### Voice Consistency Protocol

1. **Load `reference/brand-voice.md` before writing any content.** No exceptions.
2. **Mirror the creator's vocabulary.** If the creator says "clients" not "customers," every agent says "clients." If they say "system" not "program," every agent says "system."
3. **Match sentence rhythm.** If the creator uses short, punchy sentences, do not write flowing academic prose. If they use stories, do not write bullet-point listicles.
4. **Respect forbidden language.** The brand voice document lists words and phrases the creator never uses. These are hard bans across all content.
5. **Adapt tone to platform without losing identity.** LinkedIn gets a professional overlay. Twitter gets compressed. Stories get casual. But the underlying personality remains identical.

### Platform Voice Adaptation

| Platform | Voice Adjustment | What Stays | What Changes |
|----------|-----------------|------------|-------------|
| YouTube | Most natural, closest to speaking voice | Personality, vocabulary, stories | More conversational, contractions, rhetorical questions |
| Instagram Reels | Compressed, punchy | Energy level, core phrases | Shorter sentences, more visual language |
| Stories | Most casual, "texting a friend" | Humor, vulnerability level | Informal grammar OK, abbreviations OK |
| Twitter | Sharpest, most edited | Core beliefs, hot takes | Every word earns its place, aphoristic style |
| LinkedIn | Professional overlay added | Expertise, frameworks | More formal transitions, industry terminology OK |
| Paid Ads | Direct-response overlay | ICP language mirroring | More structured, benefit-focused, clear CTAs |

## 4. ICP Pain Point to Content Angle Mapping

Every ICP document contains a ranked list of pain points and desires. Content angles are derived by combining a pain point with a content format and a messaging approach.

### Angle Derivation Formula

```
Content Angle = Pain Point + Emotional Trigger + Format + Mechanism Connection
```

### The 6 Emotional Triggers

| Trigger | Mechanism | Use When |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| **Fear of Missing Out** | Show what they are leaving on the table | ICP is aware of the opportunity but not acting |
| **Pain Amplification** | Show the compounding cost of inaction | ICP is numb to their current situation |
| **Identity Shift** | Show who they could become, not just what they could achieve | ICP is motivated by status/identity transformation |
| **Pattern Interruption** | Challenge a belief they hold as truth | ICP is stuck in a paradigm that blocks progress |
| **Social Proof Cascade** | Overwhelm with volume of results | ICP is skeptical but open to evidence |
| **Scarcity of Attention** | Demonstrate that the window to act is closing | ICP is procrastinating despite understanding the value |

### Mapping Process

For each ICP pain point:
1. Identify which emotional trigger resonates strongest with that pain
2. Select 3-4 content formats that best deliver that trigger
3. Connect the angle to the offer mechanism (how the mechanism solves this specific pain)
4. Assign to a buyer journey stage
5. Slot into the content calendar at the appropriate position in the 4-week arc

## 5. Cross-Platform Repurposing Principles

### The Cascade Model

One core content piece cascades into 8-12 derivative assets. But repurposing is NOT copy-pasting. Each derivative must be platform-native.

**Repurposing Hierarchy:**
```
Tier 1 (Source Content):
  YouTube Video | Podcast Episode | Pillar Blog Post

Tier 2 (Direct Derivatives):
  3-5 Short-form clips (Reels/Shorts/TikTok)
  1 Twitter thread extracting key insights
  1 LinkedIn post reframing for professional audience
  5-7 Story slides covering BTS of creation
  2-3 Standalone tweets from quotable moments
  1 Blog post summary with SEO optimization

Tier 3 (Extended Derivatives):
  1 Email newsletter featuring the core idea
  1 Lead magnet expansion of the topic
  1 Paid ad using the best-performing derivative
  Community discussion prompt based on the topic
```

### Platform-Native Adaptation Rules

| Source Element | Twitter Adaptation | LinkedIn Adaptation | Reel Adaptation | Story Adaptation |
|---------------|-------------------|--------------------|--------------------|-------------------|
| Key insight | Standalone tweet, 1-2 sentences max | Expanded with professional context and application | 15-sec hook+value clip | Text card with bold statement |
| Personal story | 3-5 tweet story arc, punchy beats | Full narrative with professional lesson extracted | 30-sec storytelling reel | 5-slide personal connection sequence |
| Framework | Thread with one tweet per step | Framework post with visual formatting | Quick-tips reel listing steps | Education sequence with one slide per step |
| Client result | Result + lesson tweet | Case study post with business context | Transformation reel with before/after | Proof-from-many sequence |
| Hot take | Single tweet, maximize provocation | Contrarian take post with nuanced argument | Split-screen reaction reel | Mission/values story sequence |

### Repurposing Anti-Patterns (Never Do This)

- Never post the same caption across Instagram, Twitter, and LinkedIn
- Never clip a YouTube video without re-editing for vertical format and adding text overlays
- Never extract a Twitter thread from a blog post by just breaking paragraphs into tweets
- Never use the same hook on multiple platforms in the same week
- Never repurpose without checking if the derivative adds value beyond the source

## 7. The Air Bending Principle (Element 1/2/3/4)

The same core knowledge, repositioned to different ICPs through messaging. This is the scalability engine of content.

### How It Works

One transformation framework, mechanism, or body of expertise can serve multiple audiences by changing:

| Element | What Changes | What Stays |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| **Element 1: Language** | Vocabulary, jargon, references specific to the audience segment | The underlying insight or framework |
| **Element 2: Context** | Industry examples, use cases, application scenarios | The mechanism and its principles |
| **Element 3: Pain Framing** | Which specific pain point is foregrounded | The solution architecture |
| **Element 4: Identity Markers** | Aspirational identity, status signals, lifestyle cues | The transformation promise |

### Application in Content

- A YouTube video on "pricing psychology" can become a Reel for fitness coaches, a LinkedIn post for SaaS founders, and a Twitter thread for freelancers -- same knowledge, different framing
- A testimonial from a coaching client can be framed as "escape the 9-5" on Instagram and "build a scalable consulting practice" on LinkedIn
- A framework post can use gym metaphors for fitness audiences and kitchen metaphors for food creators

### Rules
- The core truth must remain intact -- Air Bending changes the packaging, never the product
- Each repositioned version must feel native to its target audience (not like a generic template with names swapped)
- Track which Element combinations perform best per platform to refine positioning over time

## 8. Hook Psychology

Hooks are the single most important element in all digital content. A great hook on mediocre content outperforms a mediocre hook on great content every time.

### The 5 Hook Mechanisms

| Mechanism | How It Works | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Pattern Interrupt** | Breaks the viewer's autopilot scrolling with something unexpected | "The worst advice I ever got made me $2M." |
| **Curiosity Gap** | Creates an information void the brain needs to close | "There's a reason 90% of coaches fail in year one. And it's not what you think." |
| **Open Loop** | Starts a narrative the brain cannot leave unfinished | "I was 24 hours from shutting down my business when I discovered..." |
| **Identity Callout** | Names the viewer so specifically they feel personally addressed | "If you're a fitness coach making under $10K/mo and posting every day..." |
| **Emotional Spike** | Triggers an immediate emotional reaction (anger, surprise, desire) | "Your followers don't care about your content. Here's what they actually care about." |

### Hook Intensity Matching

The hook must match the content's payoff. Over-promising in the hook and under-delivering in the body destroys trust faster than a weak hook loses attention.

| Hook Intensity | Content Must Deliver | Risk If Mismatched |
|---------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Extreme ("This will change everything") | Genuine paradigm shift or framework | Audience feels deceived, unfollows |
| Strong ("Most people don't know this") | Non-obvious insight with practical application | Audience feels mild disappointment |
| Moderate ("Here's what I learned") | Personal story with extractable lesson | Low risk, moderate engagement |
| Soft ("Something I've been thinking about") | Reflection or observation that resonates | Low risk, low engagement |

## 9. Platform Algorithm Overview

### What Each Platform Optimizes For

| Platform | Primary Signal | Secondary Signal | What This Means for Content |
|----------|---------------|-----------------|---------------------------|
| **YouTube** | CTR (Click-Through Rate) + AVD (Average View Duration) | Subscriber conversion, session time | Thumbnails and titles determine reach; script retention determines distribution |
| **Instagram Reels** | Saves > Shares > Comments > Likes | Watch-through rate, replays | Create content worth saving (actionable) and sharing (relatable); likes are the weakest signal |
| **Instagram Stories** | Completion rate, reply rate | Sticker interaction rate | Keep sequences short enough to complete; use interactive stickers to boost signal |
| **TikTok** | Watch-through rate > Replays > Shares | Comment velocity in first hour | Front-load the value; design for re-watch; spark comments with opinion-driven content |
| **Twitter/X** | Reply depth + Bookmarks | Quote tweets, profile clicks | Write tweets that demand a response; create threads worth saving; provoke thoughtful disagreement |
| **LinkedIn** | Dwell time + Comment quality | Share velocity in first hour | Write long enough to generate dwell time; end with genuine questions that spark quality comments |
| **Facebook/Meta Ads** | Relevance score + CTR + Conversion rate | Frequency, ad fatigue rate | Creative quality determines cost; fresh creative keeps CPMs low; audience match reduces waste |

## 10. The 40/40/20 Impact Distribution Principle

This is the governing law of all marketing in the Growth OS. It applies to every piece of content, every campaign, every launch:

| Component | Impact Weight | Implication |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| **Audience Quality** | 40% | The right message to the wrong audience always fails. ICP precision is the #1 lever. |
| **Offer Strength** | 40% | A strong offer converts with mediocre copy. A weak offer fails with great copy. |
| **Copy/Messaging** | 20% | Copy is the amplifier, not the engine. Optimize copy AFTER audience and offer are validated. |

### What This Means for Content Production

- Before writing a single word of content, verify the ICP document is complete and validated
- Before creating ad creative, verify the offer has passed its quality gate (LTV:CAC >= 3.0)
- When content underperforms, audit in this order: Are we reaching the right audience? Is the offer compelling? Then and only then, examine the copy
- Never blame the copywriting when the audience targeting is wrong or the offer is weak
- Content strategy begins with understanding the audience, not brainstorming content ideas

## 11. Content Calendar 4-Week Arc

All content across all platforms follows a 4-week rotating theme cycle:

| Week | Theme | Purpose | Content Ratio | Primary Mechanism |
|------|-------|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Week 1** | Problem Agitation | Surface the pain, build resonance, make audience feel seen | 80% value / 20% engagement | Pain amplification, identity callout |
| **Week 2** | Mechanism Introduction | Reveal the "how" without selling, position the new approach | 70% value / 20% engagement / 10% promo | Pattern interruption, education |
| **Week 3** | Proof & Results | Social proof, case studies, transformations that build belief | 60% value / 20% engagement / 20% promo | Social proof cascade, transformation |
| **Week 4** | Offer Window | Direct pitch supported by 3 weeks of accumulated trust | 50% value / 20% engagement / 30% promo | Scarcity, urgency, final objection handling |

### Arc Rules
- The arc resets every 4 weeks and can be adjusted for launch timing
- Launch windows always align with Week 4 (Offer Window)
- Weeks 1-3 are non-negotiable preparation -- skipping to Week 4 without the buildup destroys conversion rates
- Each platform plays its assigned role within each week's theme (YouTube drives deep understanding, Reels drive discovery, Stories drive intimacy, etc.)
