# Paid Ads Frameworks & Playbooks

> Complete structural blueprints for all 6 paid ad creative types. Each framework produces campaign-ready assets with copy, creative direction, targeting, and testing plans.

---

## Paid Ads Core Principles

### The 40/40/20 Rule Applied to Ads
- **40% Audience:** Targeting the right people matters more than the creative. Fix targeting before testing new creative.
- **40% Offer:** The offer must be compelling enough that even mediocre creative converts. If the offer is weak, no amount of creative genius will save the campaign.
- **20% Copy/Creative:** The creative's job is to stop the scroll, qualify the viewer, and connect the offer to the viewer's pain. It amplifies a good audience + offer combination.

### The Profile Funnel Ad Model
For creator businesses, the most effective ad architecture is:
```
Ad --> Profile Visit --> Consume 3-5 pieces of organic content --> DM or Link in Bio --> Sale
```
This means ads do not need to close the sale. They need to get the right person to the profile, where the organic content ecosystem does the trust-building.

### Creative Testing Methodology
```
Phase 1: Test 3-5 hook variants (same body, same CTA) -- 3 days, $[X]/day per variant
Phase 2: Take winning hook + test 2-3 body variants -- 3 days
Phase 3: Take winning hook+body + test 2-3 CTAs -- 3 days
Phase 4: Scale winner -- increase budget 20% every 3 days if KPIs hold
Kill trigger: If CPA exceeds 2x target for 48 hours, pause and refresh creative
```

## Framework 1: Video Ad -- Hook Style

### Purpose
Cold traffic education and lead magnet opt-in. Designed to interrupt the scroll, qualify the viewer as ICP, agitate their pain, and drive them to a resource or profile.

### Structural Blueprint

```
0-3 sec: PATTERN INTERRUPT HOOK
[VISUAL: Face close-up, direct eye contact, or unexpected visual element]
[TEXT OVERLAY: Bold statement -- 5-7 words]
"[Bold claim or provocative question that stops the scroll]"

Hook variant patterns:
A (Pain): "If you're still [painful thing], this is why."
B (Curiosity): "The [hidden thing] behind every [desirable outcome]."
C (Direct): "Stop [common mistake]. Do this instead."
D (Result): "[Specific result] in [timeframe]. Here's how."
E (Identity): "This is for [exact ICP description] only."

3-10 sec: QUALIFY THE VIEWER
[VISUAL: Slight pullback, conversational body language]
"If you're a [specific ICP description] who [specific situation]..."
"...and you've been [struggling with / trying / dealing with]..."
Purpose: Everyone who does not match self-selects out. Your CPA drops.

10-30 sec: AGITATE THE PROBLEM
[VISUAL: B-roll of relatable situation, or continue talking head]
"Here's what's really going on: [root cause of pain]."
"You've probably tried [common approach 1] and [common approach 2]."
"And you're still stuck because [why those approaches fail]."
Use ICP language verbatim from reference/icp.md.

30-45 sec: INTRODUCE THE MECHANISM
[VISUAL: Shift -- screen recording, whiteboard, graphic, or visual metaphor]
"What actually works is [mechanism principle -- high level]."
"It works because [1-sentence explanation of why]."
DO NOT pitch the offer. Educate. Create curiosity.

45-60 sec: CTA
[VISUAL: Return to face, direct eye contact, slight lean in]
[TEXT OVERLAY: Specific action text + arrow/pointer to action]
"I put everything into a free [lead magnet type] -- [specific name]."
"Click the link below to grab it. It takes 2 minutes."
OR: "Follow me for more [category]. I post [these kinds of insights] every [day/week]."
```

### Platform-Specific Adjustments

| Element | Meta (Feed) | Meta (Stories/Reels) | YouTube Pre-Roll | TikTok |
|---------|-------------|---------------------|-----------------|--------|
| Aspect Ratio | 1:1 or 4:5 | 9:16 | 16:9 | 9:16 |
| Hook Window | 3 seconds | 2 seconds | 5 seconds (skip button) | 1-2 seconds |
| Length | 30-60 sec | 15-30 sec | 15-30 sec (non-skippable) or 60 sec | 15-45 sec |
| CTA Style | "Link below" | "Swipe up" / "Tap" | "Click below" | "Link in bio" |
| Tone | Polished-casual | Native, raw | Slightly more produced | Fully native |

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] 3-5 hook variants from different angles
- [ ] ICP language from reference/icp.md used in agitation section
- [ ] Mechanism introduced but not fully explained (creates curiosity)
- [ ] CTA is specific about what happens on click
- [ ] Visual direction included for every section
- [ ] Under 60 seconds total

## Framework 2: Video Ad -- Story Style

### Purpose
Build connection with warm/cool audiences. Drive to VSSL, offer page, or longer content. Story ads feel like content, not advertising.

### Structural Blueprint

```
0-3 sec: STORY HOOK
[VISUAL: Cinematic or intimate -- golden hour, desk, morning setting]
[TEXT OVERLAY: First line of the story]
"[Time marker] ago, I [relatable low point or surprising situation]."
"I was [specific painful state that mirrors ICP reality]."

Hook variant patterns:
A (Timeline): "12 months ago, I [painful state]. Today, [aspirational result]."
B (Moment): "The day I [specific event], everything changed."
C (Confession): "I've never told anyone this, but [vulnerable admission]."
D (Impossible): "Everyone said [goal] was impossible for [person like me]."

3-30 sec: THE STRUGGLE
[VISUAL: Mix of talking head and B-roll that illustrates the struggle]
Mirror the ICP's current reality:
"I was doing [what ICP is doing now]."
"I tried [what ICP has tried]."
"Nothing was working because [root cause they haven't identified]."
This section must feel like the creator is describing the VIEWER'S life.

30-45 sec: THE DISCOVERY
[VISUAL: Shift -- energy change, lighter environment, different angle]
"Then I discovered [principle/mechanism -- not the product name]."
"What I realized was: [the paradigm shift in 1-2 sentences]."
Create the "aha" moment that makes the viewer think "what is this?"

45-60 sec: THE RESULT
[VISUAL: Aspirational -- results, lifestyle, confidence]
[TEXT OVERLAY: Key metric or outcome]
"In [timeframe]: [specific result 1], [specific result 2], [specific result 3]."
Paint the "after" picture. Make the viewer want to live in it.

60-75 sec: THE INVITATION
[VISUAL: Direct to camera, warm, inviting]
[TEXT OVERLAY: CTA text]
"I built this into a [system/program/guide] so you can do the same."
"Click below to [watch the full breakdown / get the free guide / apply]."
```

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Story mirrors ICP's current reality in the struggle section
- [ ] Discovery section creates curiosity without full explanation
- [ ] Results are specific and measurable
- [ ] CTA drives to next step in funnel (VSSL, lead magnet, application)
- [ ] Feels like content, not an ad
- [ ] Length: 45-75 seconds

## Framework 3: Video Ad -- Testimonial

### Purpose
Social proof-driven conversion. Most effective for warm/hot audiences who need proof that the mechanism works for people like them.

### Structural Blueprint

```
0-3 sec: RESULT HOOK
[VISUAL: Client on camera or creator narrating with client result on screen]
[TEXT OVERLAY: Headline result number]
"'[Specific result quote from client]' -- [Client Name]"
OR: "[Client] went from [before state] to [after state] in [timeframe]."

3-15 sec: THE BEFORE STATE
[VISUAL: Client describing or creator narrating their starting point]
"Before working with [Creator/Brand], [Client] was [relatable struggle]."
"They had tried [common approach 1], [common approach 2]."
"They were [emotional state] and ready to [give up / try something new]."
Mirror the ICP's current situation exactly.

15-30 sec: WHAT THEY TRIED BEFORE
[VISUAL: Client or creator with frustrated body language]
"Like most [ICP role], they thought [common approach] was the answer."
"But [why that approach failed for them specifically]."
"That's when they found [Creator/Brand/Mechanism]."

30-45 sec: WHAT WAS DIFFERENT
[VISUAL: Shift -- lighter, more energetic]
"What made [Mechanism Name] different was [1-2 sentence principle]."
"Instead of [old approach], they [new approach]."
"And the shift was almost immediate."

45-60 sec: THE SPECIFIC RESULTS
[VISUAL: Results on screen -- screenshots, metrics, lifestyle shots]
[TEXT OVERLAY: Key metrics]
"In [timeframe]:"
"[Result 1]"
"[Result 2]"
"[Result 3]"
"And now they're [aspirational ongoing state]."

60-75 sec: RECOMMENDATION + CTA
[VISUAL: Client speaking directly to camera]
"If you're [where I was], [this] is what changed everything."
[Creator or text overlay: CTA]
"Click below to [start your transformation / apply / watch the full story]."
```

### Client Selection Criteria
- Client must mirror the ICP demographic
- Result must be specific and verifiable
- Client must be articulate on camera (or use creator narration)
- Story must include the "before" that matches the viewer's "now"
- Multiple testimonial ads should cover different ICP sub-segments

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Client mirrors ICP (viewer sees themselves)
- [ ] Before state is relatable to the target audience
- [ ] Results are specific with numbers and timeframes
- [ ] Mechanism credited for the result (not generic "hard work")
- [ ] CTA matches the funnel stage
- [ ] Permission documented for testimonial use

## Framework 4: Image Ad

### Purpose
Quick-hit awareness, retargeting reminders, and direct offer announcements. Image ads are the workhorse of retargeting campaigns.

### Structural Blueprint

```
PRIMARY TEXT (above the image in feed):
Line 1: [Hook -- bold claim, question, or result that stops the scroll]

Line 2-3: [Body -- 2-3 sentences connecting the hook to the viewer's pain/desire]

Line 4: [CTA -- specific about what happens when they click]

HEADLINE (below the image, bold):
[7 words maximum. Benefit-driven. Not the same as the primary text hook.]

DESCRIPTION (below headline, smaller text):
[Supporting detail or social proof snippet. 1 sentence.]

IMAGE SPECIFICATIONS:
- Format: 1:1 (feed) or 9:16 (stories)
- Text overlay: Under 20% of image area
- Elements: High contrast face or result visual, 3 words max overlay
- Brand: Logo small in corner, brand colors in background
- Style: Must feel native to the platform (not stock photo, not corporate)
```

### Image Ad Variants by Funnel Stage

| Stage | Image Approach | Primary Text Approach | CTA |
|-------|---------------|----------------------|-----|
| TOFU | Face + curiosity text | Problem agitation | "Learn how" / "Watch now" |
| MOFU | Result/proof visual | Mechanism differentiation | "Get the free [resource]" |
| BOFU | Before/after or testimonial | Offer + social proof | "Apply now" / "Enroll today" |
| Retarget | Familiar face + urgency | Objection handling + deadline | "Don't miss out" / "Last chance" |

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Primary text hook is compelling in 1 line
- [ ] Image stops the scroll (high contrast, face, or unexpected)
- [ ] Text overlay under 20% of image
- [ ] Headline is benefit-driven (not feature-driven)
- [ ] CTA is specific about next step
- [ ] 3 hook variants for the primary text

## Framework 5: Carousel Ad

### Purpose
Multi-step education, value demonstration, or objection handling. Carousel format allows progressive disclosure -- each card deepens engagement.

### Structural Blueprint

```
CARD 1: HOOK + PROMISE
- Image: Most compelling visual (face + bold text)
- Headline: [Result or promise -- what they'll learn/get by swiping]
- Primary text hook: [Why they should swipe through]
- Rule: Must be independently compelling (many only see card 1)

CARD 2: PROBLEM SETUP
- Image: Visual representing the problem
- Headline: [The problem stated clearly]
- Body: [Why this problem matters -- 1-2 sentences max per card]

CARD 3: FRAMEWORK STEP 1 / VALUE POINT 1
- Image: Step visual or icon
- Headline: "Step 1: [Name]"
- Body: [What and why in 1-2 sentences]

CARD 4: FRAMEWORK STEP 2 / VALUE POINT 2
- Same structure as Card 3

CARD 5: FRAMEWORK STEP 3 / VALUE POINT 3
- Same structure

CARD 6 (optional): PROOF
- Image: Testimonial screenshot or result metric
- Headline: "[Result] in [timeframe]"
- Body: [Brief proof]

CARD 7: CTA + SOCIAL PROOF
- Image: Creator face + action prompt
- Headline: [CTA -- what to do next]
- Body: [Social proof line: "[X] people have already [action]"]
- Button: [Specific action text]
```

### Carousel Design Rules
- Each card must work independently (users may not see all cards)
- Progressive disclosure: each card adds new information
- Consistent visual style across all cards (same template, colors, fonts)
- Card 1 and final card are the most important (first impression + CTA)
- Maximum 10 cards; sweet spot is 5-7

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Card 1 standalone-compelling
- [ ] Each card adds new information (not repetitive)
- [ ] Consistent visual design across all cards
- [ ] CTA on final card is clear and specific
- [ ] 5-7 cards (not too many, not too few)
- [ ] Social proof on or near the CTA card

## Framework 6: UGC-Style Ad

### Purpose
Native-feeling content that performs as an ad. The viewer should not realize it is an ad until the CTA. This format works because it matches the organic content consumption pattern.

### Structural Blueprint

```
0-3 sec: CASUAL HOOK
[VISUAL: Selfie camera, casual setting (kitchen, bedroom, car, walking)]
[NO text overlay initially -- feels organic]
"Okay so I have to share this because [genuine excitement/surprise]..."
OR: "Okay wait -- if you're a [ICP description], you need to hear this."
OR: "I'm literally [shook/obsessed/mind-blown] right now."

3-20 sec: PROBLEM + DISCOVERY STORY
[VISUAL: Continues selfie/talking head, natural movement]
"So I've been [struggling with / trying to figure out / looking for] [ICP pain]..."
"I tried [common thing 1], tried [common thing 2]... nothing."
"Then I [stumbled on / was told about / found] [mechanism/offer]."
Language: conversational, unscripted-feeling, filler words OK ("like," "honestly," "actually")

20-40 sec: WHAT HAPPENED / RESULTS
[VISUAL: Can shift to screen recording, results, or product demo]
"And honestly? [Specific result in their words]."
"Like, I went from [before state] to [after state] in [timeframe]."
"The part that surprised me most was [unexpected benefit]."

40-50 sec: CASUAL CTA
[VISUAL: Return to selfie camera]
"If you're like me and you've been [pain], just [action]."
"I'll put the link [in my bio / below / in the comments]."
"Seriously, do yourself a favor."
```

### UGC Authenticity Signals
- Slightly shaky camera (not too polished)
- Natural lighting (not studio)
- Casual clothing and setting
- "Um," "honestly," "like" allowed (signals unscripted)
- No brand graphics or lower-thirds until the CTA
- Filmed in one take or simple jump cuts

### Who Creates It
- **Option A:** Creator films it themselves (most authentic)
- **Option B:** Client/student films it (true UGC)
- **Option C:** Creator writes the script, sends to UGC creator who films in their own style

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Feels like organic content for at least 15 seconds before ad intent shows
- [ ] Language is conversational (not copywritten)
- [ ] Setting is casual/personal (not studio/office)
- [ ] Specific results mentioned (not vague)
- [ ] CTA is casual and clear
- [ ] Under 50 seconds
- [ ] No brand elements until final 10 seconds

## Full-Funnel Campaign Template

### Campaign Architecture

```
CAMPAIGN: [Name]
Objective: [Awareness | Leads | Sales]
Monthly Budget: $[X]
Budget Split: TOFU 60% | MOFU 25% | BOFU 15%

TOFU (Cold Traffic):
Ad Type: [Hook Style Video] + [UGC-Style]
Audience: Interest-based + Lookalike (1-3%)
Creative Count: 3 hook variants x 2 body variants = 6 creatives
Objective: Video views, profile visits, lead magnet optins
KPIs: CPM < $[X], CTR > 1.5%, Cost per lead < $[X]

MOFU (Warm Traffic):
Ad Type: [Story Style Video] + [Carousel]
Audience: 25%+ video viewers, page engagers, website visitors (30 days)
Creative Count: 2 story variants + 1 carousel
Objective: Lead magnet optins, VSSL views
KPIs: CTR > 2.5%, Cost per lead < $[X], VSSL view rate > 30%

BOFU (Hot Traffic):
Ad Type: [Testimonial Video] + [Image Retarget]
Audience: Lead magnet optins, VSSL viewers (50%+), cart abandoners
Creative Count: 2 testimonial variants + 3 image variants (urgency/objection/proof)
Objective: Applications, purchases, enrollments
KPIs: CPA < $[X], ROAS > 3:1, Conversion rate > 2%

RETARGETING LOGIC:
Day 1-3 post-visit: Soft retarget (value content, social proof)
Day 4-7: Medium retarget (mechanism differentiation, deeper proof)
Day 8-14: Hard retarget (direct offer, objection handling, urgency)
Day 15-30: Re-engagement (new angle, fresh creative, testimonial)
Day 31+: Exclude or cool audience (organic nurture only)
```

### Creative Refresh Schedule
- TOFU creatives: Refresh every 2-3 weeks (highest volume, fastest fatigue)
- MOFU creatives: Refresh every 3-4 weeks
- BOFU creatives: Refresh every 4-6 weeks (smaller audience, slower fatigue)
- Monitor: If CPM increases 25%+ over 5 days, creative is fatigued -- refresh immediately
