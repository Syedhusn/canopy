# YouTube Frameworks & Playbooks

> Complete structural blueprints for all 7 YouTube video types. Each framework is production-ready -- the agent fills in creator-specific content, the structure is non-negotiable.

---

## Framework 1: Educational/Value Video

### Purpose
Build authority and deliver genuine transformation. The viewer should leave the video having learned something they can immediately apply. Zero sales intent.

### Structural Blueprint

#### Hook (0:00 - 0:30)
**Formula Options:**
- **Curiosity Gap:** "[Counterintuitive result] -- and I'm going to show you exactly how. But first you need to understand why everything you've been told about [topic] is wrong."
- **Bold Claim:** "I [achieved result] in [timeframe], and the strategy I used goes against everything the gurus teach."
- **Pain Agitation:** "If you're [doing common thing] and wondering why [desired result] isn't happening, this video is going to change everything."
- **Direct Value:** "In the next [X] minutes, I'm going to give you the exact [framework/system/strategy] I use to [result]. No fluff, no pitch -- just the playbook."

**Rules:**
- Maximum 30 seconds
- Must create an open loop
- Must be filmable as a standalone clip
- Include [B-roll suggestion] for visual interest

#### Credibility + Promise (0:30 - 1:30)
```
Quick credibility marker: [1-2 sentences establishing why viewer should listen]
Explicit promise: "By the end of this video, you'll know exactly [specific outcome]."
Structure preview: "I'm going to break this into [X] parts..."
First insight teaser: "And the [first/third] one is the one nobody talks about."
```

#### Body (1:30 - End minus 2 minutes)
**Structure for educational content:**
```
SECTION 1: The Counterintuitive Insight (strongest point first)
- Open with the most surprising element
- Explain why the conventional approach fails
- Introduce the new framework/approach
- [B-roll: relevant visual or graphic]

> PATTERN INTERRUPT (at 3-4 min mark):
> "But here's where it gets interesting..."
> [Visual shift: location change, screen recording, whiteboard]

SECTION 2: The Framework/Methodology
- Present the system step by step
- Use on-screen graphics for each step
- Include a real example for each step
- [On-screen text: key framework diagram]

> PATTERN INTERRUPT (at 6-8 min mark):
> "Now, most people stop here. But there's a second layer..."
> [B-roll shift]

SECTION 3: The Application/Implementation
- Walk through exactly how to apply this
- Include specific numbers, templates, or resources
- Address the most common mistake at this stage
- [Screen recording: demonstration if applicable]

SECTION 4 (optional): The Advanced Layer
- For viewers who want to go deeper
- Reference this as a "bonus" to reward viewers who stayed
- Connect to a broader system or philosophy
```

**Retention Mechanics (deploy every 2-3 minutes):**
- "But here's where it gets interesting..."
- "Now, most people stop here. But there's a second layer..."
- "This next part is what actually makes the difference..."
- Open a new story loop before closing the current one
- [B-roll shift, screen change, location change]

#### Close (Final 1-2 minutes)
```
Summary: "So to recap: [3 key takeaways in 1 sentence each]"
Bridge: "Now if you want to go even deeper on [related topic], watch this video next."
[End screen: point to related video]
No external CTA. No link in description mention. Keep them on YouTube.
```

### Thumbnail + Title
- **Title formulas:** "How I [Result] in [Timeframe]", "The [Adjective] Guide to [Topic]", "[X] [Things] That [Changed/Transformed] My [Area]"
- **Thumbnail:** High-contrast face + emotion, 3 words max overlay, clear focal point
- **Rule:** Title and thumbnail tell complementary stories (not duplicate)

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Hook creates a specific, closeable open loop
- [ ] Credibility established within first 90 seconds
- [ ] Pattern interrupt every 2-3 minutes
- [ ] All content written in spoken language (contractions, fragments)
- [ ] Brand voice from reference/brand-voice.md applied throughout
- [ ] ICP pain point from reference/icp.md addressed directly
- [ ] Zero sales CTAs -- only bridge to next video
- [ ] B-roll suggestions included for every section
- [ ] Thumbnail concept and 3 title options provided
- [ ] Script reads naturally when spoken aloud

## Framework 2: VSSL (Video Sales Letter)

### Purpose
The ONE video that drives all sales. Every profile link, every ad, every funnel points here. This is the creator's most important piece of content. Follows the 80/20 story-to-mechanism ratio.

### Structural Blueprint

#### Section 1: Demographic Callout (0:00 - 1:00)
```
"If you're a [specific person] who [specific situation]...
if you've been [specific struggle they're experiencing]...
and you're wondering [specific question they ask themselves]...
then this video was made for you."

Rules:
- Call out the EXACT person from icp.md
- Use ICP language patterns verbatim
- Make them feel seen and understood
- 3-4 "if you" qualifiers, escalating in specificity
```

#### Section 2: Viewer Gain (1:00 - 2:00)
```
"By the end of this video, you'll know exactly [specific transformation promise].
Not theory. Not motivation. The actual [system/framework/approach] that [specific result].
And I'm going to show you why [the thing they've been doing] has been keeping you stuck."

Rules:
- Promise must be specific and believable
- Create stakes for NOT watching ("If you click away now, you'll keep...")
- Reference the transformation from offers.md
```

#### Section 3: Storytelling -- 80% of Total Runtime
```
PART A: Life Before (the relatable struggle)
- Paint the "before" picture using ICP's current reality
- Use sensory details -- what did it feel like, look like, sound like
- Mirror the exact emotions from icp.md psychographic profile
- Length: 15-20% of storytelling section

PART B: The Breaking Point (emotional low point)
- The moment everything had to change
- Vulnerability is critical here -- real emotion, real stakes
- The viewer should think "that's exactly where I am"
- Length: 10-15% of storytelling section

PART C: The Discovery (what changed everything)
- Introduce the shift in thinking (NOT the mechanism yet)
- Frame it as accidental or counter-intuitive ("I stumbled on...")
- Create curiosity about what the discovery actually was
- Length: 15-20% of storytelling section

PART D: The Journey (building the new way)
- Show the process of figuring it out
- Include setbacks and iterations (authenticity)
- Gradually reveal elements of the mechanism
- Weave in ICP belief shifts naturally
- Length: 25-30% of storytelling section

PART E: The Results (where you are now)
- Specific, measurable outcomes
- Not just financial -- lifestyle, freedom, impact
- Paint the "after" picture as vividly as the "before"
- Length: 10-15% of storytelling section
```

#### Section 4: Mechanism -- 20% of Total Runtime
```
"So what is [Mechanism Name]?"

- Name the mechanism from positioning.md
- Explain WHY it works (the principle behind it)
- Differentiate: "Unlike [common approach], [Mechanism] works because..."
- Keep it high-level: enough to believe, not enough to DIY
- Use analogies to make complex concepts simple
- Address why previous approaches failed (positioning.md differentiation)

Structure:
1. Name it
2. Explain the principle (1-2 minutes)
3. Show why alternatives fail (1-2 minutes)
4. Demonstrate one small proof of concept (1-2 minutes)
```

#### Section 5: The Pitch
```
"So here's what I've done: I've taken [Mechanism] and built it into [Offer Name]."

Value Stack Presentation (from offers.md):
- Component 1: [Name] -- "This alone is worth [value] because [transformation]"
- Component 2: [Name] -- "This is where [specific result] happens"
- Component 3: [Name] -- "Most [ICP] never get access to this"
- Bonus 1: [Name] -- "And because I want to make sure you succeed..."
- Bonus 2: [Name]
- Guarantee: [Guarantee from offers.md]

Investment Frame:
"The investment is [price]. Now, let me put that in context..."
- Compare to cost of inaction
- Compare to cost of alternatives
- Compare to value of the transformation
- Monthly breakdown if applicable

Rules:
- Present value stack as natural continuation of the story
- Frame investment against cost of staying where they are
- Never apologize for the price
```

#### Section 6: Objection Handling
```
Address 5-7 main objections (from icp.md beliefs/objections):

"Now, you might be thinking: [Objection]"
- Acknowledge it as valid
- Reframe with story or proof
- Transition: "And that's exactly why [mechanism element] exists"

Common objection patterns:
1. "I don't have enough time" -> Reframe around efficiency
2. "I've tried similar things before" -> Differentiate mechanism
3. "I'm not sure this will work for me" -> Specificity + social proof
4. "I can't afford it" -> Cost of inaction frame
5. "I need to think about it" -> What specifically needs to change for this to feel right?

End with strongest proof point -- the objection crusher.
Final CTA: Clear, specific action ("Click the link in the description to [specific next step]")
```

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] 80/20 story-to-mechanism ratio maintained
- [ ] Demographic callout uses exact ICP language
- [ ] Story has clear 5-part arc (Before > Breaking Point > Discovery > Journey > Results)
- [ ] Mechanism is named and differentiated
- [ ] Value stack from offers.md presented in full
- [ ] Minimum 5 objections addressed
- [ ] Single clear CTA at the end
- [ ] Brand voice maintained throughout
- [ ] Runtime target: 20-45 minutes
- [ ] Emotional beats clearly marked for creator

## Framework 3: Lifestyle/Vlog

### Purpose
Show the aspirational outcome of the creator's life and business. Create desire through demonstration, not persuasion. The viewer should think "I want that life."

### Structural Blueprint

```
COLD OPEN (0:00 - 0:15)
[Visual: aspirational moment -- morning routine, workspace, travel, results screen]
"[Casual, mid-moment statement that implies a desirable lifestyle]"

CONTEXT (0:15 - 1:00)
Brief setup of what the viewer is about to see and why it matters.
Subtle connection to the transformation promise.

DAY/EXPERIENCE FLOW (1:00 - Body)
Show, don't tell. Let the lifestyle speak for itself.
Weave in 2-3 casual insights/lessons naturally (not forced teaching moments).
Include human moments: humor, imperfection, real interactions.

[Pattern interrupts: location changes, music shifts, time-lapse sequences]

MECHANISM WHISPER (brief, 30-60 sec total across video)
Not a pitch. A casual mention: "The whole reason I can [lifestyle element] is because of [mechanism principle]."
Drop this 1-2 times naturally, never as a dedicated section.

CLOSE (Final 1-2 min)
Reflective moment: what this lifestyle means to the creator.
Subtle bridge: "If you want to see how I built this, watch [VSSL title]" or bridge to educational content.
```

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Aspirational but authentic (not staged perfection)
- [ ] Mechanism mentioned casually, never formally pitched
- [ ] Brand personality shines through natural moments
- [ ] Visual variety (3+ locations/settings)
- [ ] Target length: 8-15 minutes

## Framework 4: Tutorial/How-To

### Purpose
Solve a specific problem the ICP has, demonstrating expertise through practical application. The viewer implements something real.

### Structural Blueprint

```
HOOK (0:00 - 0:20)
"Here's exactly how to [specific result] in [specific context]."
[Screen recording or setup shot showing the end result first]

PROMISE + PREREQUISITES (0:20 - 1:00)
"In the next [X] minutes, you'll have a working [deliverable]."
List what they need before starting (tools, accounts, materials).

STEP-BY-STEP BODY (1:00 - End minus 2 min)
Step 1: [Name] (timestamp)
- Explain the what and the why
- Show the action (screen recording, demonstration)
- Show the result of this step
- Common mistake to avoid

> CHECKPOINT: "Your [thing] should now look like this. If it does, you're on track."

Step 2: [Name] (timestamp)
[Same structure]

Step 3: [Name] (timestamp)
[Same structure]

[Repeat for all steps, with pattern interrupts between every 2-3 steps]

RESULT SHOWCASE (penultimate section)
Show the completed result.
Compare to where they started.
"In [X] minutes, you went from [before] to [after]."

CLOSE
"If you found this helpful, [bridge to related tutorial or educational video]."
[End screen recommendation]
```

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Each step is specific enough to follow without guessing
- [ ] Screen recordings or demonstrations included for visual steps
- [ ] Checkpoints every 2-3 steps for viewer confidence
- [ ] Common mistakes called out proactively
- [ ] End result shown at the beginning (motivation to continue)
- [ ] Zero sales intent -- pure problem-solving
- [ ] Target length: 10-25 minutes

## Framework 5: Story-Based

### Purpose
Deep emotional connection through the creator's origin story or a pivotal life/business story. The viewer should feel emotionally moved and see themselves in the creator's journey.

### Structural Blueprint

```
HOOK (0:00 - 0:30)
Start in the middle of the story's most intense moment.
"I was sitting in [specific place] when [pivotal event happened]."
[Visual: cinematic, moody, matching the emotional tone]

BACKSTORY (0:30 - 3:00)
Set the scene: who you were, where you were, what you believed.
Mirror the ICP's current reality -- they should think "that's me."
Use sensory details and specific moments, not generalizations.

RISING ACTION (3:00 - 8:00)
The escalating struggle. Each obstacle worse than the last.
Weave in the limiting beliefs the ICP holds.
Show vulnerability: doubt, fear, failure.
Pattern interrupts through emotional shifts and visual changes.

CLIMAX (8:00 - 12:00)
The breaking point or the discovery moment.
The single insight or event that changed the trajectory.
This is where the mechanism's principle is introduced (without naming it).
Maximum emotional intensity.

FALLING ACTION + RESOLUTION (12:00 - 18:00)
The journey of implementing the new approach.
Specific results and milestones.
How the new reality compares to the old.
What you lost, what you gained, what surprised you.

LESSON (18:00 - Close minus 1 min)
The universal truth extracted from the personal story.
Frame it as applicable to the viewer's life.
"If you're where I was [X years] ago, here's what I want you to know..."

CLOSE
Bridge to related content or VSSL (depending on buyer stage target).
```

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Story has clear tension arc (setup > conflict > climax > resolution)
- [ ] Viewer can see themselves in the creator's "before" state
- [ ] Vulnerability is genuine, not performed
- [ ] Mechanism principle introduced through story, not lecture
- [ ] Sensory details and specific moments used (not generalizations)
- [ ] Target length: 12-25 minutes

## Framework 6: Case Study

### Purpose
Prove the mechanism works through a real example -- a client result, a personal experiment, or a documented transformation.

### Structural Blueprint

```
HOOK (0:00 - 0:30)
"[Specific result] in [specific timeframe]. Let me show you exactly how it happened."
[Visual: before/after comparison or result metric on screen]

CONTEXT (0:30 - 2:00)
Who is this case study about? (client or personal)
What was the starting point? (specific, measurable)
What was the goal?
Why was this case particularly challenging or representative?

THE APPROACH (2:00 - 6:00)
What was done -- step by step, specifically.
Frame around the mechanism from positioning.md.
Why this approach was chosen over alternatives.
Key decisions made along the way and the reasoning.

THE CHALLENGES (6:00 - 8:00)
What went wrong or was harder than expected.
How those challenges were overcome.
(This section builds credibility -- things that went perfectly are not believable)

THE RESULTS (8:00 - 12:00)
Specific, measurable outcomes.
Before vs. after comparison.
Timeline of results (not instant -- show the progression).
Unexpected positive side effects.

THE LESSONS (12:00 - 15:00)
3-5 transferable principles the viewer can apply.
"Here's what this means for you if [you're in a similar situation]..."
Connect each lesson to a broader principle.

CLOSE
Bridge to VSSL or mechanism-deep video.
"If you want to understand the full system behind this, [watch/click]..."
```

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Specific numbers and metrics throughout (not vague "great results")
- [ ] Before/after clearly established and measurable
- [ ] Challenges included for credibility
- [ ] Mechanism from positioning.md is clearly the driver of results
- [ ] Lessons are transferable (not just "this worked for this one person")
- [ ] Target length: 10-20 minutes

## Framework 7: Interview/Podcast-Style

### Purpose
Borrow authority from a guest, expand reach through their audience, and provide diverse perspectives while reinforcing the creator's positioning.

### Structural Blueprint

```
COLD OPEN (0:00 - 0:30)
The most compelling 20-second clip from the interview.
[Text overlay: "Full conversation below"]

INTRO (0:30 - 2:00)
Creator introduces the guest: who they are, why they matter, what the viewer will learn.
"Today I'm sitting down with [Guest] who [credibility marker]. We're going to talk about [topic]."

QUESTION BLOCKS (2:00 - End minus 5 min)
Block 1: Origin/Background (warm-up, establish guest credibility)
- "How did you get into [field]?"
- "What was the turning point for you?"

Block 2: Core Topic (the meat, aligned with content pillar)
- "Walk us through [specific process/framework]..."
- "What's the biggest mistake people make with [topic]?"
- "If someone is starting from zero, what's the first thing they should do?"

Block 3: Contrarian/Deep (the differentiated insight)
- "What's something most people in [industry] get completely wrong?"
- "What changed your mind about [common belief]?"

Block 4: Tactical (actionable takeaway)
- "Give us one thing someone can do this week to [result]."

CLOSE (Final 5 min)
Creator summarizes top 3 insights.
Guest shares where to find them.
Creator bridges to related content.
```

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Guest is credible and relevant to ICP
- [ ] Best clip used as cold open
- [ ] Questions build in depth (warm-up > core > contrarian > tactical)
- [ ] Creator's positioning reinforced through question framing
- [ ] Actionable takeaway for the viewer
- [ ] Target length: 30-60 minutes
