## Their Strengths (Be Honest)

1. **High YouTube production quality.** Jake's videos are well-edited with good thumbnails, clear structure, and professional B-roll. His average views (8K-15K per video) indicate strong organic reach in the fitness-for-professionals niche.

2. **Volume of social proof.** Jake has 70+ video testimonials on his website, more than most competitors in this niche. Many include before/after photos with 3-6 month timelines.

3. **Accessible price point.** At $1,497, his program is positioned at roughly half our price ($3,000). For price-sensitive prospects, this is a meaningful difference.

4. **Consistent content cadence.** Jake publishes 2 YouTube videos and 3 Instagram posts weekly without fail. This consistency builds trust and maintains audience awareness.

---

## Their Weaknesses (Evidence-Based)

1. **No personalization in the program.** Based on his sales page and email sequence, the "Executive Body Blueprint" is a pre-built 8-week plan with weekly group calls. There is no custom programming, no 1-on-1 component, and no adaptation based on individual goals. Multiple client reviews on TrustPilot mention: "The program is good but generic -- I had to figure out how to adapt it to my schedule." (Source: TrustPilot, 3 reviews from Jan-Mar 2026)

2. **No mechanism differentiation.** "The 3-Phase Metabolic Reset" is standard calorie cycling rebranded. The sales page does not explain why this works differently from other programs. For prospects who have tried calorie-based approaches before (our primary ICP segment), this is a vulnerability.

3. **Refund complaints on social media.** Two public Instagram comment threads (Jan 15 and Feb 28, 2026) include users claiming difficulty getting refunds processed. Jake's team responded but the threads were not resolved publicly. This creates a trust concern for prospects who research before buying.

4. **No high-ticket pathway.** Jake has no upsell beyond the $1,497 program. Clients who want more support or advanced coaching have nowhere to go, which limits his LTV and creates an opportunity for us to capture graduates who want deeper work.

5. **Content focuses on aesthetics over performance.** Jake's content is primarily about "looking good" -- six-pack abs, muscle definition, body composition. Our ICP research shows that busy professionals (35-50) are more motivated by energy, performance, and longevity than aesthetics. This is a messaging gap we can exploit.

## When We Win

1. **When the prospect has tried generic programs before and failed.** Our Metabolic Momentum Method is specifically designed for people who have tried standard calorie-based approaches (like Jake's) and been frustrated by the rigidity. When a prospect says "I've tried programs before but couldn't stick with them because of my schedule," we win.

2. **When the prospect values personalization over price.** A $3,000 fully customized program vs a $1,497 cookie-cutter plan is not a 2:1 price difference -- it is a completely different product. When the prospect understands this, we win.

3. **When the prospect is 35-50 and motivated by health/energy, not aesthetics.** Jake's content targets vanity-driven outcomes. Our positioning around "sustainable fitness that works around your life" resonates with the segment that has outgrown the six-pack goal.

4. **When the prospect needs accountability and cannot self-manage.** Our weekly 1-on-1 calls create a level of accountability that a pre-built program with group calls cannot match.

---

## When We Lose

1. **When the prospect's primary decision factor is price.** At 2x Jake's price, we lose the price-sensitive buyer. This is acceptable -- price-sensitive buyers have lower LTV and higher churn. We should not compete on price.

2. **When the prospect has a short timeline.** Jake's 8-week program appeals to prospects who want fast results. Our 12-week program requires more patience. If a prospect says "I need to look good for an event in 8 weeks," Jake is a better fit.

## Objection Handling Talk Tracks

### "I'm also looking at FitPro Academy / Jake Thornton's program"

**Response:** "Jake puts out great content -- I actually follow some of his stuff too. The key question is: what specifically do you need to be different this time? Because here's the thing -- Jake's program is a pre-built plan that works for a general audience. It's well-made, but it's the same plan for everyone. What we do at FitFoundation is build a program around YOUR schedule, YOUR dietary preferences, and YOUR specific body. Plus, you get weekly 1-on-1 calls with Marcus, not just group calls. If you've tried following a generic plan before and it didn't stick, that's exactly why -- it wasn't built for you."

### "FitPro Academy is half the price"

**Response:** "You're right -- and here's why. Jake's program is a pre-recorded 8-week plan with group calls. There's no custom programming, no 1-on-1 coaching, and no post-program support. FitFoundation is 12 weeks of fully customized programming with weekly 1-on-1 calls with Marcus and 6 months of follow-up check-ins after. When you divide the investment by the level of personalization and support, the per-session cost is actually comparable. More importantly -- what's the cost of doing another program that doesn't work? You've already invested in programs before. This time, it's about investing in the RIGHT one."

### "Jake has more testimonials than you"

**Response:** "He does have a lot of testimonials -- and many of them are genuine. What I'd encourage you to look at is the depth. Look at our case studies -- [pull up David's story]. David was a 44-year-old VP working 60 hours a week. He lost 42 pounds in 16 weeks while maintaining his work schedule and never missing a client dinner. We tracked his journey week by week with specific metrics. That level of detailed, documented transformation is what separates our results from general 'great program' testimonials. Also -- ask Jake's team what their average client's results are at 12 weeks, not just their best stories."

### "My friend recommended Jake's program"

**Response:** "Referrals are powerful -- your friend clearly had a positive experience. Can I ask: does your friend's situation match yours? What I mean is -- are they the same age, same schedule demands, same fitness starting point? Because Jake's program works well for a certain profile -- generally younger, more time-flexible, motivated by aesthetics. If that matches your friend but not you, the results might be different. Let me share a case study of someone who matches YOUR profile exactly..."
