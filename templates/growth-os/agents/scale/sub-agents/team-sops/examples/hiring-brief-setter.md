# Hiring Brief: Appointment Setter

**Department:** Sales
**Reports To:** Marcus Rivera (Creator / Head of Sales)
**Compensation:** $3,000/month base + $100 per qualified booking that shows up for the call
**Employment Type:** Full-time contractor (remote)
**Location:** Remote (any timezone, but must be available 9am-6pm EST)
**Total OTE:** $6,000-$9,000/month

---

## Role Description

The Appointment Setter is the first human touchpoint in our sales process. You are the bridge between a curious lead and a revenue-generating sales call. Your job is to respond to every inbound lead within 5 minutes, qualify them using our BANT framework, and book the best-fit leads onto Marcus's closer calendar. You are not selling. You are qualifying and booking. The difference matters -- setters who try to sell on the qualification call burn leads that a closer would have converted.

This role directly impacts revenue: every qualified booking you create feeds the pipeline that generates $3,000+ per closed deal. A great setter fills the closer's calendar with 3-5 qualified calls per day, enabling $30K-$75K in monthly revenue from your pipeline alone.

---

## A Day in the Life

**9:00am** -- Log into GoHighLevel. Review "New Leads Today" smart list. Check overnight DM responses on Instagram.

**9:15am-12:00pm** -- Work through lead queue. For each lead: review their info (30 sec), call or DM within 5 min of identification (5-10 min conversation), qualify using BANT, book if qualified or route to nurture. Aim for 8-12 qualification conversations before lunch.

**12:00-12:30pm** -- Update CRM notes for all morning conversations. Send booking confirmations and follow-ups.

**12:30-1:30pm** -- Lunch.

**1:30-5:00pm** -- Continue lead outreach. Follow up on morning no-answers. Send Day 2 and Day 5 follow-ups to leads in the pipeline. Check for re-engagement from nurture sequences.

**5:00-5:30pm** -- Send end-of-day report to Marcus: leads contacted, bookings made, notable conversations, any escalations needed.

**5:30-6:00pm** -- Prepare tomorrow's lead list. Organize CRM. Respond to any late DMs.

**Total daily calls/conversations:** 15-25
**Total daily bookings (target):** 3-5 qualified bookings

---

## Key Responsibilities

1. **Respond to every inbound lead within 5 minutes** via phone, SMS, or DM -- speed is the single biggest factor in qualification rates
2. **Qualify leads using the BANT framework** (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline) through natural conversation, not robotic questioning
3. **Book 3-5 qualified calls per day** on Marcus's closer calendar using Calendly, ensuring every booking includes BANT notes and relevant context
4. **Execute the follow-up cadence** for every non-closed lead -- Day 0, Day 2, Day 5, Day 8, Day 10, Day 15 -- without exception
5. **Manage the pre-call experience** by ensuring booked leads receive confirmation, reminders (24hr and 1hr), and the pre-call questionnaire
6. **Maintain CRM hygiene** -- every lead has current status, BANT scores, notes, and proper tagging in GoHighLevel
7. **Send daily performance reports** to Marcus by 5:30pm EST with key metrics and notable conversations

## Required Skills

- **Conversational qualification:** Ability to assess fit through natural conversation, not scripted interrogation. Must sound like a helpful human, not a call center.
- **Speed and responsiveness:** Comfortable working in a fast-paced environment where 5-minute response time is non-negotiable.
- **CRM proficiency:** Experience with GoHighLevel, HubSpot, or similar CRM. Must learn GHL within first 2 weeks.
- **Written communication:** Strong DM and SMS communication -- most initial contacts happen via text, not phone.
- **Objection navigation:** Ability to handle initial pushback ("I'm just looking," "Send me more info") without being pushy and without losing the lead.
- **Empathy and active listening:** Leads are often frustrated with their fitness situation. The setter must listen first, qualify second.

## Who This Role Is NOT For

- **People who want to "sell."** This is a qualifying and booking role, not a closing role. If you pitch on the qualification call, you will burn leads.
- **People who need constant supervision.** This is a remote, autonomous role. You manage your own time within the 9am-6pm window.
- **People who take rejection personally.** Most leads will not book a call. That is normal. You need resilience and short memory.
- **People who are slow communicators.** If you take 30 minutes to respond to a DM, this role is not for you. Speed is the core competency.
- **People who cut corners on follow-up.** The follow-up cadence is not optional. If you skip steps because the lead "probably won't respond," you are leaving money on the table.

## Interview Questions

### Behavioral (Past Behavior)

1. **"Tell me about a time you had to follow up with someone 5+ times before getting a response. What was your approach?"**
   Looking for: Persistence without being annoying. Varied follow-up approaches. Not giving up after 2 attempts.

2. **"Describe a situation where you had to quickly assess if someone was a good fit for something. How did you decide?"**
   Looking for: Systematic thinking about qualification. Not "gut feel" but specific criteria applied.

3. **"Tell me about a time you received a negative response from someone you were trying to help. How did you handle it?"**
   Looking for: Emotional resilience. Did not take it personally. Maintained professionalism. Moved on quickly.

4. **"Give me an example of a day when you had 20+ tasks to complete. How did you prioritize?"**
   Looking for: Time management system. Not just "I worked hard" but specific prioritization framework.

5. **"Tell me about a time you had to learn a new tool or system quickly. How did you approach it?"**
   Looking for: Self-directed learning. Used tutorials, asked questions, practiced. Did not wait to be trained on everything.

### Situational (Problem-Solving)

1. **"A lead DMs us at 5pm saying 'What's your price?' How do you respond?"**
   Looking for: Does NOT just answer the price question. Redirects to qualification. "Great question! Before I answer, can I ask what made you reach out? I want to make sure I give you the right info."

2. **"You call a lead and they say 'I'm not interested, take me off your list.' What do you do?"**
   Looking for: Respectful compliance. "Absolutely, I'll remove you right now. Sorry for the bother." Tags as "Do Not Contact" in CRM. Does NOT argue or try to re-qualify.

3. **"A lead scores HIGH on all 4 BANT criteria but says 'I want to think about it before booking a call.' What do you do?"**
   Looking for: Gentle probe: "Totally understand. What specifically do you want to think about?" If they share, address it. If they insist, respect it: "No problem. I'll check in with you on Thursday. What time works?"

4. **"You realize at 4pm that you've only made 2 bookings today and your target is 4. What do you do?"**
   Looking for: Does not panic. Reviews pipeline for warm follow-ups. Sends DMs to engaged leads. Works the follow-up list harder. Does not lower quality standards to inflate numbers.

5. **"Marcus tells you that 3 of your last 5 bookings were unqualified -- leads that should not have been booked. How do you respond?"**
   Looking for: Accountability. Asks for specifics on what they missed. Reviews the BANT scoring for those leads. Adjusts their qualification threshold. Does not get defensive.

## Red Flags to Watch For

1. **Talks more than they listen during the interview.** Setters must be listeners first. If they dominate the conversation in the interview, they will dominate qualification calls and miss critical information.

2. **Cannot give specific examples -- only theory.** "I'm great with people" without a specific story is a red flag. Past behavior predicts future behavior.

3. **Asks about commission before asking about the role.** Compensation-first mindset usually means they will cut corners when the bonuses are not flowing.

4. **Resists the role-play exercise.** If they will not do a mock qualification call in the interview, they will avoid uncomfortable conversations with real leads.

5. **Blames previous employers for lack of results.** "The leads were bad" or "the product was hard to sell" signals low accountability.
